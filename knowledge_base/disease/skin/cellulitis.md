# Cellulitis

## Overview

Cellulitis is a bacterial infection of the deeper layers of skin, causing an area of redness, warmth, and swelling that can spread quickly if untreated. Unlike more superficial skin conditions, cellulitis can become serious and requires prompt medical attention, especially if fever develops.

---

# Common Symptoms

- Spreading redness with poorly defined, expanding borders
- Warmth to the touch over the affected area
- Swelling
- Tenderness or pain
- Fever or chills in more advanced cases
- Skin that may appear tight or shiny

---

# Causes

### Bacterial infection

Most commonly *Streptococcus* or *Staphylococcus* bacteria entering through a break in the skin (cut, insect bite, surgical wound, or even cracked dry skin).

---

# Risk Factors

- Any break in the skin (cuts, insect bites, ulcers)
- Diabetes
- Weakened immune system
- Poor circulation or lymphedema
- Obesity
- Prior episodes of cellulitis

---

# Severity Classification

## 🟡 Moderate

- Localized redness and warmth
- No fever
- Mild tenderness

Recommendation: Prompt medical evaluation — cellulitis is treated with antibiotics and should not be managed with home care alone.

---

## 🔴 High — Possible Emergency

Any of the following:

- Rapidly spreading redness
- Fever or chills
- Increasing pain
- Red streaking extending from the area
- Signs of confusion or feeling very unwell

Recommendation: **Seek emergency medical care immediately.** Cellulitis can progress to a serious systemic infection if untreated.

---

# Home Care

Cellulitis requires prescription antibiotics from a doctor — it is not appropriately managed with home care alone. While awaiting evaluation: keep the affected area clean, elevate it if it's a limb to reduce swelling, and avoid pressure or trauma to the area.

---

# Prevention

- Clean and cover any skin wounds promptly.
- Manage chronic conditions like diabetes and lymphedema well.
- Moisturize dry, cracked skin to prevent entry points for bacteria.
- Seek early treatment for any skin infection before it can spread.

---

# When to Consult a Doctor

Any suspected cellulitis warrants **prompt** medical evaluation — this is not a condition to monitor at home and wait on.

---

# Emergency Warning Signs

Seek emergency care immediately if:

- Rapidly spreading redness
- Fever or chills
- Red streaking extending from the affected area
- Increasing severe pain
- Confusion, dizziness, or feeling very unwell

---

# Recommended Specialist

Dermatologist (or emergency care if rapidly spreading/with fever)
