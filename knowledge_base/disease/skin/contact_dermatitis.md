# Contact Dermatitis

## Overview

Contact dermatitis is skin inflammation triggered by direct contact with an irritant or allergen — such as soaps, metals, cosmetics, or plants. It typically appears in the shape or area of contact with the trigger substance.

---

# Common Symptoms

- Redness at the contact site
- Itching
- Burning sensation
- Swelling
- Occasional blistering in severe cases

---

# Causes

### Irritants

Detergents, chemicals, solvents damaging the skin directly.

### Allergens

Nickel, fragrance, latex, or certain plants (e.g., poison ivy) triggering an immune response.

---

# Risk Factors

- Frequent hand-washing or wet work
- Known metal or fragrance allergies
- Occupational chemical exposure
- Pre-existing sensitive skin or eczema

# Severity Classification

## 🟢 Low

- Mild localized redness
- Slight itching
- No blistering

Recommendation: Remove the trigger and monitor with home care.

---

## 🟡 Moderate

- Spreading redness
- Persistent itching/burning
- Mild swelling

Recommendation: Consult a dermatologist if it doesn't resolve after removing the trigger.

---

## 🔴 High

Any of the following:

- Facial or airway swelling
- Widespread blistering
- Signs of infection

Recommendation: Seek immediate medical attention.

---

# Home Care

- Identify and remove the triggering substance.
- Apply a cool compress to soothe irritation.
- Avoid further contact with the suspected trigger.
- Use fragrance-free, gentle moisturizers.

---

# Prevention

- Use hypoallergenic products where sensitivities are known.
- Wear protective gloves for chemical or wet work.
- Patch-test new cosmetic products before full use.

---

# When to Consult a Doctor

Consult a dermatologist if:

- The rash spreads or worsens after removing the suspected trigger
- Symptoms persist beyond a week
- The cause of the reaction is unclear

---

# Emergency Warning Signs

Seek emergency care immediately if:

- Facial swelling or difficulty breathing after exposure
- Widespread blistering with fever
