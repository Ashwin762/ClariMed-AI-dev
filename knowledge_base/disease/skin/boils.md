# Boils (Skin Abscess)

## Overview

A boil, also known as a skin abscess or furuncle, is a painful,
pus-filled lump that develops beneath the skin due to a bacterial
infection, most commonly caused by *Staphylococcus aureus*. Boils
often begin as small, tender red bumps and gradually enlarge as pus
collects inside. Larger or multiple boils may require medical treatment.

**Note:** This condition can often be evaluated using both visible
skin changes and reported symptoms. However, confirmation by a
healthcare professional may still be required.

---

# Common Symptoms

- Painful red lump on the skin

- Swelling around the affected area

- Pus or fluid drainage

- Warmth over the lump

- Tenderness to touch

- Fever (in severe infections)

---

# Causes

Boils occur when bacteria infect a hair follicle or oil gland, leading
to the collection of pus beneath the skin.

### Common causes

Bacterial skin infection, poor hygiene, cuts or abrasions, weakened
immune system, and friction on the skin.

---

# Risk Factors

- Diabetes

- Poor hygiene

- Weakened immune system

- Close contact with infected individuals

- Skin injuries

- Obesity

---

# Severity Classification

## 🟢 Low

- Small boil with mild pain

- No fever or spreading redness

Recommendation: Maintain good hygiene and monitor the boil.

---

## 🟡 Moderate

- Large painful boil

- Pus formation

- Difficulty performing daily activities

Recommendation: Consult a dermatologist for evaluation.

---

## 🔴 High

Any of the following:

- Fever with the boil

- Rapidly spreading redness

- Multiple boils

- Boil on the face with severe swelling

- Signs of severe infection

Recommendation: Seek immediate medical care as the infection may spread.

---

# Home Care

- Apply a warm compress several times a day.

- Keep the affected area clean.

- Do not squeeze or puncture the boil.

- Wash hands before and after touching the area.

- Cover draining boils with a clean dressing.

---

# Prevention

- Maintain good personal hygiene.

- Wash hands regularly.

- Keep cuts and wounds clean.

- Avoid sharing towels or personal items.

- Manage diabetes and other chronic conditions.

---

# When to Consult a Doctor

Consult a dermatologist if:

- The boil becomes very painful.

- It does not improve within a few days.

- Multiple boils develop.

- Fever occurs.

---

# Emergency Warning Signs

Seek immediate medical care if you experience:

- High fever

- Rapidly spreading redness

- Severe swelling of the face

- Difficulty moving the affected area

- Signs of bloodstream infection
