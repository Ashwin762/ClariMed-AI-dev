# Vitiligo

## Overview

Vitiligo is a condition in which the skin loses its pigment-producing cells (melanocytes), resulting in smooth, well-defined white or pale patches. It is not contagious, not painful, and not a sign of a skin infection — it's an autoimmune-related pigment condition.

---

# Common Symptoms

- Smooth, flat white or pale patches of skin
- Well-defined patch borders (not scaly or textured, unlike psoriasis)
- Patches often symmetrical on both sides of the body
- Premature whitening of hair on affected areas (in some cases)
- No itching, pain, or scaling associated with the patches themselves

---

# Causes

### Autoimmune process

The immune system mistakenly attacks and destroys melanocytes (pigment-producing cells).

### Contributing factors

Family history, other autoimmune conditions, and sometimes stress or skin trauma triggering new patches.

---

# Risk Factors

- Family history of vitiligo or other autoimmune conditions
- Personal history of autoimmune disease (thyroid disorders, etc.)
- Skin trauma at the site of new patches (Koebner phenomenon)

---

---

# Severity Classification

## 🟢 Low

- Small, isolated patch
- No spreading noted

Recommendation: Monitor and consult a dermatologist for confirmation and options.

---

## 🟡 Moderate

- Multiple or spreading patches
- Noticeable cosmetic impact

Recommendation: Consult a dermatologist to discuss treatment options (topical therapy, light therapy, and others).

---

# Home Care

- Use sunscreen on depigmented areas, which are more prone to sunburn.
- Avoid skin trauma where possible, as new patches can develop at injury sites.
- Cosmetic camouflage products can help if desired — entirely optional.

---

# Prevention

- There is no known way to prevent vitiligo, as it's an autoimmune process.
- Early dermatology consultation can help manage progression and explore treatment options.

---

# When to Consult a Doctor

Consult a dermatologist if:

- New patches appear or existing ones spread
- You want to explore treatment or cosmetic options
- You have other symptoms suggesting an associated autoimmune condition (fatigue, thyroid symptoms)

---

# Emergency Warning Signs

Vitiligo itself is not an emergency. However, since it's linked to autoimmune conditions, significant fatigue, rapid weight change, or other systemic symptoms alongside new patches warrant a general medical evaluation.
