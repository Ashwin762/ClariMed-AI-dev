# Acne

## Overview

Acne is a common skin condition that occurs when hair follicles become blocked by oil (sebum) and dead skin cells, leading to pimples, blackheads, or whiteheads. It most commonly affects the face, chest, and back, and is especially common during adolescence, though it can occur at any age.

Most cases are manageable with consistent skin care and, when needed, medical treatment.

---

# Common Symptoms

- Redness around affected pores
- Swelling or raised bumps
- Blackheads and whiteheads
- Occasional itching or burning
- Oily skin texture
- Occasional pain in inflamed lesions

---

# Causes

### Excess oil production

Overactive sebaceous glands increase pore blockage risk.

### Clogged pores

Dead skin cells combine with oil to block hair follicles.

### Bacterial involvement

*Cutibacterium acnes* bacteria can multiply in blocked pores, triggering inflammation.

### Hormonal changes

Puberty, menstrual cycles, and hormonal fluctuations increase oil production.

---

# Risk Factors

- Adolescence and hormonal changes
- Family history of acne
- Certain cosmetic or hair products
- High-glycemic diet in some individuals
- Stress
- Friction from helmets, straps, or tight clothing

# Severity Classification

## 🟢 Low

- Occasional blackheads/whiteheads
- Minimal redness
- No pain

Recommendation: Home care and gentle skincare routine.

---

## 🟡 Moderate

- Multiple inflamed pimples
- Persistent redness and swelling
- Mild discomfort

Recommendation: Consult a dermatologist for a treatment plan.

---

## 🔴 High

Any of the following:

- Painful, deep cystic lesions
- Rapid spreading with fever
- Signs of skin infection (increasing warmth, pus, spreading redness)

Recommendation: Seek prompt medical evaluation.

---

# Home Care

- Wash the affected area gently twice daily.
- Use non-comedogenic (oil-free) skincare products.
- Avoid touching or picking at lesions.
- Change pillowcases regularly.

---

# Prevention

- Maintain a consistent, gentle cleansing routine.
- Avoid heavy, oil-based cosmetics.
- Avoid excessive scrubbing, which can worsen irritation.
- Manage stress where possible.

---

# When to Consult a Doctor

Consult a dermatologist if:

- Acne persists beyond several weeks of home care
- Scarring is developing
- Lesions are painful or cystic
- Over-the-counter treatments show no improvement

---

# Emergency Warning Signs

Seek prompt medical attention if:

- Rapidly spreading redness accompanied by fever
- Signs of a deep skin infection (increasing pain, warmth, pus)
