# Warts

## Overview

Warts are small, rough skin growths caused by infection with the human
papillomavirus (HPV). They commonly appear on the hands, feet, or other
areas of the body and are generally harmless, though they may spread
through direct skin contact.

**Note:** Warts often have visible characteristics, and image
analysis may assist the AI. However, confirmation by a healthcare
professional may still be required.

---

# Common Symptoms

- Small rough skin growth

- Raised bump with a rough surface

- Skin-colored or slightly darker lesion

- Pain when located on pressure-bearing areas such as the feet

- Multiple warts developing nearby

---

# Causes

Warts are caused by infection with certain types of the human
papillomavirus (HPV).

### Common causes

Direct skin contact with HPV, touching contaminated surfaces, or breaks
in the skin that allow the virus to enter.

---

# Risk Factors

- Children and teenagers

- Weakened immune system

- Walking barefoot in public places

- Frequent skin injuries

- Close contact with infected individuals

---

# Severity Classification

## 🟢 Low

- Single small wart

- No pain or discomfort

Recommendation: Monitor the wart and maintain good skin hygiene.

---

## 🟡 Moderate

- Multiple warts

- Painful plantar wart

- Warts interfering with daily activities

Recommendation: Consult a dermatologist for evaluation and treatment.

---

# Home Care

- Avoid picking or scratching the wart.

- Keep the affected area clean and dry.

- Do not share towels or personal items.

- Wash hands after touching the wart.

- Cover the wart if necessary to reduce spread.

---

# Prevention

- Wear footwear in public showers and pools.

- Avoid direct contact with other people\'s warts.

- Maintain good hand hygiene.

- Avoid sharing personal items.

---

# When to Consult a Doctor

Consult a dermatologist if:

- The wart becomes painful.

- It spreads rapidly.

- It bleeds frequently.

- You are unsure whether the skin growth is a wart.
