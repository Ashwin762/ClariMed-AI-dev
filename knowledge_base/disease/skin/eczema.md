# Eczema

## Overview

Eczema (atopic dermatitis) is a chronic condition causing dry, itchy, inflamed patches of skin. It is often linked to a sensitive skin barrier, allergies, or asthma, and tends to flare and subside over time rather than resolve permanently.

---

# Common Symptoms

- Intense itching
- Dry, scaly or peeling patches
- Redness
- Skin thickening in long-standing areas
- Occasional oozing or crusting during flares
- Swelling in affected areas

---

# Causes

### Skin barrier dysfunction

A weakened skin barrier allows moisture loss and irritant entry.

### Allergens and irritants

Soaps, detergents, dust, and pet dander can trigger flares.

### Genetic predisposition

Family history of eczema, asthma, or allergies increases risk.

### Stress and climate

Both can trigger or worsen flare-ups.

---

# Risk Factors

- Family history of eczema, asthma, or hay fever
- Dry climates or low humidity
- Frequent hot showers
- Harsh soaps or fragranced products
- Stress

# Severity Classification

## 🟢 Low

- Mild, occasional itching
- Small dry patches
- No oozing

Recommendation: Home care and regular moisturizing.

---

## 🟡 Moderate

- Persistent itching affecting sleep/routine
- Larger inflamed patches
- Occasional cracking

Recommendation: Consult a dermatologist.

---

## 🔴 High

Any of the following:

- Widespread oozing or crusting
- Signs of skin infection (increasing warmth, pus, fever)
- Severe uncontrolled itching disrupting daily life

Recommendation: Seek prompt medical evaluation.

---

# Home Care

- Moisturize within minutes of bathing, using fragrance-free products.
- Use lukewarm (not hot) water for washing.
- Wear soft, breathable fabrics.
- Avoid known irritants and allergens.

---

# Prevention

- Maintain a consistent moisturizing routine even when skin looks clear.
- Identify and avoid personal triggers.
- Use a humidifier in dry environments.
- Avoid scratching — keep nails trimmed short.

---

# When to Consult a Doctor

Consult a dermatologist if:

- Patches crack, ooze, or show signs of infection
- Symptoms don't improve with consistent home care
- Itching significantly disrupts sleep or daily activity

---

# Emergency Warning Signs

Seek prompt medical attention if:

- Widespread oozing with fever (possible infection)
- Rapidly spreading redness across large body areas
