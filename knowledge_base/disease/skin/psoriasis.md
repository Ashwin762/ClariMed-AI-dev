# Psoriasis

## Overview

Psoriasis is a chronic autoimmune condition that causes skin cells to build up rapidly, forming thick, scaly patches — often described as silvery-white — most commonly on the elbows, knees, scalp, and lower back.

---

# Common Symptoms

- Thick, scaly patches
- Silvery-white scaling
- Itching
- Redness beneath the scales
- Occasional cracking or bleeding in severe cases

---

# Causes

### Immune system dysfunction

The immune system mistakenly speeds up skin cell turnover, causing buildup.

### Triggers

Stress, infections, skin injury, and certain medications can trigger flares.

---

# Risk Factors

- Family history of psoriasis
- Stress
- Smoking
- Obesity
- Certain infections (e.g., strep throat)

---

---

# Severity Classification

## 🟢 Low

- Small, isolated patches
- Mild scaling
- Minimal itching

Recommendation: Home care and regular moisturizing.

---

## 🟡 Moderate

- Larger or multiple patches
- Persistent itching
- Visible scaling affecting daily comfort

Recommendation: Consult a dermatologist for a management plan.

---

## 🔴 High

Any of the following:

- Widespread redness covering large body areas with fever (erythrodermic psoriasis)
- Joint pain and swelling alongside skin symptoms (possible psoriatic arthritis)

Recommendation: Seek prompt medical evaluation.

---

# Home Care

- Moisturize regularly to reduce scaling and cracking.
- Allow gentle, brief sun exposure as tolerated.
- Manage stress levels.
- Avoid picking or scratching at scales.

---

# Prevention

- Identify and avoid personal flare triggers.
- Maintain a consistent skincare routine.
- Avoid skin trauma (cuts, sunburn) which can trigger new patches.

---

# When to Consult a Doctor

Consult a dermatologist for:

- Any new, persistent scaly patches
- A proper long-term management plan
- Joint pain accompanying skin symptoms

---

# Emergency Warning Signs

Seek prompt medical attention if:

- Widespread redness with fever (erythrodermic psoriasis)
- Signs of skin infection in cracked areas
