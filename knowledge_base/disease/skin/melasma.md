# Melasma

## Overview

Melasma is a common skin condition that causes brown or grayish patches,
usually on the face. It most commonly affects the cheeks, forehead,
nose, and upper lip. Although harmless, melasma may affect appearance
and self-confidence.

**Note:** This condition often has visible skin changes, and image
analysis may assist the AI. However, diagnosis should be confirmed by a
dermatologist.

---

# Common Symptoms

- Brown or gray patches on the face

- Uneven skin pigmentation

- Symmetrical facial discoloration

- Dark patches that gradually enlarge

---

# Causes

Melasma develops due to increased melanin production in the skin.

### Common causes

Sun exposure, hormonal changes, pregnancy, oral contraceptives, certain
medications, and genetic predisposition.

---

# Risk Factors

- Female gender

- Pregnancy

- Hormonal therapy

- Family history

- Frequent sun exposure

- Darker skin tone

---

# Severity Classification

## 🟢 Low

- Small localized pigmentation

- No discomfort

Recommendation: Protect the skin from sunlight and monitor the patches.

---

## 🟡 Moderate

- Large facial patches

- Cosmetic concerns

- Gradually worsening pigmentation

Recommendation: Consult a dermatologist for evaluation and management.

---

# Home Care

- Use broad-spectrum sunscreen daily.

- Wear a wide-brimmed hat outdoors.

- Avoid excessive sun exposure.

- Follow the skincare routine recommended by your dermatologist.

---

# Prevention

- Apply sunscreen every day.

- Wear protective clothing outdoors.

- Avoid prolonged sun exposure.

- Manage hormonal factors under medical guidance.

---

# When to Consult a Doctor

Consult a dermatologist if:

- Dark patches continue to spread.

- Pigmentation changes suddenly.

- You are unsure whether the patches are melasma.
