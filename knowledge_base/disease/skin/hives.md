# Hives (Urticaria)

## Overview

Hives, also known as urticaria, are raised, itchy welts that appear on
the skin due to an allergic reaction or other triggers. They may vary in
size and location and often disappear within a few hours, though some
cases can persist longer. Severe allergic reactions associated with
hives may require emergency medical care.

**Note:** This condition can sometimes be evaluated using both
reported symptoms and visible skin changes. However, clinical
examination may still be necessary for an accurate diagnosis.

---

# Common Symptoms

- Raised red or skin-colored welts

- Intense itching

- Swelling of the skin

- Burning or stinging sensation

- Welts that change shape or move around the body

---

# Causes

Hives occur when the body releases histamine, causing swelling in the
skin.

### Common causes

Food allergies, medications, insect bites, infections, stress, heat,
cold exposure, and unknown triggers.

---

# Risk Factors

- History of allergies

- Asthma or eczema

- Family history of allergic conditions

- Recent infections

- Exposure to allergens

---

# Severity Classification

## 🟢 Low

- Small localized itchy patches

- Symptoms improve within a few hours

Recommendation: Avoid known triggers and monitor symptoms.

---

## 🟡 Moderate

- Widespread hives

- Persistent itching

- Symptoms lasting more than 24 hours

Recommendation: Consult a dermatologist or general physician.

---

## 🔴 High

Any of the following:

- Swelling of the lips, tongue, or throat

- Difficulty breathing

- Dizziness or fainting

- Rapidly spreading rash with breathing difficulty

Recommendation: Seek immediate emergency medical care as this may
indicate a severe allergic reaction (anaphylaxis).

---

# Home Care

- Avoid known allergy triggers.

- Apply cool compresses.

- Wear loose-fitting clothing.

- Avoid scratching the affected skin.

- Follow medical advice for symptom management.

---

# Prevention

- Identify and avoid allergy triggers.

- Use skin-friendly products.

- Manage stress effectively.

- Inform healthcare providers about known allergies.

---

# When to Consult a Doctor

Consult a dermatologist if:

- Hives last more than 24--48 hours.

- Symptoms occur repeatedly.

- Itching becomes severe.

- The cause is unknown.

---

# Emergency Warning Signs

Seek immediate emergency medical care if you experience:

- Difficulty breathing

- Swelling of the face, lips, or throat

- Loss of consciousness

- Severe allergic reaction
