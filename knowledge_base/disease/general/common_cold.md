# Common Cold

## Overview

The common cold is a mild viral infection of the nose and throat, usually harmless and resolving on its own within a week to ten days. It's one of the most frequent illnesses, with adults typically experiencing several colds a year.

**Note:** This condition is assessed from reported symptoms only — there is no relevant photo to screen for a cold, so the AI does not request an image for this body-part category.

---

# Common Symptoms

- Runny or stuffy nose
- Sore throat
- Sneezing
- Mild cough
- Mild fatigue
- Low-grade fever (sometimes)

---

# Causes

### Viral infection

Most commonly rhinoviruses, spread through respiratory droplets or contaminated surfaces.

---

# Risk Factors

- Close contact with an infected person
- Weakened immune system
- Seasonal changes (more common in cooler months)
- Lack of sleep or high stress

---

# Severity Classification

## 🟢 Low

- Mild congestion, sneezing
- No fever or very low-grade fever
- Normal energy levels

Recommendation: Home care and rest.

---

## 🟡 Moderate

- Persistent symptoms beyond 10 days
- Fever above 100.4°F (38°C)
- Significant fatigue affecting daily activity

Recommendation: Consult a general physician, especially if symptoms worsen instead of improving.

---

# Home Care

- Rest and stay hydrated.
- Use warm fluids (soups, herbal tea) to soothe the throat.
- Use a humidifier if congestion is significant.
- Over-the-counter symptom relief as advised by a pharmacist/doctor.

---

# Prevention

- Wash hands frequently.
- Avoid close contact with people who are sick.
- Avoid touching your face with unwashed hands.
- Maintain general good health (sleep, nutrition).

---

# When to Consult a Doctor

Consult a general physician if:

- Symptoms last beyond 10 days without improvement
- Fever is high or persistent
- Symptoms significantly worsen after initially improving

---

# Emergency Warning Signs

Seek prompt medical care if:

- Difficulty breathing or shortness of breath
- Persistent high fever
- Severe chest pain
