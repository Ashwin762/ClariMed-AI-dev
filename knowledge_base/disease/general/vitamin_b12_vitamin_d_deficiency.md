# Vitamin B12 / Vitamin D Deficiency

## Overview

Vitamin B12 and Vitamin D deficiencies are common nutritional conditions
that can affect energy levels, nerve function, muscle strength, and bone
health. These deficiencies often develop gradually and may cause a wide
range of symptoms if left untreated.

**Note:** This condition is assessed primarily from reported
symptoms. Laboratory testing is required to confirm the diagnosis.

---

# Common Symptoms

- Fatigue

- Weakness

- Muscle aches

- Bone pain

- Numbness or tingling in the hands and feet

- Difficulty walking

- Mood changes

- Poor concentration

---

# Causes

Vitamin deficiencies develop when the body does not receive or absorb
enough essential nutrients.

### Common causes

Poor diet, inadequate sunlight exposure, digestive disorders affecting
nutrient absorption, certain medications, and aging.

---

# Risk Factors

- Vegetarian or vegan diet (Vitamin B12)

- Limited sun exposure (Vitamin D)

- Older age

- Digestive disorders

- Pregnancy

- Obesity

---

# Severity Classification

## 🟢 Low

- Mild tiredness

- Occasional muscle aches

Recommendation: Consult a general physician for evaluation.

---

## 🟡 Moderate

- Persistent fatigue

- Numbness

- Bone pain

- Muscle weakness

Recommendation: Seek medical evaluation and laboratory testing.

---

## 🔴 High

Any of the following:

- Severe weakness

- Difficulty walking

- Frequent falls

- Confusion

- Significant neurological symptoms

Recommendation: Seek prompt medical evaluation.

---

# Home Care

- Eat a balanced, nutritious diet.

- Spend safe time outdoors for sunlight exposure when appropriate.

- Maintain regular physical activity.

- Follow your healthcare provider\'s recommendations.

---

# Prevention

- Eat foods rich in essential vitamins.

- Get appropriate sunlight exposure.

- Attend regular health check-ups.

- Seek evaluation if symptoms develop.

---

# When to Consult a Doctor

Consult a general physician if:

- Fatigue persists.

- Numbness develops.

- Bone pain continues.

- Symptoms interfere with daily activities.
