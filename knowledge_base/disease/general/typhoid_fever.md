# Typhoid Fever

## Overview

Typhoid fever is a bacterial infection spread through contaminated food or water, common in areas with inadequate sanitation. It causes a sustained, often gradually rising fever along with gastrointestinal symptoms, and requires antibiotic treatment.

**Note:** This condition is assessed from reported symptoms only — there is no relevant photo, so the AI does not request an image for this body-part category.

---

# Common Symptoms

- Fever that rises in a stepwise pattern over several days, often high by the end of the first week
- Prolonged fever lasting more than a few days
- Abdominal pain and discomfort
- Headache
- Weakness and fatigue
- Loss of appetite
- Constipation (more common in adults) or diarrhea (more common in children)
- Sometimes a faint rash of pink spots on the trunk

---

# Causes

### Bacterial infection

*Salmonella Typhi* bacteria, spread through food or water contaminated by the feces of an infected person.

---

# Risk Factors

- Consuming contaminated food or water
- Areas with poor sanitation infrastructure
- Travel to or living in endemic regions
- Close contact with a typhoid carrier

---

# Severity Classification

## 🟡 Moderate

- Fever present for several days
- Abdominal discomfort
- Able to eat and drink, no severe complications

Recommendation: See a doctor promptly for testing and antibiotic treatment — typhoid does not resolve without appropriate antibiotics.

---

## 🔴 High — Possible Emergency

Any of the following:

- Severe abdominal pain (possible intestinal perforation)
- Confusion or reduced consciousness
- Significant bleeding (blood in stool)
- High persistent fever not responding to treatment
- Signs of dehydration

Recommendation: **Seek emergency medical care immediately.** Untreated or complicated typhoid can be life-threatening.

---

# Home Care

Typhoid requires prescription antibiotics — it cannot be treated with home care alone. While awaiting medical evaluation: stay hydrated, eat easily digestible food, and rest.

---

# Prevention

- Drink only clean, treated, or boiled water.
- Avoid raw or unwashed food, especially from street vendors in high-risk areas.
- Wash hands thoroughly, especially before eating and after using the toilet.
- Typhoid vaccination is available and recommended in endemic areas.

---

# When to Consult a Doctor

Any suspected typhoid warrants prompt medical evaluation — blood or stool testing is needed to confirm diagnosis, and specific antibiotics are required for treatment.

---

# Emergency Warning Signs

Seek emergency care immediately if:

- Severe abdominal pain
- Confusion or reduced alertness
- Blood in stool
- High fever not responding to treatment
- Signs of significant dehydration

---

# Recommended Specialist

General Physician (emergency care for warning signs)
