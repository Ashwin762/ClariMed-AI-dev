# Food Poisoning

## Overview

Food poisoning results from consuming food or water contaminated with bacteria, viruses, parasites, or toxins, leading to gastrointestinal symptoms. Most cases are mild and resolve within a few days, though some can become severe, especially with significant fluid loss.

**Note:** This condition is assessed from reported symptoms only — there is no relevant photo to screen for food poisoning, so the AI does not request an image for this body-part category.

---

# Common Symptoms

- Nausea
- Vomiting
- Diarrhea
- Stomach cramps or pain
- Mild fever (sometimes)
- Fatigue and weakness

---

# Causes

### Contaminated food or water

Bacteria (e.g., Salmonella, E. coli), viruses (e.g., Norovirus), parasites, or toxins in improperly handled or stored food.

---

# Risk Factors

- Eating undercooked meat, eggs, or seafood
- Consuming unwashed produce
- Poor food storage or handling
- Drinking contaminated water
- Weakened immune system

---

# Severity Classification

## 🟢 Low

- Mild nausea or a few episodes of diarrhea
- Able to keep fluids down
- No fever

Recommendation: Home care with fluids and rest.

---

## 🟡 Moderate

- Persistent vomiting or diarrhea beyond 24 hours
- Moderate dehydration signs (reduced urination, dry mouth)
- Fever present

Recommendation: Consult a general physician, especially if not improving.

---

## 🔴 High

Any of the following:

- Signs of severe dehydration (dizziness, very little urination, confusion)
- Blood in vomit or stool
- High fever
- Symptoms lasting more than 3 days without improvement

Recommendation: Seek prompt medical attention.

---

# Home Care

- Stay hydrated with small, frequent sips of water or oral rehydration solution.
- Rest and avoid solid food until nausea settles, then reintroduce bland foods gradually.
- Avoid dairy, caffeine, and fatty foods while recovering.

---

# Prevention

- Wash hands before eating and after using the restroom.
- Cook meat, eggs, and seafood thoroughly.
- Store and refrigerate food properly.
- Wash fruits and vegetables before eating.
- Avoid drinking untreated water.

---

# When to Consult a Doctor

Consult a general physician if:

- Symptoms persist beyond 2–3 days
- Signs of dehydration appear
- Blood is present in vomit or stool
- Fever is high or persistent

---

# Emergency Warning Signs

Seek immediate medical care if:

- Signs of severe dehydration (dizziness, confusion, very little/no urination)
- Blood in vomit or stool
- High fever with severe abdominal pain
- Inability to keep any fluids down for an extended period
