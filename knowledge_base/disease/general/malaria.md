# Malaria

## Overview

Malaria is a mosquito-borne parasitic infection, common in many parts of India, especially during and after monsoon season. It typically causes cyclical episodes of fever, chills, and sweating, and can become severe rapidly if untreated, particularly with certain parasite species.

---

# Common Symptoms

- Cyclical pattern of fever, often with distinct cold, hot, and sweating stages
- Shaking chills
- Profuse sweating as fever breaks
- Headache
- Fatigue and body aches
- Nausea and vomiting
- In some cases, symptoms may not follow a clear cycle, especially early on

---

# Causes

### Parasitic infection

*Plasmodium* parasites, transmitted through the bite of infected female *Anopheles* mosquitoes, which typically bite between dusk and dawn.

---

# Risk Factors

- Living in or travelling to malaria-endemic areas
- Monsoon and post-monsoon season
- Standing water near the home (mosquito breeding sites)
- Not using mosquito nets or repellents

---

# Severity Classification

## 🟡 Moderate

- Cyclical fever with chills and sweating
- Able to eat, drink, and stay alert
- No confusion or severe symptoms

Recommendation: See a doctor promptly for blood testing and antimalarial treatment.

---

## 🔴 High — Possible Emergency (Severe Malaria)

Any of the following, especially with *P. falciparum* infection:

- Confusion, drowsiness, or seizures
- Difficulty breathing
- Very high fever not responding to treatment
- Dark or reduced urine output
- Jaundice (yellowing of skin/eyes)
- Repeated vomiting, unable to keep fluids down

Recommendation: **Seek emergency medical care immediately.** Severe malaria can be life-threatening within hours.

---

# Home Care

Malaria requires prescription antimalarial medication and confirmed diagnosis via blood test — it cannot be safely treated with home care alone. While awaiting medical evaluation: stay hydrated and rest.

---

# Prevention

- Sleep under insecticide-treated mosquito nets.
- Use mosquito repellents, especially from dusk to dawn.
- Eliminate standing water around the home.
- Consider antimalarial prophylaxis if travelling to high-risk areas (consult a doctor beforehand).

---

# When to Consult a Doctor

Any suspected malaria warrants prompt medical evaluation — a blood test is needed to confirm diagnosis and identify the parasite species, which determines treatment.

---

# Emergency Warning Signs

Seek emergency care immediately if:

- Confusion, drowsiness, or seizures
- Difficulty breathing
- Very high fever unresponsive to treatment
- Yellowing of skin or eyes
- Dark urine or reduced urination
- Repeated vomiting
