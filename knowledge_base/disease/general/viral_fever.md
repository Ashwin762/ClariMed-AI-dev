# Viral Fever

## Overview

Viral fever refers to a fever caused by a viral infection, often accompanied by body aches, fatigue, and headache. It's typically self-limiting, resolving within a few days to a week with rest and supportive care.

**Note:** This condition is assessed from reported symptoms only — there is no relevant photo to screen for a viral fever, so the AI does not request an image for this body-part category.

---

# Common Symptoms

- Fever (often 100–103°F / 38–39.4°C)
- Body aches and muscle pain
- Fatigue and weakness
- Headache
- Chills
- Sometimes sore throat or mild cough

---

# Causes

### Viral infection

Various viruses (influenza, and many other common respiratory/systemic viruses) causing a systemic immune response.

---

# Risk Factors

- Close contact with infected individuals
- Seasonal outbreaks
- Weakened immune system
- Crowded environments

---

# Severity Classification

## 🟢 Low

- Mild fever
- Manageable body ache
- Normal hydration and alertness

Recommendation: Home care, rest, and fluids.

---

## 🟡 Moderate

- Fever persisting beyond 3 days
- Significant fatigue affecting daily function
- Poor appetite

Recommendation: Consult a general physician for evaluation.

---

## 🔴 High

Any of the following:

- Fever above 103°F (39.4°C)
- Signs of dehydration
- Difficulty breathing
- Confusion or severe lethargy

Recommendation: Seek prompt medical attention.

---

# Home Care

- Rest and stay well hydrated.
- Use fever-reducing medication as advised by a doctor/pharmacist.
- Eat light, easily digestible food as appetite allows.
- Monitor temperature regularly.

---

# Prevention

- Wash hands frequently, especially during outbreak seasons.
- Maintain good general health and sleep.
- Avoid close contact with infected individuals where possible.

---

# When to Consult a Doctor

Consult a general physician if:

- Fever persists beyond 3 days
- Symptoms worsen instead of improving
- You have underlying health conditions (diabetes, heart/lung disease)

---

# Emergency Warning Signs

Seek immediate medical care if:

- High fever above 103°F (39.4°C)
- Stiff neck with fever
- Difficulty breathing
- Severe dehydration or confusion
