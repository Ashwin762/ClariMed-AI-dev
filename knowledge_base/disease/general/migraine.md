# Migraine

## Overview

A migraine is a neurological condition causing intense, often throbbing headaches, typically on one side of the head, frequently accompanied by nausea and heightened sensitivity to light or sound. Episodes can last from a few hours to several days.

**Note:** This condition is assessed from reported symptoms only — there is no relevant photo to screen for a migraine, so the AI does not request an image for this body-part category.

---

# Common Symptoms

- Throbbing or pulsing headache, often one-sided
- Sensitivity to light and/or sound
- Nausea, sometimes with vomiting
- Visual disturbances (in some cases, before the headache — known as aura)
- Worsening pain with physical activity

---

# Causes

The exact cause isn't fully understood but involves changes in brain activity affecting nerve signals, blood vessels, and chemicals.

### Common triggers

Stress, certain foods, hormonal changes, lack of sleep, bright lights, and dehydration.

---

# Risk Factors

- Family history of migraines
- Hormonal changes (more common in women)
- High stress levels
- Irregular sleep patterns
- Certain dietary triggers (caffeine, alcohol, processed foods)

---

# Severity Classification

## 🟢 Low

- Mild, manageable headache
- No significant disruption to daily activity

Recommendation: Home care and rest in a quiet, dark room.

---

## 🟡 Moderate

- Headache significantly affecting daily activities
- Frequent episodes (multiple per month)
- Nausea affecting eating

Recommendation: Consult a general physician for a management plan.

---

## 🔴 High

Any of the following:

- Sudden, "worst headache of your life"
- Headache with confusion, difficulty speaking, or weakness on one side
- Headache following a head injury

Recommendation: Seek immediate medical attention — these can indicate a more serious condition.

---

# Home Care

- Rest in a quiet, dark room.
- Apply a cool compress to the forehead.
- Stay hydrated.
- Use pain relief as advised by a doctor/pharmacist.

---

# Prevention

- Identify and avoid personal triggers (certain foods, lack of sleep).
- Maintain a regular sleep schedule.
- Manage stress levels.
- Stay well hydrated throughout the day.

---

# When to Consult a Doctor

Consult a general physician if:

- Migraines are frequent or worsening
- Over-the-counter treatments aren't providing relief
- Migraines are significantly disrupting daily life

---

# Emergency Warning Signs

Seek immediate medical care if:

- Sudden, severe "thunderclap" headache
- Headache with confusion, slurred speech, or one-sided weakness
- Headache after a head injury
- Headache with stiff neck and fever

---

# Recommended Specialist

General Physician (may refer to a Neurologist for recurring cases)
