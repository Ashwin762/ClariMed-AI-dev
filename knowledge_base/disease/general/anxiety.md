# Anxiety / Panic Attack

## Overview

Anxiety is a feeling of excessive fear, worry, or nervousness that can
interfere with daily life. A panic attack is a sudden episode of intense
fear that may cause physical symptoms such as rapid heartbeat, chest
tightness, sweating, and shortness of breath. Although panic attacks are
not usually life-threatening, their symptoms can resemble those of
serious medical conditions such as a heart attack.

**Note:** This condition is assessed primarily from reported
symptoms. There are no reliable visual signs that can be identified
through image analysis.

---

# Common Symptoms

- Excessive worry or fear

- Rapid heartbeat

- Chest tightness

- Shortness of breath

- Sweating

- Trembling

- Dizziness

- Feeling of losing control

- Restlessness

---

# Causes

Anxiety may develop due to a combination of psychological, biological,
and environmental factors.

### Common causes

Stress, traumatic events, family history, certain medical conditions,
and substance use.

---

# Risk Factors

- Family history of anxiety disorders

- Chronic stress

- Major life changes

- Previous trauma

- Other mental health conditions

---

# Severity Classification

## 🟢 Low

- Occasional anxiety

- Symptoms resolve quickly

Recommendation: Practice stress management and consult a healthcare
professional if symptoms become frequent.

---

## 🟡 Moderate

- Frequent anxiety

- Panic attacks affecting daily activities

- Difficulty concentrating

Recommendation: Consult a psychiatrist for evaluation.

---

## 🔴 High

Any of the following:

- Chest pain

- Difficulty breathing

- Loss of consciousness

- Thoughts of self-harm

- Panic symptoms that do not improve

Recommendation: Seek immediate emergency medical care to rule out
serious physical illness and obtain urgent mental health support.

---

# Home Care

- Practice deep breathing exercises.

- Maintain regular physical activity.

- Get adequate sleep.

- Reduce caffeine intake if it worsens symptoms.

- Reach out to trusted family or friends for support.

---

# Prevention

- Practice stress management techniques.

- Maintain a healthy lifestyle.

- Exercise regularly.

- Get sufficient sleep.

- Seek early help if anxiety becomes persistent.

---

# When to Consult a Doctor

Consult a psychiatrist if:

- Anxiety affects daily life.

- Panic attacks occur repeatedly.

- Sleep becomes disturbed.

- Symptoms continue for several weeks.

---

# Emergency Warning Signs

Seek immediate medical care if you experience:

- Chest pain

- Severe difficulty breathing

- Loss of consciousness

- Thoughts of self-harm or suicide

- Confusion
