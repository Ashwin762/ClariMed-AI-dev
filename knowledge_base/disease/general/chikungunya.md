# Chikungunya

## Overview

Chikungunya is a viral illness spread through the bite of infected
*Aedes* mosquitoes. It commonly causes sudden fever, severe joint
pain, headache, muscle pain, and skin rash. Most people recover within a
few weeks, although joint pain may persist for months in some cases.

**Note:** This condition is assessed primarily from reported
symptoms. Image analysis alone cannot confirm chikungunya.

---

# Common Symptoms

- Sudden high fever

- Severe joint pain

- Muscle pain

- Headache

- Skin rash

- Fatigue

- Joint swelling

---

# Causes

Chikungunya is caused by the chikungunya virus and is transmitted
through infected mosquito bites.

### Common causes

Bites from infected *Aedes aegypti* or *Aedes albopictus*
mosquitoes.

---

# Risk Factors

- Living in mosquito-prone areas

- Outdoor exposure

- Lack of mosquito protection

- Travel to affected regions

---

# Severity Classification

## 🟢 Low

- Mild fever

- Mild joint pain

Recommendation: Rest, stay hydrated, and consult a healthcare provider.

---

## 🟡 Moderate

- High fever

- Severe joint pain

- Rash

- Fatigue

Recommendation: Consult a general physician for evaluation and
supportive care.

---

## 🔴 High

Any of the following:

- Persistent high fever

- Severe dehydration

- Difficulty walking due to joint pain

- Confusion

- Symptoms in infants, older adults, or pregnant women

Recommendation: Seek immediate medical evaluation.

---

# Home Care

- Drink plenty of fluids.

- Get adequate rest.

- Protect yourself from further mosquito bites.

- Eat nutritious meals.

- Follow your healthcare provider\'s recommendations.

---

# Prevention

- Use mosquito repellents.

- Eliminate standing water around the home.

- Wear long-sleeved clothing.

- Use mosquito nets where appropriate.

---

# When to Consult a Doctor

Consult a general physician if:

- Fever lasts more than two days.

- Joint pain becomes severe.

- Rash worsens.

- Symptoms do not improve.
