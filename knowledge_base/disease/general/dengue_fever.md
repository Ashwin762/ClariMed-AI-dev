# Dengue Fever

## Overview

Dengue is a mosquito-borne viral infection common in tropical and subtropical regions, including much of India, especially during and after monsoon season. Most cases are self-limiting, but a small proportion progress to severe dengue, which is a medical emergency.

---

# Common Symptoms

- Sudden high fever (often 104°F / 40°C)
- Severe headache
- Pain behind the eyes
- Joint and muscle pain (hence "breakbone fever")
- Skin rash, usually appearing 2-5 days after fever onset
- Nausea and vomiting
- Fatigue

---

# Causes

### Viral infection

Dengue virus, transmitted through the bite of infected *Aedes* mosquitoes, which typically bite during the day.

---

# Risk Factors

- Living in or travelling to areas with active dengue transmission
- Standing water near the home (mosquito breeding sites)
- Monsoon and post-monsoon season in endemic regions
- Previous dengue infection (increases risk of severe dengue on reinfection)

---

# Severity Classification

## 🟡 Moderate

- High fever with headache, joint/muscle pain
- Rash present
- No bleeding or warning signs
- Able to stay hydrated

Recommendation: Rest, fluids, and monitor closely. See a doctor for confirmation testing.

---

## 🔴 High — Possible Emergency (Severe Dengue)

Any of the following warning signs, especially as fever begins to subside:

- Severe abdominal pain
- Persistent vomiting
- Bleeding gums or nose
- Blood in vomit or stool
- Extreme fatigue or restlessness
- Difficulty breathing
- Cold, clammy skin

Recommendation: **Seek emergency medical care immediately.** Severe dengue can progress rapidly.

---

# Home Care

- Rest and stay well hydrated with water, oral rehydration solution, or coconut water.
- Use paracetamol for fever and pain — **avoid aspirin and ibuprofen**, which increase bleeding risk in dengue.
- Monitor temperature and watch closely for warning signs, especially days 3-7.
- Use mosquito nets/repellents to avoid spreading infection to others via mosquitoes.

---

# Prevention

- Eliminate standing water around the home (flower pots, containers, coolers).
- Use mosquito repellents and nets, especially during the day.
- Wear long sleeves and pants in high-risk areas.
- Support community mosquito control efforts.

---

# When to Consult a Doctor

Consult a doctor promptly for any suspected dengue — blood tests confirm diagnosis and platelet counts need monitoring, which cannot be done at home.

---

# Emergency Warning Signs

Seek emergency care immediately if:

- Severe abdominal pain or persistent vomiting
- Bleeding from gums, nose, or in vomit/stool
- Difficulty breathing
- Cold, clammy skin or restlessness
- Symptoms worsening as fever drops (this is when severe dengue often develops)
