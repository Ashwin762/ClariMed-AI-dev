# Diabetes Warning Signs

## Overview

Diabetes is a condition where blood sugar (glucose) levels stay too high, either because the body doesn't produce enough insulin or doesn't use it effectively. Type 2 diabetes, the most common form, often develops gradually and can go unnoticed for years. Left unmanaged, it damages blood vessels and nerves throughout the body — but it's highly manageable once identified.

**Note:** This condition is assessed from reported symptoms only — blood sugar cannot be measured from a photo, so the AI does not request an image for this body-part category.

---

# Common Symptoms

- Excessive thirst
- Frequent urination, including at night
- Unexplained weight loss
- Increased hunger despite eating
- Persistent fatigue
- Blurred vision
- Slow-healing cuts or wounds
- Frequent infections
- Tingling or numbness in hands or feet

---

# Causes

### Type 2 diabetes

The body becomes resistant to insulin or doesn't produce enough of it, causing glucose to build up in the blood. Strongly linked to weight, diet, and activity level, though genetics also play a major role.

### Type 1 diabetes

An autoimmune condition where the body attacks insulin-producing cells — usually develops earlier in life and requires insulin from diagnosis.

---

# Risk Factors

- Overweight or obesity
- Sedentary lifestyle
- Family history of diabetes
- Age over 45
- History of gestational diabetes
- High blood pressure or abnormal cholesterol

---

# Severity Classification

## 🟡 Moderate

- Classic symptoms present (thirst, frequent urination, fatigue)
- No signs of acute complications

Recommendation: See a doctor promptly for a blood sugar test — early diagnosis significantly changes long-term outcomes.

---

## 🔴 High — Possible Emergency

Any of the following, which can indicate a dangerous blood sugar crisis (diabetic ketoacidosis or severe hyperglycemia):

- Rapid, deep breathing
- Fruity-smelling breath
- Severe nausea or vomiting
- Confusion or difficulty staying alert
- Extreme thirst with very frequent urination and rapid weight loss over days

Recommendation: **Seek emergency medical care immediately.**

---

# Home Care

Diabetes requires proper medical diagnosis and an individualized treatment plan — it is not a condition to self-manage based on symptoms alone. While awaiting evaluation: stay hydrated, avoid sugary drinks, and note your symptoms and their timeline to share with a doctor.

---

# Prevention

- Maintain a healthy weight.
- Stay physically active most days of the week.
- Choose whole foods over processed and sugary options.
- Get regular blood sugar screening, especially with risk factors present.

---

# When to Consult a Doctor

Consult a doctor promptly if you notice a combination of excessive thirst, frequent urination, unexplained weight loss, and fatigue — these together are a strong pattern worth testing for, even if each symptom alone seems minor.

---

# Emergency Warning Signs

Seek emergency care immediately if:

- Rapid, deep breathing or fruity-smelling breath
- Severe vomiting or inability to keep fluids down
- Confusion or reduced alertness
- Rapid worsening of symptoms over hours to days

---

# Recommended Specialist

General Physician (may refer to an endocrinologist for ongoing management; emergency care for warning signs)
