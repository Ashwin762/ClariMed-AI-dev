# Heat Stroke / Dehydration

## Overview

Heat stroke is a life-threatening condition that occurs when the body\'s
temperature rises rapidly and can no longer be controlled. It often
develops after prolonged exposure to high temperatures or strenuous
physical activity in hot weather. Dehydration commonly accompanies heat
stroke and can worsen the condition if not treated promptly.

**Note:** This condition is assessed primarily from reported
symptoms. Image analysis cannot reliably diagnose heat stroke or
dehydration.

---

# Common Symptoms

- High body temperature

- Excessive sweating or hot, dry skin

- Dizziness

- Headache

- Extreme thirst

- Weakness

- Nausea or vomiting

- Confusion

- Fainting

---

# Causes

Heat stroke occurs when the body is unable to cool itself effectively.

### Common causes

Prolonged exposure to hot weather, dehydration, strenuous physical
activity, wearing heavy clothing, and inadequate fluid intake.

---

# Risk Factors

- Hot and humid environments

- Older adults

- Infants and young children

- Outdoor workers

- Athletes

- Chronic medical conditions

---

# Severity Classification

## 🟢 Low

- Mild thirst

- Slight dizziness

- Fatigue

Recommendation: Move to a cool place and drink fluids.

---

## 🟡 Moderate

- Persistent dizziness

- Heavy sweating

- Muscle cramps

- Nausea

Recommendation: Seek prompt medical evaluation.

---

## 🔴 High

Any of the following:

- Body temperature above 40°C

- Confusion

- Loss of consciousness

- Seizures

- Hot, dry skin or inability to sweat

Recommendation: Seek immediate emergency medical care. Heat stroke is a
medical emergency.

---

# Home Care

- Move to a cool, shaded area.

- Drink water or oral rehydration fluids if fully alert.

- Loosen or remove excess clothing.

- Apply cool, wet cloths to the skin.

- Rest until symptoms improve.

---

# Prevention

- Drink plenty of fluids.

- Avoid prolonged exposure to extreme heat.

- Wear lightweight clothing.

- Take frequent breaks during outdoor activities.

- Avoid strenuous exercise during the hottest part of the day.

---

# When to Consult a Doctor

Consult a general physician if:

- Symptoms persist despite cooling and hydration.

- Repeated episodes of heat exhaustion occur.

- Weakness or dizziness continues.

---

# Emergency Warning Signs

Seek immediate emergency medical care if you experience:

- Confusion

- Loss of consciousness

- Seizures

- Body temperature above 40°C

- Difficulty breathing
