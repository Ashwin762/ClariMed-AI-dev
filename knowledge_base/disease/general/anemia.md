# Anemia

## Overview

Anemia occurs when the body lacks enough healthy red blood cells to carry adequate oxygen to tissues, most commonly due to iron deficiency. It causes persistent fatigue and paleness, and is especially common in menstruating women, pregnant women, and people with poor dietary iron intake.

# Common Symptoms

- Persistent fatigue and weakness
- Pale skin, lips, or inner eyelids
- Shortness of breath, especially with exertion
- Dizziness or lightheadedness
- Cold hands and feet
- Brittle nails
- Headache

---

# Causes

### Iron deficiency

Inadequate dietary iron intake, blood loss (heavy menstrual periods, gastrointestinal bleeding), or poor iron absorption.

### Other causes

Vitamin B12 or folate deficiency, chronic disease, or underlying blood disorders.

---

# Risk Factors

- Heavy menstrual periods
- Pregnancy
- Diet low in iron-rich foods (common in vegetarian/vegan diets without adequate planning)
- Chronic gastrointestinal conditions affecting absorption
- Frequent blood donation

---

# Severity Classification

## 🟢 Low

- Mild fatigue
- No significant impact on daily activities

Recommendation: Dietary iron increase and monitoring; consult a doctor for confirmation via blood test.

---

## 🟡 Moderate

- Persistent fatigue affecting daily activities
- Noticeable paleness
- Shortness of breath with mild exertion

Recommendation: Consult a doctor for blood testing (hemoglobin/ferritin levels) and treatment.

---

## 🔴 High

- Severe shortness of breath at rest
- Chest pain
- Fainting or severe dizziness

Recommendation: Seek prompt medical care.

---

# Home Care

- Eat iron-rich foods (leafy greens, legumes, red meat, fortified cereals).
- Pair iron-rich foods with vitamin C to improve absorption.
- Avoid tea/coffee with meals, as they can reduce iron absorption.
- Iron supplements should only be taken as advised by a doctor, after confirming deficiency via blood test.

---

# Prevention

- Maintain a balanced, iron-rich diet.
- Address heavy menstrual bleeding with a doctor if present.
- Routine blood testing during pregnancy and for those at higher risk.

---

# When to Consult a Doctor

Consult a doctor if:

- Fatigue and paleness persist beyond a few weeks
- Symptoms affect daily functioning
- You have heavy menstrual bleeding or suspect blood loss from another source

Anemia should be confirmed with a blood test — it cannot be diagnosed from symptoms alone, and the underlying cause needs to be identified.

---

# Emergency Warning Signs

Seek prompt medical care if:

- Severe shortness of breath at rest
- Chest pain
- Fainting or severe dizziness
- Rapid heartbeat with weakness
