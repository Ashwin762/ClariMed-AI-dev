# Tooth Sensitivity

## Overview

Tooth sensitivity is a sharp, short-lived pain triggered by hot, cold, sweet, or acidic foods and drinks. It occurs when the protective enamel thins or gums recede, exposing the underlying dentin and its tiny nerve channels.

**Note:** This condition is assessed primarily from reported symptoms — visible signs are often subtle or absent in a photo.

---

# Common Symptoms

- Sharp, sudden pain with cold food or drinks
- Discomfort with hot beverages
- Pain triggered by sweet or acidic foods
- Discomfort when brushing or with cold air
- Pain that subsides quickly once the trigger is removed

---

# Causes

### Enamel wear

Aggressive brushing, acidic diets, or grinding wear down protective enamel.

### Gum recession

Receding gums expose sensitive root surfaces not covered by enamel.

---

# Risk Factors

- Brushing too hard or with a stiff brush
- Teeth grinding (bruxism)
- Frequent acidic foods and drinks
- Gum disease or recession
- Recent dental procedures (usually temporary)

---

# Severity Classification

## 🟢 Low

- Occasional mild twinges
- Doesn't affect eating habits

Recommendation: Switch to desensitizing toothpaste and a soft brush.

---

## 🟡 Moderate

- Frequent, sharp pain affecting eating or drinking
- Not improving after several weeks of home care

Recommendation: See a dentist — may indicate decay, a crack, or gum recession needing treatment.

---

# Home Care

- Use a desensitizing toothpaste consistently for several weeks.
- Switch to a soft-bristled toothbrush and brush gently.
- Reduce acidic foods and drinks.
- Avoid brushing immediately after acidic foods.

---

# Prevention

- Brush gently with a soft brush.
- Limit acidic beverages; use a straw when you do drink them.
- Address teeth grinding (a night guard may help).
- Maintain regular dental check-ups.

---

# When to Consult a Doctor

Consult a dentist if:

- Sensitivity persists beyond a few weeks of home care
- Pain lingers well after the trigger is removed
- Sensitivity is localized to one tooth (may indicate decay or a crack)

---

# Emergency Warning Signs

Sensitivity alone is not an emergency, but seek prompt care if:

- Pain becomes severe, throbbing, or constant
- Facial swelling develops
