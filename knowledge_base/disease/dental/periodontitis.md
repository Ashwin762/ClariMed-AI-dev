# Periodontitis

## Overview

Periodontitis is an advanced gum infection that damages the soft tissue and destroys the bone supporting the teeth. It typically develops from untreated gingivitis and is a leading cause of tooth loss in adults.

---

# Common Symptoms

- Swollen, tender, or bleeding gums
- Receding gums (teeth appear longer)
- Persistent bad breath or bad taste
- Loose or shifting teeth
- Pain when chewing
- Pus between teeth and gums

---

# Causes

### Untreated plaque and tartar buildup

Bacteria beneath the gumline trigger a chronic inflammatory response that erodes bone and connective tissue.

---

# Risk Factors

- Untreated gingivitis
- Smoking or tobacco use
- Diabetes
- Poor oral hygiene
- Genetic predisposition
- Hormonal changes

---

---

# Severity Classification

## 🟡 Moderate

- Persistent bleeding and swelling
- Early gum recession
- Bad breath

Recommendation: See a dentist promptly — deep cleaning is likely needed.

---

## 🔴 High

Any of the following:

- Loose or shifting teeth
- Pus between teeth and gums
- Severe pain with facial swelling
- Fever

Recommendation: Seek prompt dental care — advanced infection risks tooth loss.

---

# Home Care

Periodontitis cannot be reversed with home care alone — professional treatment is required. While awaiting care: brush gently twice daily, floss carefully, and use an antiseptic mouthwash if recommended.

---

# Prevention

- Treat gingivitis early before it progresses.
- Brush twice daily and floss consistently.
- Get professional cleanings every six months.
- Avoid smoking.
- Manage diabetes well.

---

# When to Consult a Doctor

Any suspected periodontitis warrants prompt dental evaluation — this condition progresses and causes irreversible bone loss if untreated.

---

# Emergency Warning Signs

Seek prompt care if:

- Pus discharge from gums
- Severe pain with facial swelling
- Fever accompanying gum symptoms
- Rapidly loosening teeth
