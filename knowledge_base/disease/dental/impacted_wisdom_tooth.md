# Impacted Wisdom Tooth

## Overview

An impacted wisdom tooth occurs when the third molar does not have
enough space to erupt normally through the gums. It may remain partially
or completely trapped beneath the gum or bone, causing pain, swelling,
infection, or damage to nearby teeth.

**Note:** Swelling around the affected area may be visible, but
diagnosis usually requires a dental examination and imaging.

---

# Common Symptoms

- Pain at the back of the mouth

- Swollen or tender gums

- Jaw pain

- Difficulty opening the mouth

- Bad breath

- Unpleasant taste in the mouth

- Swelling around the jaw

---

# Causes

An impacted wisdom tooth develops when there is insufficient space in
the jaw for the tooth to erupt properly.

### Common causes

Crowded teeth, abnormal tooth position, delayed eruption, and jaw size.

---

# Risk Factors

- Age between 17 and 25 years

- Small jaw

- Family history

- Crowded teeth

- Delayed tooth eruption

---

# Severity Classification

## 🟢 Low

- Mild discomfort

- Occasional gum irritation

Recommendation: Schedule a routine dental evaluation.

---

## 🟡 Moderate

- Persistent pain

- Gum swelling

- Difficulty chewing

Recommendation: Consult a dentist for assessment.

---

## 🔴 High

Any of the following:

- Severe swelling

- Difficulty swallowing

- Difficulty opening the mouth

- Fever

- Spreading infection

Recommendation: Seek urgent dental care.

---

# Home Care

- Maintain good oral hygiene.

- Rinse with warm salt water.

- Avoid chewing on the affected side.

- Eat soft foods.

- Follow your dentist\'s recommendations.

---

# Prevention

- Attend regular dental check-ups.

- Monitor wisdom tooth eruption.

- Maintain excellent oral hygiene.

---

# When to Consult a Doctor

Consult a dentist if:

- Pain lasts several days.

- Gum swelling worsens.

- Difficulty chewing develops.

- Recurrent infections occur.
