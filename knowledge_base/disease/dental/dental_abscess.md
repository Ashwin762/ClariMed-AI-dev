# Dental Abscess

## Overview

A dental abscess is a pocket of pus caused by a bacterial infection
affecting the tooth, gums, or surrounding tissues. It usually causes
severe pain and swelling and requires prompt dental treatment. If left
untreated, the infection may spread to nearby tissues or other parts of
the body.

**Note:** Visible swelling may assist image analysis, but diagnosis
requires clinical examination by a dental professional.

---

# Common Symptoms

- Severe, throbbing toothache

- Swollen gums

- Facial swelling

- Sensitivity to hot or cold foods

- Bad taste in the mouth

- Pus drainage around the tooth

- Fever

- Difficulty chewing

---

# Causes

A dental abscess develops when bacteria enter the inner part of the
tooth or surrounding gum tissue.

### Common causes

Untreated tooth decay, gum disease, cracked tooth, poor oral hygiene,
and dental injury.

---

# Risk Factors

- Poor oral hygiene

- Untreated cavities

- Gum disease

- High sugar intake

- Smoking

- Weakened immune system

---

# Severity Classification

## 🟢 Low

- Mild localized tooth pain

- Small gum swelling

Recommendation: Schedule a dental appointment as soon as possible.

---

## 🟡 Moderate

- Persistent severe toothache

- Swollen gums with pus

- Difficulty chewing

Recommendation: Consult a dentist immediately.

---

## 🔴 High

Any of the following:

- Facial swelling

- Fever

- Difficulty swallowing

- Difficulty breathing

- Rapidly spreading infection

Recommendation: Seek immediate emergency medical or dental care.

---

# Home Care

- Maintain good oral hygiene.

- Rinse the mouth with warm salt water.

- Avoid chewing on the affected side.

- Eat soft foods until evaluated.

- Seek prompt dental treatment.

---

# Prevention

- Brush and floss daily.

- Attend regular dental check-ups.

- Limit sugary foods and drinks.

- Treat cavities early.

- Maintain good oral hygiene.

---

# When to Consult a Doctor

Consult a dentist if:

- Tooth pain persists.

- Gum swelling develops.

- Pus is present.

- Fever accompanies tooth pain.

---

# Emergency Warning Signs

Seek immediate medical care if:

- Facial swelling spreads rapidly.

- Difficulty breathing or swallowing.

- High fever.

- Severe facial pain.
