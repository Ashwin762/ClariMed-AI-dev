# Bruxism (Teeth Grinding)

## Overview

Bruxism is a condition in which a person unconsciously grinds, clenches,
or gnashes their teeth, often during sleep or periods of stress. Over
time, it can damage teeth, strain the jaw muscles, and lead to chronic
pain if left untreated.

**Note:** This condition is assessed primarily from reported
symptoms. Visible tooth wear may support the AI\'s assessment, but a
dental examination is required to confirm the diagnosis.

---

# Common Symptoms

- Grinding or clenching of teeth

- Jaw pain or stiffness

- Morning headaches

- Worn, flattened, or chipped teeth

- Tooth sensitivity

- Ear pain without infection

- Clicking or locking of the jaw

- Facial muscle soreness

---

# Causes

Bruxism may occur due to physical, psychological, or lifestyle-related
factors.

### Common causes

Stress, anxiety, sleep disorders, abnormal bite alignment, certain
medications, excessive caffeine intake, smoking, and alcohol
consumption.

---

# Risk Factors

- High stress levels

- Anxiety

- Sleep disorders

- Family history of bruxism

- Smoking

- Excessive caffeine or alcohol intake

---

# Severity Classification

## 🟢 Low

- Occasional teeth grinding

- Mild jaw discomfort

- No significant tooth damage

Recommendation: Reduce stress and schedule a routine dental evaluation.

---

## 🟡 Moderate

- Frequent grinding

- Jaw pain

- Tooth sensitivity

- Noticeable tooth wear

Recommendation: Consult a dentist for evaluation and appropriate
management.

---

## 🔴 High

Any of the following:

- Severe tooth damage

- Difficulty opening or closing the mouth

- Persistent jaw locking

- Severe facial pain

- Significant difficulty chewing

Recommendation: Seek prompt dental evaluation to prevent further
complications.

---

# Home Care

- Practice stress-reduction techniques.

- Avoid chewing hard objects such as ice or pens.

- Limit caffeine and alcohol, especially before bedtime.

- Maintain good sleep habits.

- Follow your dentist\'s recommendations.

---

# Prevention

- Manage stress effectively.

- Maintain good sleep hygiene.

- Attend regular dental check-ups.

- Avoid excessive caffeine, tobacco, and alcohol.

- Seek treatment for sleep disorders if present.

---

# When to Consult a Doctor

Consult a dentist if:

- Teeth grinding occurs regularly.

- Jaw pain persists.

- Teeth become worn or chipped.

- You experience frequent morning headaches.
