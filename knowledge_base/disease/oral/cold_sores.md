# Cold Sores

## Overview

Cold sores are small, fluid-filled blisters that typically appear on or around the lips, caused by the herpes simplex virus (HSV-1). They often begin with a tingling sensation before the blister forms, and typically heal within 1–2 weeks.

---

# Common Symptoms

- Tingling, itching, or burning sensation before blisters appear
- Small fluid-filled blisters, often clustered
- Blisters that burst and crust over
- Mild pain or tenderness in the area

---

# Causes

### Viral infection

Herpes simplex virus type 1 (HSV-1), which remains dormant in the body and can reactivate.

### Triggers

Stress, sun exposure, fever, hormonal changes, or a weakened immune system can trigger outbreaks.

---

# Risk Factors

- Previous HSV-1 infection (virus stays dormant for life)
- High stress levels
- Sun/wind exposure
- Weakened immune system
- Fatigue

# Severity Classification

## 🟢 Low

- Small, single cluster
- Mild tingling/discomfort

Recommendation: Home care; typically resolves within 1–2 weeks.

---

## 🟡 Moderate

- Large or spreading cluster
- Significant pain
- Frequent recurrence

Recommendation: Consult a doctor or dentist about antiviral treatment options.

---

# Home Care

- Avoid touching or picking at the blister to prevent spreading.
- Apply a cool compress for comfort.
- Keep the area clean.
- Avoid sharing utensils, cups, or lip products during an outbreak.

---

# Prevention

- Manage stress levels.
- Use lip balm with sun protection if sunlight is a known trigger.
- Avoid close contact (kissing, sharing items) during active outbreaks.

---

# When to Consult a Doctor

Consult a doctor if:

- Outbreaks are frequent or severe
- Sores don't heal within 2 weeks
- You have a weakened immune system and develop a cold sore

---

# Emergency Warning Signs

Seek prompt medical care if:

- Sores spread near the eyes
- Signs of secondary bacterial infection (increasing pain, pus, fever)
- Difficulty swallowing or breathing

---

# Recommended Specialist

Dentist (or General Physician for antiviral treatment)
