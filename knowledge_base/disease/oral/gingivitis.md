# Gingivitis

## Overview

Gingivitis is an early, common form of gum disease caused by plaque buildup along the gumline, leading to inflammation. It's generally reversible with good oral hygiene, but if untreated, can progress to more serious periodontal disease.

---

# Common Symptoms

- Red, swollen gums
- Bleeding during brushing or flossing
- Bad breath
- Tender gums
- Gums that appear puffy or shiny

---

# Causes

### Plaque buildup

Bacterial plaque accumulating along the gumline irritates gum tissue.

### Contributing factors

Poor oral hygiene, smoking, hormonal changes, certain medications, and inadequate nutrition.

---

# Risk Factors

- Infrequent brushing/flossing
- Smoking or tobacco use
- Diabetes
- Pregnancy or hormonal changes
- Dry mouth

# Severity Classification

## 🟢 Low

- Mild redness
- Occasional light bleeding

Recommendation: Improve brushing/flossing routine; monitor.

---

## 🟡 Moderate

- Persistent bleeding
- Noticeable swelling
- Bad breath

Recommendation: Consult a dentist for a professional cleaning and evaluation.

---

## 🔴 High

Any of the following:

- Loose teeth
- Pus at the gumline
- Severe pain

Recommendation: Seek prompt dental care — may indicate progression to periodontitis.

---

# Home Care

- Brush twice daily with a soft-bristled brush.
- Floss daily to remove plaque between teeth.
- Use an antiseptic mouthwash if recommended.
- Avoid tobacco products.

---

# Prevention

- Maintain a consistent oral hygiene routine.
- Get regular dental checkups and cleanings.
- Eat a balanced diet, limiting sugary snacks.
- Avoid smoking.

---

# When to Consult a Doctor

Consult a dentist if:

- Bleeding persists despite improved hygiene
- Gums remain swollen or tender
- You notice loose teeth or receding gums

---

# Emergency Warning Signs

Seek prompt dental care if:

- Pus discharge at the gumline
- Severe pain with facial swelling
- Loose or shifting teeth
