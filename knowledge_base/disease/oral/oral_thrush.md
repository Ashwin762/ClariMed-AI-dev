# Oral Thrush

## Overview

Oral thrush is a fungal infection caused by an overgrowth of Candida yeast in the mouth, appearing as creamy white patches — often on the tongue or inner cheeks — that can sometimes be gently scraped off, leaving a red area underneath.

---

# Common Symptoms

- White, slightly raised patches (often described as cottage-cheese-like)
- Redness or soreness underneath patches
- Burning sensation in the mouth
- Loss of taste or an unpleasant taste
- Slight bleeding if patches are scraped

---

# Causes

### Candida overgrowth

Normally-present yeast multiplies excessively due to a disrupted oral balance.

### Risk conditions

Weakened immunity, antibiotic use, inhaled steroid use, dentures, or diabetes.

---

# Risk Factors

- Infants and older adults
- Diabetes
- Weakened immune system
- Recent antibiotic or corticosteroid use
- Dry mouth
- Ill-fitting dentures

---

---

# Severity Classification

## 🟢 Low

- A few small white patches
- Mild or no discomfort

Recommendation: Home care and monitoring.

---

## 🟡 Moderate

- Widespread patches
- Pain affecting eating/swallowing
- Recurrent episodes

Recommendation: Consult a dentist or doctor for antifungal treatment.

---

# Home Care

- Maintain good oral hygiene — brush gently, rinse after meals.
- Clean dentures thoroughly and let them dry overnight if applicable.
- Avoid excess sugar, which can encourage yeast growth.

---

# Prevention

- Rinse mouth after using inhaled steroids.
- Manage underlying conditions like diabetes well.
- Replace toothbrush after infection clears.

---

# When to Consult a Doctor

Consult a dentist or doctor if:

- Patches don't improve within 1–2 weeks
- Pain affects eating or swallowing
- Thrush recurs frequently (may signal an underlying condition)

---

# Emergency Warning Signs

Seek prompt care if:

- Difficulty swallowing or breathing
- Spreading into the throat with fever

---

# Recommended Specialist

Dentist (or General Physician for underlying causes)
