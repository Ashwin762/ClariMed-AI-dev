# Fibromyalgia

## Overview

Fibromyalgia is a chronic condition characterized by widespread
musculoskeletal pain, fatigue, sleep disturbances, and problems with
memory and concentration. Although it does not damage the joints or
muscles, it can significantly affect daily activities and quality of
life. Early diagnosis and long-term management can help reduce symptoms.

**Note:** This condition is assessed primarily from reported
symptoms. There are no reliable visual signs that can be identified
through image analysis, so the AI relies mainly on symptom-based
assessment.

---

# Common Symptoms

- Widespread muscle and joint pain

- Chronic fatigue

- Tender areas throughout the body

- Difficulty sleeping

- Morning stiffness

- Headaches

- Difficulty concentrating (\"brain fog\")

- Anxiety or depression

---

# Causes

The exact cause of fibromyalgia is not fully understood, but it is
believed to involve abnormal pain processing by the brain and nervous
system.

### Common causes

Genetic factors, physical or emotional trauma, infections, chronic
stress, and abnormalities in pain perception.

---

# Risk Factors

- Female gender

- Family history of fibromyalgia

- Rheumatoid arthritis or other autoimmune diseases

- Chronic stress

- Previous physical trauma

- Sleep disorders

---

# Severity Classification

## 🟢 Low

- Mild widespread pain

- Occasional fatigue

- Minimal effect on daily activities

Recommendation: Maintain regular exercise and consult an orthopedist for
evaluation.

---

## 🟡 Moderate

- Persistent pain

- Fatigue affecting daily life

- Sleep disturbances

Recommendation: Consult an orthopedist for comprehensive evaluation and
long-term management.

---

## 🔴 High

Any of the following:

- Severe widespread pain

- Extreme fatigue

- Significant difficulty performing daily activities

- Persistent symptoms affecting mental well-being

Recommendation: Seek prompt medical evaluation for appropriate
management.

---

# Home Care

- Engage in regular low-impact exercise.

- Maintain a consistent sleep schedule.

- Practice stress management techniques.

- Eat a balanced diet.

- Pace daily activities to avoid overexertion.

---

# Prevention

Although fibromyalgia cannot always be prevented, the following may
reduce symptom severity:

- Exercise regularly.

- Manage stress effectively.

- Maintain healthy sleep habits.

- Seek early treatment for chronic pain conditions.

---

# When to Consult a Doctor

Consult an orthopedist if:

- Widespread pain lasts longer than three months.

- Fatigue becomes persistent.

- Sleep problems worsen.

- Daily activities become difficult.
