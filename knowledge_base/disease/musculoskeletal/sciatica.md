# Sciatica

## Overview

Sciatica refers to pain that radiates along the path of the sciatic nerve, from the lower back through the hip and down one leg, usually caused by compression of a nerve root in the spine. Most cases improve with conservative treatment within a few weeks.

**Note:** This condition is assessed from reported symptoms only — there is no reliable visual sign in a standard photo, so the AI does not request an image for this body-part category.

---

# Common Symptoms

- Sharp, shooting, or burning pain radiating from the lower back through the buttock and down one leg
- Numbness or tingling along the leg
- Muscle weakness in the affected leg or foot
- Pain worsening with sitting, coughing, or sneezing
- Usually affects one side only

---

# Causes

### Nerve root compression

A herniated disc, bone spur, or spinal narrowing pressing on the sciatic nerve root.

### Other contributors

Piriformis muscle spasm can also compress the nerve in some cases.

---

# Risk Factors

- Prolonged sitting
- Improper lifting technique
- Obesity
- Age-related spinal changes
- Sedentary lifestyle

---

# Severity Classification

## 🟢 Low

- Mild, intermittent pain
- No numbness or weakness
- Doesn't affect daily function significantly

Recommendation: Gentle activity, stretching, and monitoring.

---

## 🟡 Moderate

- Persistent pain radiating down the leg
- Numbness or tingling
- Affecting daily activities or sleep

Recommendation: Consult an orthopedist or physiotherapist for evaluation and treatment.

---

## 🔴 High — Possible Emergency

Any of the following ("red flags," same as low back pain):

- Loss of bladder or bowel control
- Numbness in the groin or inner thighs
- Progressive weakness in the leg
- Pain following major trauma

Recommendation: **Seek emergency medical care immediately.** These may indicate a serious spinal condition requiring urgent treatment.

---

# Home Care

- Stay gently active — avoid prolonged bed rest.
- Apply heat or cold for symptomatic relief.
- Avoid heavy lifting and prolonged sitting.
- Gentle stretching once acute pain subsides.
- Pain relief as advised by a doctor or pharmacist.

---

# Prevention

- Maintain good posture, especially when sitting for long periods.
- Use proper lifting technique.
- Strengthen core muscles.
- Stay physically active and maintain a healthy weight.

---

# When to Consult a Doctor

Consult an orthopedist if:

- Pain persists beyond a few weeks
- Numbness, tingling, or weakness develops
- Pain significantly affects daily activities or sleep

---

# Emergency Warning Signs

Seek emergency care immediately if:

- Loss of bladder or bowel control
- Numbness in the groin or inner thigh area
- Progressive leg weakness
- Sciatica following significant trauma

---

# Recommended Specialist

Orthopedist (or Physiotherapist for rehabilitation; emergency care if red flags present)
