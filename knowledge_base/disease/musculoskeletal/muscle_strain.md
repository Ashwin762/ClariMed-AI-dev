# Muscle Strain

## Overview

A muscle strain is an injury where muscle fibers are overstretched or torn, usually from sudden movement, overexertion, or inadequate warm-up. Most mild strains heal with rest within a couple of weeks.

**Note:** This condition is assessed from reported symptoms only — there is no reliable visual sign in a standard photo, so the AI does not request an image for this body-part category.

---

# Common Symptoms

- Sudden pain during or after activity
- Muscle soreness and tenderness
- Stiffness and reduced range of motion
- Localized swelling or bruising
- Muscle weakness in the affected area
- Pain that worsens with movement

---

# Causes

### Overstretching or overexertion

Sudden movements, lifting heavy objects incorrectly, or pushing a muscle beyond its capacity.

### Contributing factors

Inadequate warm-up, fatigue, poor conditioning, or muscle imbalance.

---

# Risk Factors

- Sudden increase in activity intensity
- Inadequate warm-up before exercise
- Poor flexibility or conditioning
- Previous muscle injury
- Fatigue

---

# Severity Classification

## 🟢 Low (Grade 1)

- Mild soreness
- Full range of motion retained
- Minimal strength loss

Recommendation: Rest and standard RICE care; typically heals in 1-2 weeks.

---

## 🟡 Moderate (Grade 2)

- Significant pain and swelling
- Noticeable strength loss
- Difficulty using the muscle normally

Recommendation: Consult an orthopedist or physiotherapist for assessment and rehabilitation.

---

## 🔴 High (Grade 3)

Any of the following:

- Complete loss of muscle function
- Severe pain with visible deformity
- A "pop" felt at the time of injury with immediate severe pain
- Inability to bear weight

Recommendation: Seek prompt medical care — may indicate a complete tear.

---

# Home Care

Follow the RICE approach for the first 48-72 hours:

- **Rest** the injured muscle.
- **Ice** for 15-20 minutes several times daily.
- **Compression** with an elastic bandage (not too tight).
- **Elevation** above heart level where practical.

Gradually reintroduce gentle movement as pain allows. Pain relief as advised by a doctor or pharmacist.

---

# Prevention

- Warm up properly before exercise.
- Increase activity intensity gradually.
- Maintain good overall conditioning and flexibility.
- Use correct lifting technique.
- Rest adequately between intense sessions.

---

# When to Consult a Doctor

Consult an orthopedist if:

- Pain doesn't improve within a week of rest
- You can't use or bear weight on the affected area
- Significant swelling or bruising develops
- You heard or felt a "pop" at the time of injury

---

# Emergency Warning Signs

Seek prompt medical care if:

- Complete inability to move or use the muscle
- Severe pain with visible deformity
- Numbness or tingling in the limb
- Signs of poor circulation (cold, pale, or blue skin below the injury)

---

# Recommended Specialist

Orthopedist (or Physiotherapist for rehabilitation)
