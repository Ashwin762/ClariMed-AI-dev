# Osteoarthritis

## Overview

Osteoarthritis is the most common form of arthritis, occurring when protective cartilage cushioning the ends of bones wears down over time. It commonly affects the knees, hips, hands, and spine, causing pain and stiffness that typically worsen gradually.

**Note:** This condition is assessed from reported symptoms only — there is no reliable visual sign in a standard photo, so the AI does not request an image for this body-part category.

---

# Common Symptoms

- Joint pain that worsens with activity and improves with rest
- Stiffness, especially in the morning or after inactivity (usually under 30 minutes)
- Reduced range of motion
- A grating or crackling sensation when using the joint
- Mild joint swelling
- Bony enlargements around affected joints (in the hands)

---

# Causes

### Cartilage breakdown

Protective cartilage gradually wears away, allowing bones to rub against each other.

### Contributing factors

Age, joint injury, repetitive stress, obesity, and genetics.

---

# Risk Factors

- Older age
- Obesity (particularly for knee and hip arthritis)
- Previous joint injury
- Repetitive joint stress from work or sport
- Family history
- Female sex (more common after menopause)

---

# Severity Classification

## 🟢 Low

- Occasional joint pain with activity
- Minimal stiffness
- Daily activities unaffected

Recommendation: Exercise, weight management, and monitoring.

---

## 🟡 Moderate

- Daily joint pain
- Persistent stiffness affecting activities
- Reduced mobility

Recommendation: Consult an orthopedist for a management plan (physiotherapy, pain management).

---

## 🔴 High

- Severe pain at rest and at night
- Significant loss of joint function
- Marked impact on independence

Recommendation: Consult an orthopedist to discuss advanced options, including joint replacement.

---

# Home Care

- Stay physically active with low-impact exercise (swimming, cycling, walking).
- Maintain a healthy weight to reduce joint load.
- Apply heat for stiffness or cold for acute pain.
- Use supportive footwear.
- Pain relief as advised by a doctor or pharmacist.

---

# Prevention

- Maintain a healthy weight.
- Stay active with joint-friendly exercise.
- Protect joints from injury during sport and work.
- Strengthen muscles around vulnerable joints.

---

# When to Consult a Doctor

Consult an orthopedist if:

- Joint pain persists for several weeks
- Stiffness or reduced mobility affects daily activities
- Pain disturbs sleep
- Joint swelling develops

---

# Emergency Warning Signs

Osteoarthritis is not typically an emergency, but seek prompt care if:

- A joint becomes suddenly hot, red, and severely swollen (may indicate infection or gout)
- Fever accompanies joint pain
- A joint becomes suddenly unusable after an injury

---

# Recommended Specialist

Orthopedist (or Rheumatologist for complex cases)
