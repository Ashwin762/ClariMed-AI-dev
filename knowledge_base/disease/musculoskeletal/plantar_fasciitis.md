# Plantar Fasciitis

## Overview

Plantar fasciitis is one of the most common causes of heel pain. It
occurs when the plantar fascia, a thick band of tissue connecting the
heel bone to the toes, becomes inflamed due to excessive strain. The
pain is usually worse with the first steps after waking up or after long
periods of rest.

**Note:** This condition is assessed primarily from reported
symptoms. Image analysis alone cannot diagnose plantar fasciitis.

---

# Common Symptoms

- Sharp pain in the heel

- Pain during the first steps in the morning

- Pain after standing for long periods

- Foot stiffness

- Pain after exercise

- Tenderness near the heel

---

# Causes

Plantar fasciitis develops due to repeated stress on the plantar fascia.

### Common causes

Prolonged standing, excessive walking or running, obesity, flat feet,
high arches, and improper footwear.

---

# Risk Factors

- Obesity

- Running or prolonged walking

- Occupations requiring long hours of standing

- Flat feet

- High arches

- Poor footwear

---

# Severity Classification

## 🟢 Low

- Mild heel pain after activity

Recommendation: Rest and monitor symptoms.

---

## 🟡 Moderate

- Daily heel pain

- Difficulty walking long distances

Recommendation: Consult an orthopedist.

---

## 🔴 High

Any of the following:

- Severe inability to bear weight

- Sudden severe foot pain after injury

- Significant swelling or deformity

Recommendation: Seek urgent medical evaluation.

---

# Home Care

- Rest the affected foot.

- Apply cold packs.

- Wear supportive footwear.

- Perform gentle stretching exercises.

- Avoid prolonged standing if possible.

---

# Prevention

- Wear well-fitting supportive shoes.

- Stretch before exercise.

- Maintain a healthy weight.

- Increase physical activity gradually.

---

# When to Consult a Doctor

Consult an orthopedist if:

- Heel pain lasts longer than a few weeks.

- Walking becomes difficult.

- Pain worsens despite home care.
