# Fracture / Suspected Broken Bone

## Overview

A fracture is a break or crack in a bone caused by trauma, falls, sports
injuries, or other medical conditions that weaken bones. Fractures can
range from small cracks to complete breaks. Prompt medical evaluation is
essential to ensure proper healing and prevent complications.

**Note:** Visible deformity or swelling may support image analysis,
but diagnosis requires medical examination and imaging such as X-rays.

---

# Common Symptoms

- Severe pain

- Swelling

- Bruising

- Visible deformity

- Inability to move the affected limb

- Tenderness

- Difficulty bearing weight

---

# Causes

Fractures occur when the force applied to a bone exceeds its strength.

### Common causes

Falls, road traffic accidents, sports injuries, direct blows,
osteoporosis, and repetitive stress injuries.

---

# Risk Factors

- Osteoporosis

- Older age

- Contact sports

- Previous fractures

- Poor bone health

---

# Severity Classification

## 🟢 Low

- Mild pain

- Small crack with minimal swelling

Recommendation: Seek prompt medical evaluation.

---

## 🟡 Moderate

- Significant pain

- Swelling

- Difficulty moving the limb

Recommendation: Visit a hospital or orthopedist as soon as possible.

---

## 🔴 High

Any of the following:

- Bone protruding through the skin

- Severe deformity

- Heavy bleeding

- Loss of sensation

- Suspected neck or spine injury

Recommendation: Seek immediate emergency medical care. Avoid moving the
injured area unnecessarily.

---

# Home Care

- Keep the injured limb still.

- Apply a cold pack wrapped in a cloth.

- Elevate the injured limb if possible.

- Do not attempt to straighten the bone.

- Seek medical care immediately.

---

# Prevention

- Wear protective sports equipment.

- Prevent falls at home.

- Maintain strong bones through a healthy lifestyle.

- Exercise regularly.

---

# When to Consult a Doctor

Consult an orthopedist immediately if:

- A fracture is suspected.

- Pain or swelling is severe.

- Movement is difficult.

- The injury follows major trauma.

---

# Emergency Warning Signs

Seek immediate emergency medical care if you experience:

- Bone visible through the skin

- Severe bleeding

- Loss of sensation

- Severe deformity

- Suspected spinal injury

---
