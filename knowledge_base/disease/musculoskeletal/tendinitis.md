# Tendinitis

## Overview

Tendinitis is inflammation or irritation of a tendon — the thick cords connecting muscle to bone. It commonly affects the shoulders, elbows, wrists, knees, and heels, typically from repetitive motion or overuse.

**Note:** This condition is assessed from reported symptoms only — there is no reliable visual sign in a standard photo, so the AI does not request an image for this body-part category.

---

# Common Symptoms

- Dull, aching pain near a joint, worsening with movement
- Tenderness when the area is pressed
- Mild swelling
- Stiffness, often worse in the morning
- Pain that improves with rest

---

# Causes

### Repetitive motion

Repeating the same movement over time irritates the tendon (common in sports and certain occupations).

### Sudden overload

An abrupt increase in activity intensity or an awkward movement.

---

# Risk Factors

- Repetitive occupational or sporting movements
- Sudden increase in training intensity
- Poor technique in sport or lifting
- Older age (tendons lose elasticity)
- Certain conditions (diabetes, rheumatoid arthritis)

---

# Severity Classification

## 🟢 Low

- Mild pain only during activity
- Minimal swelling
- Full range of motion

Recommendation: Rest, activity modification, and ice.

---

## 🟡 Moderate

- Pain during and after activity
- Persistent tenderness beyond 2 weeks
- Reduced function of the affected limb

Recommendation: Consult an orthopedist or physiotherapist.

---

# Home Care

- Rest the affected tendon and avoid aggravating activities.
- Apply ice for 15-20 minutes several times daily.
- Gentle stretching once acute pain subsides.
- Pain relief as advised by a doctor or pharmacist.
- Gradually return to activity, correcting technique.

---

# Prevention

- Warm up before physical activity.
- Increase training intensity gradually.
- Use correct technique in sport and at work.
- Take regular breaks from repetitive tasks.
- Strengthen supporting muscles.

---

# When to Consult a Doctor

Consult an orthopedist if:

- Pain persists beyond 2 weeks despite rest
- The affected area becomes significantly swollen
- Function of the limb is noticeably reduced
- The condition recurs frequently

---

# Emergency Warning Signs

Tendinitis is not typically an emergency, but seek prompt care if:

- You hear a "pop" and cannot use the limb (may indicate a tendon rupture)
- The area becomes hot, red, and severely swollen with fever
- Sudden severe pain with visible deformity

---

# Recommended Specialist

Orthopedist (or Physiotherapist for rehabilitation)
