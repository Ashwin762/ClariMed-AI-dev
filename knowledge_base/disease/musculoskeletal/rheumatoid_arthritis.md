# Rheumatoid Arthritis

## Overview

Rheumatoid arthritis (RA) is a chronic autoimmune disease in which the
body\'s immune system mistakenly attacks the lining of the joints. It
commonly affects the hands, wrists, knees, and feet, causing pain,
swelling, stiffness, and reduced joint function. Early diagnosis and
treatment can help prevent permanent joint damage.

**Note:** This condition is assessed using reported symptoms and,
when available, visible joint swelling. Image analysis alone cannot
confirm rheumatoid arthritis.

---

# Common Symptoms

- Painful joints

- Swollen joints

- Morning stiffness lasting more than 30 minutes

- Warm or tender joints

- Fatigue

- Reduced joint movement

- Symmetrical joint involvement

---

# Causes

Rheumatoid arthritis is an autoimmune condition in which the immune
system attacks healthy joint tissue.

### Common causes

The exact cause is unknown, but genetic factors, immune system
abnormalities, hormonal influences, and environmental triggers may
contribute.

---

# Risk Factors

- Family history

- Female gender

- Smoking

- Middle age

- Autoimmune diseases

---

# Severity Classification

## 🟢 Low

- Mild joint stiffness

- Occasional pain

Recommendation: Consult an orthopedist for evaluation.

---

## 🟡 Moderate

- Persistent joint swelling

- Daily pain

- Difficulty performing routine activities

Recommendation: Seek medical evaluation for diagnosis and treatment.

---

## 🔴 High

Any of the following:

- Severe joint deformity

- Inability to move affected joints

- High fever with swollen joints

- Severe pain affecting mobility

Recommendation: Seek urgent medical evaluation.

---

# Home Care

- Perform gentle stretching exercises.

- Maintain a healthy weight.

- Balance activity with adequate rest.

- Protect joints from excessive strain.

- Follow your healthcare provider\'s recommendations.

---

# Prevention

- Avoid smoking.

- Exercise regularly.

- Maintain a healthy weight.

- Attend regular medical check-ups.

---

# When to Consult a Doctor

Consult an orthopedist if:

- Joint pain lasts longer than six weeks.

- Morning stiffness persists.

- Swelling worsens.

- Daily activities become difficult.
