# Carpal Tunnel Syndrome

## Overview

Carpal Tunnel Syndrome (CTS) is a condition caused by compression of the
median nerve as it passes through the carpal tunnel in the wrist. It
commonly affects people who perform repetitive hand or wrist movements.
Early diagnosis and treatment can help prevent permanent nerve damage.

**Note:** This condition is assessed primarily from reported
symptoms. Image analysis cannot reliably diagnose Carpal Tunnel
Syndrome.

---

# Common Symptoms

- Tingling or numbness in the thumb, index, middle, and ring fingers

- Wrist pain

- Hand weakness

- Difficulty gripping objects

- Symptoms worse at night

- Burning sensation in the hand

---

# Causes

Carpal Tunnel Syndrome develops when pressure is placed on the median
nerve inside the wrist.

### Common causes

Repetitive hand movements, wrist injury, arthritis, diabetes, pregnancy,
and fluid retention.

---

# Risk Factors

- Repetitive keyboard or tool use

- Diabetes

- Pregnancy

- Obesity

- Rheumatoid arthritis

- Female gender

---

# Severity Classification

## 🟢 Low

- Mild tingling

- Occasional numbness

Recommendation: Reduce repetitive wrist activities and consult an
orthopedist if symptoms persist.

---

## 🟡 Moderate

- Frequent numbness

- Hand weakness

- Difficulty holding objects

Recommendation: Seek medical evaluation.

---

## 🔴 High

Any of the following:

- Severe hand weakness

- Permanent numbness

- Muscle wasting at the base of the thumb

Recommendation: Seek prompt specialist evaluation to prevent permanent
nerve damage.

---

# Home Care

- Rest the affected wrist.

- Avoid repetitive hand movements.

- Maintain proper wrist posture during work.

- Perform stretching exercises as advised by your healthcare provider.

- Follow your healthcare provider\'s recommendations.

---

# Prevention

- Take regular breaks during repetitive work.

- Maintain proper wrist ergonomics.

- Stretch hands and wrists frequently.

- Maintain a healthy body weight.

---

# When to Consult a Doctor

Consult an orthopedist if:

- Symptoms persist for several weeks.

- Hand weakness develops.

- Daily activities become difficult.

- Night-time symptoms worsen.
