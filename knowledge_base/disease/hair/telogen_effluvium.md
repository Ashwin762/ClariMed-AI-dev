# Telogen Effluvium

## Overview

Telogen effluvium is a temporary form of hair loss that occurs when a
larger-than-normal number of hair follicles enter the resting (telogen)
phase of the hair growth cycle. It commonly develops after physical or
emotional stress, illness, surgery, childbirth, or nutritional
deficiencies. Most people experience gradual hair regrowth once the
underlying cause is addressed.

**Note:** This condition may be assessed using both reported
symptoms and visible scalp findings. Image analysis may support the AI
assessment but cannot replace professional evaluation.

---

# Common Symptoms

- Excessive hair shedding

- Hair falling out while combing or washing

- Diffuse thinning across the scalp

- Increased hair on the pillow or shower drain

- Reduced hair volume

---

# Causes

Telogen effluvium occurs when hair follicles prematurely enter the
resting phase of the hair growth cycle.

### Common causes

Physical or emotional stress, childbirth, major surgery, high fever,
rapid weight loss, nutritional deficiencies, thyroid disorders, and
certain medications.

---

# Risk Factors

- Recent illness or surgery

- Emotional stress

- Pregnancy and childbirth

- Nutritional deficiencies

- Thyroid disorders

- Crash dieting

---

# Severity Classification

## 🟢 Low

- Mild increase in hair shedding

- No visible bald patches

Recommendation: Maintain a balanced diet and monitor hair growth.

---

## 🟡 Moderate

- Noticeable hair thinning

- Hair shedding lasting more than three months

Recommendation: Consult a dermatologist for evaluation.

---

## 🔴 High

Any of the following:

- Sudden severe hair loss

- Hair loss with scalp pain or inflammation

- Hair loss accompanied by other systemic symptoms

Recommendation: Seek prompt medical evaluation to identify the
underlying cause.

---

# Home Care

- Eat a balanced, nutrient-rich diet.

- Avoid excessive heat styling and harsh hair treatments.

- Manage stress effectively.

- Sleep adequately.

- Follow your dermatologist\'s advice.

---

# Prevention

- Maintain a healthy diet.

- Manage stress.

- Avoid crash dieting.

- Treat underlying medical conditions promptly.

- Attend regular health check-ups.

---

# When to Consult a Doctor

Consult a dermatologist if:

- Hair shedding continues for more than three months.

- Hair thinning becomes noticeable.

- Hair loss occurs with scalp redness or pain.

- You are unsure of the cause.
