# Scalp Folliculitis

## Overview

Scalp folliculitis is inflammation or infection of the hair follicles on the scalp, appearing as small, itchy, sometimes painful red bumps or pustules. Mild cases often clear on their own; persistent cases may need treatment.

---

# Common Symptoms

- Small red bumps or white-headed pustules around hair follicles
- Itching or tenderness
- Scalp redness
- Occasional crusting
- Mild pain in affected areas

---

# Causes

### Bacterial infection

*Staphylococcus* bacteria entering damaged or blocked follicles.

### Other contributors

Fungal infection, excessive sweating, occlusive hair products, tight headwear, or shaving irritation.

---

# Risk Factors

- Frequent use of heavy hair products or oils
- Wearing tight hats or helmets regularly
- Excessive sweating
- Weakened immune system
- Diabetes

# Severity Classification

## 🟢 Low

- A few small bumps
- Mild itching

Recommendation: Gentle scalp hygiene; often resolves on its own.

---

## 🟡 Moderate

- Widespread bumps
- Persistent itching or tenderness
- Not improving after a week or two

Recommendation: Consult a dermatologist — topical or oral treatment may be needed.

---

## 🔴 High

Any of the following:

- Large painful boils
- Spreading redness with fever
- Hair loss in affected areas with scarring

Recommendation: Seek prompt medical care — may indicate a deeper infection.

---

# Home Care

- Wash the scalp gently with a mild shampoo.
- Avoid heavy oils, pomades, and occlusive hair products.
- Avoid scratching or picking at bumps.
- Avoid tight hats or helmets during a flare-up.
- Wash hats, pillowcases, and combs regularly.

---

# Prevention

- Keep the scalp clean, especially after sweating.
- Avoid sharing combs, brushes, or hats.
- Limit use of heavy hair products.
- Manage underlying conditions like diabetes.

---

# When to Consult a Doctor

Consult a dermatologist if:

- Bumps persist beyond 1-2 weeks of gentle home care
- Bumps become painful, large, or pus-filled
- Hair loss develops in affected areas
- The condition recurs frequently

---

# Emergency Warning Signs

Seek prompt medical care if:

- Spreading redness with fever
- Large, very painful boils
- Signs of a deeper spreading infection
