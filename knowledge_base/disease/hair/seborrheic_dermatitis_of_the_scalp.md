# Seborrheic Dermatitis of the Scalp

## Overview

Seborrheic dermatitis is a common inflammatory skin condition that
affects the scalp, causing dandruff, redness, itching, and greasy
scales. It is not contagious and often occurs in areas with many
oil-producing glands. Symptoms may come and go over time.

**Note:** This condition may be assessed using both visible scalp
changes and reported symptoms. Image analysis may assist the AI but does
not replace professional evaluation.

---

# Common Symptoms

- White or yellow scalp flakes

- Itchy scalp

- Redness of the scalp

- Greasy patches

- Mild hair shedding

- Scalp irritation

---

# Causes

Seborrheic dermatitis develops due to inflammation associated with
excess oil production and an overgrowth of naturally occurring skin
yeast.

### Common causes

Overgrowth of *Malassezia* yeast, excess scalp oil, stress, hormonal
changes, cold weather, and certain neurological conditions.

---

# Risk Factors

- Oily skin

- Stress

- Parkinson\'s disease

- HIV infection

- Cold or dry weather

- Family history

---

# Severity Classification

## 🟢 Low

- Mild dandruff

- Occasional itching

Recommendation: Maintain regular scalp hygiene and monitor symptoms.

---

## 🟡 Moderate

- Persistent dandruff

- Noticeable scalp redness

- Frequent itching

Recommendation: Consult a dermatologist for evaluation.

---

## 🔴 High

Any of the following:

- Extensive scalp inflammation

- Crusting or oozing lesions

- Secondary skin infection

- Severe persistent itching affecting daily life

Recommendation: Seek prompt dermatological evaluation.

---

# Home Care

- Wash the scalp regularly.

- Avoid scratching the scalp.

- Manage stress effectively.

- Maintain good scalp hygiene.

- Follow your dermatologist\'s recommendations.

---

# Prevention

- Maintain regular hair washing.

- Reduce stress.

- Avoid harsh hair products.

- Keep the scalp clean.

---

# When to Consult a Doctor

Consult a dermatologist if:

- Dandruff persists despite good scalp care.

- Scalp redness worsens.

- There is significant hair shedding.

- Scalp becomes painful or infected.
