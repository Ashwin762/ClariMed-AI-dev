# Head Lice

## Overview

Head lice are tiny parasitic insects that live on the scalp and feed on
small amounts of blood. They spread mainly through direct head-to-head
contact and are especially common among school-aged children. Although
uncomfortable, head lice do not usually spread serious diseases.

**Note:** This condition can often be assessed using both visible
findings and reported symptoms. Image analysis may help identify lice or
nits (eggs), but confirmation by a healthcare professional may be
required.

---

# Common Symptoms

- Intense itching of the scalp

- Tickling sensation in the hair

- Visible lice moving on the scalp

- White or yellow nits attached to hair shafts

- Red bumps or sores from scratching

- Irritability or difficulty sleeping

---

# Causes

Head lice infest the scalp through direct contact with an infected
person\'s hair or, less commonly, by sharing personal items.

### Common causes

Direct head-to-head contact, sharing combs or brushes, sharing hats,
pillows, towels, or close contact in schools and childcare settings.

---

# Risk Factors

- School-aged children

- Close contact with infected individuals

- Sharing personal hair items

- Living in crowded environments

- Previous head lice infestation

---

# Severity Classification

## 🟢 Low

- Mild itching

- Small number of lice or nits

Recommendation: Consult a dermatologist or healthcare provider for
confirmation and appropriate treatment.

---

## 🟡 Moderate

- Persistent itching

- Multiple lice and nits

- Skin irritation from scratching

Recommendation: Seek medical evaluation to prevent spread and
complications.

---

## 🔴 High

Any of the following:

- Severe scalp infection

- Extensive sores or bleeding from scratching

- Swollen lymph nodes around the neck

- Signs of bacterial skin infection

Recommendation: Seek prompt medical care for evaluation and treatment.

---

# Home Care

- Avoid sharing combs, brushes, hats, or towels.

- Wash clothing, bedding, and personal items in hot water if
appropriate.

- Clean combs and brushes thoroughly.

- Check household members for lice.

- Follow your healthcare provider\'s treatment recommendations.

---

# Prevention

- Avoid head-to-head contact during outbreaks.

- Do not share personal hair care items.

- Perform regular scalp checks in children.

- Maintain good personal hygiene.

- Inform schools or childcare centers if an infestation is confirmed.

---

# When to Consult a Doctor

Consult a dermatologist if:

- Lice remain after appropriate treatment.

- Scalp sores develop.

- Itching becomes severe.

- Multiple family members become affected.
