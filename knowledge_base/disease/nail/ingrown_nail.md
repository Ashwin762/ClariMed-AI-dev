# Ingrown Nail

## Overview

An ingrown nail occurs when the edge of the nail grows into the surrounding skin, causing pain, irritation, and sometimes infection. It most commonly affects the big toe.

---

# Common Symptoms

- Pain along one or both sides of the nail
- Redness
- Swelling
- Tenderness when pressure is applied
- Occasional pus if infected

---

# Causes

### Improper nail trimming

Cutting nails too short or in a curved shape encourages ingrowth.

### Footwear pressure

Tight-fitting shoes push the nail into the surrounding skin.

### Injury

Trauma to the toe/finger can alter nail growth direction.

---

# Risk Factors

- Tight or narrow footwear
- Nail trimming habits (cutting too short or rounded)
- Sweaty feet
- Previous nail injury

# Severity Classification

## 🟢 Low

- Mild redness at the nail edge
- Slight tenderness
- No pus

Recommendation: Home care — warm soaks and careful nail trimming.

---

## 🟡 Moderate

- Persistent pain
- Increasing swelling
- Discomfort with footwear

Recommendation: Consult a dermatologist if it doesn't improve within a few days.

---

## 🔴 High

Any of the following:

- Pus discharge
- Spreading redness
- Fever

Recommendation: Seek prompt medical attention.

---

# Home Care

- Soak the foot/hand in warm water for 15–20 minutes, a few times daily.
- Gently lift the nail edge away from the skin with clean cotton, if tolerable.
- Wear roomy, open-toed footwear where possible during healing.
- Keep the area clean and dry.

---

# Prevention

- Trim nails straight across, not rounded.
- Avoid cutting nails too short.
- Choose well-fitting, non-restrictive footwear.
- Keep feet clean and dry.

---

# When to Consult a Doctor

Consult a dermatologist if:

- Pain or swelling worsens despite home care
- Pus develops
- Ingrown nails recur frequently

---

# Emergency Warning Signs

Seek urgent care if:

- Signs of spreading infection (redness moving up the toe/finger)
- Fever accompanying nail pain
- You have diabetes and notice any infection signs (higher complication risk)
