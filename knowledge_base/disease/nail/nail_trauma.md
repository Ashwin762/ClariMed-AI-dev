# Nail Trauma (Subungual Hematoma)

## Overview

A subungual hematoma is a collection of blood beneath the fingernail or
toenail, usually caused by direct injury or trauma. It often causes
severe throbbing pain and discoloration of the nail. Depending on the
severity, the nail may eventually loosen or fall off.

**Note:** This condition can often be assessed using both visible
nail changes and reported symptoms. However, a medical examination may
be required to determine whether there is an underlying fracture or
significant nail-bed injury.

---

# Common Symptoms

- Black or dark purple discoloration under the nail

- Severe throbbing pain

- Swollen fingertip or toe

- Tenderness around the nail

- Partial lifting of the nail

- Difficulty using the affected finger or toe

---

# Causes

A subungual hematoma occurs when blood vessels beneath the nail break
due to injury.

### Common causes

Heavy object falling on the finger or toe, sports injuries, door crush
injuries, repetitive trauma, and workplace accidents.

---

# Risk Factors

- Participation in sports

- Manual labor

- Wearing tight footwear

- Previous nail injuries

- Poor protective equipment

---

# Severity Classification

## 🟢 Low

- Small bruise under the nail

- Mild pain

Recommendation: Apply a cold compress and monitor symptoms.

---

## 🟡 Moderate

- Larger blood collection

- Persistent pain

- Difficulty using the finger or toe

Recommendation: Consult a dermatologist or healthcare professional for
evaluation.

---

## 🔴 High

Any of the following:

- Severe pain with extensive blood under the nail

- Suspected broken finger or toe

- Nail completely detached

- Heavy bleeding or open wound

Recommendation: Seek immediate medical care.

---

# Home Care

- Apply a cold compress during the first 24 hours.

- Elevate the affected hand or foot.

- Keep the nail clean and dry.

- Avoid removing the nail yourself.

- Protect the injured nail from further trauma.

---

# Prevention

- Wear protective footwear and gloves.

- Use caution while handling heavy objects.

- Wear appropriate sports protective gear.

- Keep work areas safe.

---

# When to Consult a Doctor

Consult a doctor if:

- Pain becomes severe.

- The nail lifts completely.

- Blood covers most of the nail.

- You suspect a fracture.

---

# Emergency Warning Signs

Seek immediate medical care if you experience:

- Severe bleeding

- Suspected broken bone

- Complete nail avulsion

- Signs of infection (pus, increasing redness, fever)
