# Paronychia

## Overview

Paronychia is an infection of the skin fold surrounding the nail, causing redness, swelling, and pain. It can be acute (sudden, usually bacterial) or chronic (longer-lasting, often related to repeated moisture exposure).

---

# Common Symptoms

- Redness around the nail fold
- Swelling
- Pain or tenderness
- Warmth in the affected area
- Pus formation in more severe cases

---

# Causes

### Bacterial infection

Often follows minor trauma such as nail-biting, hangnail picking, or manicure injury.

### Fungal infection (chronic form)

Repeated moisture exposure allows fungal overgrowth in the nail fold.

---

# Risk Factors

- Nail-biting or cuticle picking
- Frequent hand-washing or wet work (dishwashing, cleaning)
- Ingrown nails
- Diabetes

# Severity Classification

## 🟢 Low

- Mild redness
- Slight tenderness
- No pus

Recommendation: Warm water soaks and home care.

---

## 🟡 Moderate

- Increasing swelling and pain
- Visible pus pocket
- Symptoms lasting more than a few days

Recommendation: Consult a dermatologist — may require drainage.

---

## 🔴 High

Any of the following:

- Spreading redness up the finger/toe
- Fever
- Severe pain

Recommendation: Seek prompt medical attention.

---

# Home Care

- Soak the affected area in warm water several times a day.
- Keep the area clean and dry afterward.
- Avoid further trauma to the nail fold.
- Avoid nail-biting during healing.

---

# Prevention

- Avoid biting nails or picking at cuticles.
- Keep hands dry during wet work; use gloves when possible.
- Trim nails carefully to avoid injury to the nail fold.

---

# When to Consult a Doctor

Consult a dermatologist if:

- Swelling and pain worsen despite home care
- Pus develops
- Infection recurs frequently (may indicate chronic paronychia)

---

# Emergency Warning Signs

Seek urgent care if:

- Pus with fever
- Spreading redness up the finger or toe
- Signs of a deeper hand/foot infection
