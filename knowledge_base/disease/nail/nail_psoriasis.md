# Nail Psoriasis

## Overview

Nail psoriasis is a manifestation of psoriasis affecting the nails, causing pitting, discoloration, thickening, and sometimes separation of the nail from the nail bed. It often occurs alongside skin psoriasis, though it can appear on its own.

---

# Common Symptoms

- Small pits or dents in the nail surface
- White or yellow-brown discoloration patches
- Thickened nail plate
- Crumbling nail edges in advanced cases
- Nail separating from the nail bed (in some cases)

---

# Causes

### Autoimmune process

The same immune dysfunction driving skin psoriasis affects nail-bed skin cells, disrupting normal nail growth.

---

# Risk Factors

- Personal or family history of psoriasis
- Existing psoriatic arthritis
- Nail trauma, which can trigger new changes

---

---

# Severity Classification

## 🟢 Low

- A few small white spots or pits
- No thickening
- No discomfort

Recommendation: Moisturize and monitor; manage alongside any skin psoriasis care.

---

## 🟡 Moderate

- Multiple nails affected
- Noticeable thickening or crumbling
- Mild discomfort

Recommendation: Consult a dermatologist for a coordinated management plan.

---

# Home Care

- Keep nails trimmed and clean.
- Moisturize the nail and surrounding cuticle regularly.
- Avoid trauma to the nails (aggressive manicures, biting).

---

# Prevention

- Manage underlying skin psoriasis consistently.
- Protect nails from injury.
- Avoid harsh manicure procedures on affected nails.

---

# When to Consult a Doctor

Consult a dermatologist for:

- Any new or worsening nail changes alongside known psoriasis
- Nail separation from the nail bed
- Discomfort affecting daily activities

---

# Emergency Warning Signs

Nail psoriasis alone is not typically an emergency, but seek care if:

- Signs of secondary infection develop (pus, spreading redness, pain)

---
