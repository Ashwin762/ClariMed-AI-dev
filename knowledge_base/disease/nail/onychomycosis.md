# Onychomycosis

## Overview

Onychomycosis is a fungal infection of the nail, causing discoloration (often yellow or brown), thickening, and brittleness. It develops slowly and most commonly affects toenails, often linked to warm, moist environments.

---

# Common Symptoms

- Yellow or brownish discoloration
- Thickened nail plate
- Brittle or crumbling nail edges
- Distorted nail shape in advanced cases
- Mild odor in some cases

---

# Causes

### Fungal infection

Dermatophyte fungi (similar to those causing ringworm) infect the nail bed.

### Risk environments

Moist environments such as shared showers, swimming pools, and tight, non-breathable footwear.

---

# Risk Factors

- Older age
- Diabetes or poor circulation
- Excessive sweating
- Tight-fitting shoes
- Previous fungal skin infections (e.g., athlete's foot)

---

# Severity Classification

## 🟢 Low

- Mild discoloration on part of one nail
- No pain
- No thickening

Recommendation: Home care and monitoring, OTC antifungal treatment as advised.

---

## 🟡 Moderate

- Discoloration spreading across the nail
- Noticeable thickening
- Mild discomfort in footwear

Recommendation: Consult a dermatologist for treatment options.

---

# Home Care

- Keep nails clean, dry, and trimmed.
- Avoid sharing nail clippers or files.
- Wear moisture-wicking socks and breathable footwear.
- Disinfect footwear regularly.

---

# Prevention

- Keep feet dry, especially between toes.
- Avoid walking barefoot in public showers or pools.
- Don't share nail grooming tools.
- Treat athlete's foot promptly if present.

---

# When to Consult a Doctor

Consult a dermatologist if:

- Discoloration or thickening persists or spreads
- Nail becomes painful
- You have diabetes or circulation issues (higher complication risk)

---

# Emergency Warning Signs

Seek medical attention if:

- Spreading infection with pain, swelling, or fever
- Signs of skin breakdown around the nail in a person with diabetes
