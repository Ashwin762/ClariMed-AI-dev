# Angina (Exertional Chest Pain)

## Overview

Angina is chest discomfort that occurs when the heart muscle isn't getting enough oxygen-rich blood, usually due to narrowed coronary arteries. Classic ("stable") angina is triggered predictably by exertion or stress and eases with rest — but any new, worsening, or rest-onset chest pain needs urgent evaluation, since it can signal an impending heart attack.

# Common Symptoms

- Chest tightness, pressure, or squeezing brought on by physical exertion, stress, or cold weather
- Discomfort that eases within minutes of resting
- Pain that may spread to the arm, jaw, neck, or back
- Shortness of breath during episodes
- Predictable pattern — the same amount of activity tends to trigger it

---

# Causes

### Coronary artery narrowing

Fatty deposits (plaque) narrow the coronary arteries, limiting blood flow to the heart muscle when demand increases, such as during exercise.

---

# Risk Factors

- High blood pressure, high cholesterol, or diabetes
- Smoking
- Family history of heart disease
- Sedentary lifestyle and obesity
- Age

---

# Severity Classification

## 🟡 Moderate

- Chest discomfort with exertion that reliably eases with rest, matching a known prior pattern
- No new worsening

Recommendation: Consult a cardiologist for evaluation and management — this pattern, even when "typical," still needs proper diagnosis and treatment.

---

## 🔴 High — Possible Emergency

Any of the following — these suggest the pattern is becoming unstable, a warning sign for heart attack:

- Chest pain occurring at rest or waking you from sleep
- Pain lasting longer than usual or not relieved by rest
- New chest pain that's more frequent, severe, or triggered by less activity than before
- Chest pain with cold sweat, nausea, or severe shortness of breath

Recommendation: **Seek emergency medical care immediately.** This pattern can precede a heart attack.

---

# Home Care

Angina requires proper medical evaluation and treatment — it is not a condition to self-manage. While awaiting evaluation: avoid known triggers, stop activity immediately if discomfort starts, and use prescribed medication (such as nitroglycerin) exactly as directed if already diagnosed.

---

# Prevention

- Manage blood pressure, cholesterol, and blood sugar.
- Don't smoke.
- Stay physically active within limits advised by your doctor.
- Maintain a healthy weight and heart-healthy diet.

---

# When to Consult a Doctor

Any new exertional chest discomfort needs prompt cardiology evaluation, even if it fits the "classic, resolves with rest" pattern — proper diagnosis and treatment reduce heart attack risk significantly.

---

# Emergency Warning Signs

Seek emergency care immediately if chest pain:

- Occurs at rest or wakes you from sleep
- Lasts longer than usual or isn't relieved by rest
- Is new, worsening, or triggered by less activity than before
- Comes with cold sweat, nausea, or severe shortness of breath
