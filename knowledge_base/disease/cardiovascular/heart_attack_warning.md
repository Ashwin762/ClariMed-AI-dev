# Heart Attack Warning Signs

## Overview

A heart attack occurs when blood flow to part of the heart muscle is suddenly blocked, usually by a clot in a narrowed artery. It is a medical emergency where every minute of delay increases the risk of permanent heart damage or death. Recognizing the warning signs and acting immediately — not waiting to "see if it passes" — is the single most important factor in survival.

**Note:** This condition is assessed from reported symptoms only — it cannot be assessed from a photo, and it should never be evaluated through an app during an active episode. If these symptoms are happening right now, call emergency services immediately rather than continuing to use this screening.

---

# Common Symptoms

- Chest pain, pressure, squeezing, or fullness — often described as an "elephant sitting on the chest"
- Pain radiating to the arm (especially the left), jaw, neck, back, or stomach
- Shortness of breath, with or without chest discomfort
- Cold sweat
- Nausea or vomiting
- Lightheadedness or sudden dizziness
- **Symptoms can be less classic in women, older adults, and people with diabetes** — sometimes presenting as unusual fatigue, indigestion-like discomfort, or shortness of breath alone, without dramatic chest pain

---

# Causes

### Coronary artery blockage

A blood clot forms over a narrowed, plaque-lined coronary artery, suddenly cutting off blood supply to part of the heart muscle.

---

# Risk Factors

- High blood pressure, high cholesterol, or diabetes
- Smoking
- Family history of heart disease
- Obesity and sedentary lifestyle
- Age (risk increases with age, though heart attacks can occur at any age)
- Prior heart attack or known coronary artery disease

---

# Severity Classification

## 🔴 High — This Is Always a Possible Emergency

Any combination of the symptoms above, especially chest pressure lasting more than a few minutes, or pain radiating to the arm/jaw with shortness of breath or cold sweat, must be treated as a possible heart attack.

Recommendation: **Call emergency services immediately. Do not drive yourself. Do not wait to see if it improves. Chew an aspirin if available and not allergic, while waiting for help, if advised by emergency dispatch.**

There is no "moderate" or "low" tier for this condition — any credible presentation is treated as a medical emergency by design.

---

# Home Care

There is no safe home care for a suspected heart attack. This is an emergency-only condition. While waiting for emergency services:

- Sit down and try to stay calm.
- Loosen tight clothing.
- If prescribed nitroglycerin, use as directed by a doctor.
- Do not attempt to drive yourself to a hospital if symptoms are severe.

---

# Prevention

- Manage blood pressure, cholesterol, and blood sugar.
- Don't smoke.
- Stay physically active and maintain a healthy weight.
- Eat a heart-healthy diet.
- Know your family history and get regular checkups.

---

# When to Consult a Doctor

This is not a "schedule an appointment" condition. Any credible presentation requires **emergency care immediately**, not a routine consultation.

---

# Emergency Warning Signs

**All of the symptoms in this condition are, by definition, emergency warning signs.** Call emergency services immediately for:

- Chest pain, pressure, or squeezing lasting more than a few minutes
- Pain spreading to the arm, jaw, neck, or back
- Shortness of breath with or without chest pain
- Cold sweat, nausea, or lightheadedness accompanying chest discomfort

---

# Recommended Specialist

Emergency care first, always. Cardiologist for follow-up care after emergency treatment.
