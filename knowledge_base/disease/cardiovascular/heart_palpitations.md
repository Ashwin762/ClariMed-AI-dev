# Heart Palpitations

## Overview

Palpitations are the sensation of your own heartbeat — feeling like it's racing, pounding, fluttering, or skipping beats. Most palpitations are harmless and triggered by caffeine, stress, or exertion, but some can signal an underlying heart rhythm problem (arrhythmia) that needs evaluation, especially when they occur alongside other symptoms.

---

# Common Symptoms

- A sensation of the heart racing, pounding, or fluttering
- Feeling like the heart "skipped a beat"
- Awareness of your own heartbeat when you normally wouldn't notice it
- Sometimes accompanied by mild lightheadedness
- Episodes may last seconds to several minutes

---

# Causes

### Common, usually harmless triggers

Caffeine, alcohol, nicotine, stress, anxiety, dehydration, exercise, or hormonal changes.

### Underlying causes needing evaluation

Thyroid problems, anemia, certain medications, or a true cardiac arrhythmia (such as atrial fibrillation).

---

# Risk Factors

- High caffeine or stimulant intake
- Anxiety or high stress levels
- Thyroid disorders
- Existing heart conditions
- Dehydration or electrolyte imbalance

---

# Severity Classification

## 🟢 Low

- Brief, occasional palpitations
- Clear trigger identifiable (caffeine, stress, exercise)
- No chest pain, fainting, or breathlessness

Recommendation: Monitor and reduce known triggers; mention at your next routine checkup if it continues.

---

## 🟡 Moderate

- Frequent or prolonged episodes
- No clear trigger
- Mild lightheadedness without fainting

Recommendation: Consult a cardiologist for an ECG and evaluation, especially if episodes are recurring.

---

## 🔴 High — Possible Emergency

Any of the following:

- Palpitations with chest pain
- Palpitations with fainting or near-fainting
- Severe shortness of breath
- A very rapid, sustained heartbeat that doesn't settle

Recommendation: **Seek emergency medical care immediately.**

---

# Home Care

- Reduce caffeine, alcohol, and nicotine.
- Stay well hydrated.
- Manage stress and anxiety with relaxation techniques.
- Keep a log of when episodes happen and any triggers, to share with a doctor.

---

# Prevention

- Limit stimulants (caffeine, energy drinks).
- Manage stress levels.
- Stay hydrated and maintain balanced electrolytes.
- Get underlying conditions like thyroid disorders properly managed.

---

# When to Consult a Doctor

Consult a cardiologist if:

- Palpitations are frequent, prolonged, or worsening
- There's no obvious trigger
- You have a family history of heart rhythm problems

---

# Emergency Warning Signs

Seek emergency care immediately if palpitations occur with:

- Chest pain or pressure
- Fainting or near-fainting
- Severe shortness of breath
- A sustained very rapid heartbeat

---
