# Hypertension

## Overview

Hypertension (high blood pressure) is a chronic condition where the force of blood against artery walls stays persistently elevated. It rarely causes symptoms early on — often called a "silent" condition — but over years it significantly raises the risk of heart attack, stroke, and kidney disease. It is one of the most common conditions worldwide and is highly manageable once identified.

**Note:** This condition is assessed from reported symptoms only — blood pressure cannot be measured from a photo, so the AI does not request an image for this body-part category.

---

# Common Symptoms

- Often no symptoms at all — frequently discovered during a routine check
- Headache, especially at the back of the head
- Dizziness
- Blurred or disturbed vision
- Nosebleeds
- A general feeling of chest discomfort or pounding heartbeat in some cases

---

# Causes

### Primary (essential) hypertension

No single identifiable cause — develops gradually from a combination of genetics, diet, and lifestyle factors over years.

### Secondary hypertension

Caused by an underlying condition such as kidney disease, thyroid problems, or certain medications.

---

# Risk Factors

- Family history of high blood pressure
- High salt intake
- Obesity or being overweight
- Sedentary lifestyle
- Smoking and heavy alcohol use
- Chronic stress
- Age (risk increases with age)

---

# Severity Classification

## 🟡 Moderate

- Elevated readings on routine or home checks
- No severe symptoms
- No signs of organ strain

Recommendation: See a doctor for confirmation over multiple readings and to discuss lifestyle changes or medication.

---

## 🔴 High — Possible Emergency (Hypertensive Crisis)

Any of the following:

- Severe headache with confusion
- Chest pain
- Severe shortness of breath
- Vision changes with very high readings
- Blood pressure reading in a severely elevated range with any of the above symptoms

Recommendation: **Seek emergency medical care immediately.** A hypertensive crisis can cause organ damage rapidly.

---

# Home Care

- Reduce salt intake significantly.
- Maintain a healthy weight and stay physically active.
- Limit alcohol; avoid smoking.
- Manage stress through regular relaxation practices.
- Take prescribed medication consistently if already diagnosed — never stop abruptly without medical advice.
- Monitor blood pressure regularly if you have a home monitor.

---

# Prevention

- Maintain a diet low in salt and rich in fruits and vegetables.
- Stay physically active most days of the week.
- Maintain a healthy body weight.
- Limit alcohol and avoid tobacco.
- Get blood pressure checked regularly, especially after age 40.

---

# When to Consult a Doctor

Consult a doctor if:

- You've had multiple elevated blood pressure readings
- You have a family history of hypertension and haven't been screened recently
- You're on blood pressure medication and experience new symptoms

---

# Emergency Warning Signs

Seek emergency care immediately if:

- Severe headache with confusion
- Chest pain or severe shortness of breath
- Sudden vision changes
- Very high blood pressure reading with any of the above

---

# Recommended Specialist

Cardiologist (emergency care for a hypertensive crisis)
