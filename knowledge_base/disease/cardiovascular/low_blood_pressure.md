# Low Blood Pressure

## Overview

Low blood pressure (hypotension) means the force of blood against artery walls is lower than normal. For many people, especially the young and fit, this is completely normal and causes no problems. But when it drops enough to reduce blood flow to the brain, it can cause dizziness, fainting, and falls — particularly on standing up quickly.

**Note:** This condition is assessed from reported symptoms only — blood pressure cannot be assessed from a photo, so the AI does not request an image for this body-part category.

---

# Common Symptoms

- Dizziness or lightheadedness, especially on standing up quickly
- Fainting or near-fainting
- Blurred vision
- Fatigue
- Difficulty concentrating
- Cold, clammy, pale skin in more severe cases

---

# Causes

### Orthostatic (positional) hypotension

Blood pressure drops when standing up quickly, due to gravity pooling blood in the legs faster than the body compensates.

### Other causes

Dehydration, blood loss, certain medications, prolonged bed rest, heart problems, or severe infection.

---

# Risk Factors

- Dehydration
- Certain blood pressure or heart medications
- Prolonged standing or bed rest
- Older age
- Pregnancy
- Underlying heart or endocrine conditions

---

# Severity Classification

## 🟢 Low

- Occasional mild dizziness on standing
- Resolves quickly
- No fainting

Recommendation: Stay hydrated, rise slowly from sitting or lying positions; monitor.

---

## 🟡 Moderate

- Frequent dizziness affecting daily activities
- Near-fainting episodes
- Persistent fatigue

Recommendation: Consult a doctor to identify the underlying cause.

---

## 🔴 High — Possible Emergency

Any of the following:

- Actual fainting (loss of consciousness)
- Blood pressure drop with chest pain, severe shortness of breath, or confusion
- Signs of shock: cold clammy skin, rapid weak pulse, extreme paleness
- Suspected significant blood loss

Recommendation: **Seek emergency medical care immediately.**

---

# Home Care

- Rise slowly from sitting or lying down.
- Stay well hydrated.
- Avoid standing still for long periods.
- Wear compression stockings if advised by a doctor.
- Eat small, frequent meals rather than large ones if symptoms occur after eating.

---

# Prevention

- Stay well hydrated, especially in hot weather or during illness.
- Rise gradually from sitting or lying positions.
- Avoid prolonged standing in one place.
- Review medications with a doctor if symptoms started after a new prescription.

---

# When to Consult a Doctor

Consult a doctor if:

- Dizziness on standing happens frequently
- You've had a fainting episode
- Symptoms are affecting daily life

---

# Emergency Warning Signs

Seek emergency care immediately if:

- You actually faint or lose consciousness
- Low blood pressure comes with chest pain, confusion, or severe shortness of breath
- Signs of shock appear (cold clammy skin, rapid weak pulse)
- Significant blood loss is suspected
