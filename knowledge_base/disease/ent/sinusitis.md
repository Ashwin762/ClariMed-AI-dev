# Sinusitis

## Overview

Sinusitis is inflammation of the sinus cavities, causing facial pressure, nasal congestion, and thick discharge. Most cases are viral, follow a cold, and resolve within 7-10 days without antibiotics.

**Note:** Assessed primarily from reported symptoms — the sinuses are internal cavities not visible in a standard photo.

---

# Common Symptoms

- Facial pain or pressure (around the cheeks, eyes, or forehead)
- Nasal congestion or blockage
- Thick yellow or green nasal discharge
- Reduced sense of smell
- Headache
- Post-nasal drip and cough

---

# Causes

### Viral infection

Most commonly follows a common cold, causing sinus lining inflammation.

### Bacterial infection

Less common; suspected when symptoms persist beyond 10 days or worsen after initial improvement.

### Allergies or structural issues

Allergic rhinitis, nasal polyps, or a deviated septum can predispose to sinusitis.

---

# Risk Factors

- Recent cold or upper respiratory infection
- Allergies
- Nasal polyps or deviated septum
- Smoking
- Weakened immune system

---

# Severity Classification

## 🟢 Low

- Mild congestion and facial pressure
- No fever
- Improving within a week

Recommendation: Home care; typically resolves on its own.

---

## 🟡 Moderate

- Symptoms persisting beyond 10 days
- Symptoms worsening after initially improving
- Fever

Recommendation: Consult a doctor or ENT specialist — bacterial sinusitis may need antibiotics.

---

## 🔴 High

Any of the following:

- Severe headache with high fever
- Swelling or redness around the eyes
- Vision changes
- Confusion or stiff neck

Recommendation: Seek prompt medical care — may indicate a spreading infection.

---

# Home Care

- Saline nasal rinses to clear congestion.
- Steam inhalation to ease sinus pressure.
- Stay well hydrated.
- Apply a warm compress over the face.
- Rest.

---

# Prevention

- Wash hands frequently to reduce colds.
- Manage allergies well.
- Avoid cigarette smoke.
- Use a humidifier in dry environments.

---

# When to Consult a Doctor

Consult a doctor or ENT specialist if:

- Symptoms persist beyond 10 days
- Symptoms worsen after an initial improvement
- Fever accompanies the sinus symptoms
- You have frequent recurrent sinus infections

---

# Emergency Warning Signs

Seek prompt medical care if:

- Swelling or redness around the eyes
- Vision changes or double vision
- Severe headache with stiff neck
- Confusion or high fever
