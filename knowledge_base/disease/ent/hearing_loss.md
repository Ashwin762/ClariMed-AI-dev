# Hearing Loss

## Overview

Hearing loss is a partial or complete inability to hear sounds in one or
both ears. It may develop gradually due to aging or suddenly because of
infection, injury, excessive noise exposure, or underlying medical
conditions. Early diagnosis and treatment can help prevent further
hearing deterioration.

**Note:** This condition is assessed primarily from reported
symptoms. Since hearing loss cannot be reliably identified through image
analysis, the AI does not request an image for this body-part category.

---

# Common Symptoms

- Difficulty hearing conversations

- Frequently asking others to repeat themselves

- Muffled hearing

- Ringing in the ears (tinnitus)

- Difficulty hearing on the phone

- Trouble understanding speech in noisy environments

---

# Causes

Hearing loss can result from problems affecting the outer, middle, or
inner ear.

### Common causes

Age-related hearing loss, prolonged exposure to loud noise, ear
infections, earwax buildup, injury, certain medications, and inherited
conditions.

---

# Risk Factors

- Older age

- Frequent exposure to loud noise

- Family history of hearing loss

- Recurrent ear infections

- Diabetes

- Smoking

---

# Severity Classification

## 🟢 Low

- Mild difficulty hearing soft sounds

- Occasional need for repetition

Recommendation: Schedule a hearing evaluation with an ENT specialist.

---

## 🟡 Moderate

- Difficulty understanding conversations

- Hearing problems affecting daily activities

Recommendation: Consult an ENT specialist for complete assessment.

---

## 🔴 High

Any of the following:

- Sudden hearing loss

- Hearing loss after head injury

- Hearing loss with severe dizziness

- Hearing loss with ear bleeding

Recommendation: Seek immediate medical evaluation.

---

# Home Care

- Avoid loud noise exposure.

- Use hearing protection in noisy environments.

- Maintain good ear hygiene.

- Avoid inserting objects into the ear.

- Follow medical advice regarding hearing care.

---

# Prevention

- Wear ear protection around loud machinery.

- Keep headphone volume at safe levels.

- Treat ear infections promptly.

- Attend regular hearing check-ups if at risk.

---

# When to Consult a Doctor

Consult an ENT specialist if:

- Hearing gradually worsens.

- Ringing in the ears persists.

- Difficulty hearing affects daily life.

- Sudden hearing changes occur.

---

# Emergency Warning Signs

Seek immediate medical care if you experience:

- Sudden complete hearing loss

- Ear bleeding following injury

- Hearing loss with severe dizziness

- Sudden hearing loss in one ear
