# Vertigo

## Overview

Vertigo is a sensation of spinning or movement when there is none, usually caused by an inner ear problem affecting balance. The most common cause, BPPV, involves tiny calcium crystals in the inner ear becoming displaced, and often responds well to specific repositioning maneuvers.

---

# Common Symptoms

- A spinning or whirling sensation, either of yourself or your surroundings
- Triggered or worsened by specific head movements or position changes
- Nausea or vomiting
- Loss of balance
- Brief episodes, typically lasting seconds to a few minutes (in BPPV)
- Occasional ringing in the ears

---

# Causes

### Inner ear crystal displacement (BPPV)

Tiny calcium crystals in the inner ear become dislodged and send false signals about head position.

### Other causes

Inner ear infections (labyrinthitis), Meniere's disease, and less commonly, issues affecting the brain's balance centers.

---

# Risk Factors

- Age (more common over 50)
- Head injury
- Prolonged bed rest
- Inner ear infections
- Migraine history

---

# Severity Classification

## 🟢 Low

- Brief, mild episodes
- Triggered by specific movements
- No hearing changes or neurological symptoms

Recommendation: Consult an ENT specialist — BPPV often resolves with specific repositioning exercises.

---

## 🟡 Moderate

- Frequent or prolonged episodes
- Significant nausea
- Affecting daily activities or safety (e.g., driving)

Recommendation: Consult an ENT specialist for evaluation and treatment.

---

## 🔴 High — Possible Emergency

Any of the following:

- Sudden severe headache with dizziness
- Double vision, slurred speech, or facial drooping
- Weakness or numbness on one side of the body
- Difficulty walking or severe loss of coordination
- Dizziness following a head injury

Recommendation: **Seek emergency medical care immediately.** These combinations can indicate a stroke or other serious neurological condition, not simple inner-ear vertigo.

---

# Home Care

- Move slowly when changing position (sitting up, standing).
- Avoid sudden head movements during episodes.
- Sit or lie down immediately when dizziness occurs to avoid falls.
- Specific repositioning exercises (like the Epley maneuver) can help BPPV, ideally taught by a specialist first.

---

# Prevention

- Avoid rapid head position changes.
- Manage underlying conditions like migraines.
- Stay well hydrated.
- Follow up on inner ear infections promptly.

---

# When to Consult a Doctor

Consult an ENT specialist if:

- Vertigo episodes are frequent or recurring
- Symptoms affect daily activities or safety
- Hearing changes or ringing accompany the dizziness

---

# Emergency Warning Signs

Seek emergency care immediately if vertigo is accompanied by:

- Sudden severe headache
- Double vision, slurred speech, or facial drooping
- Weakness or numbness on one side of the body
- Severe loss of coordination or difficulty walking
- Onset following a head injury

---

# Recommended Specialist

ENT Specialist (emergency care if neurological warning signs present)
