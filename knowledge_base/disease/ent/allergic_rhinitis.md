# Allergic Rhinitis

## Overview

Allergic rhinitis is an allergic reaction affecting the nasal passages, causing sneezing, a runny or blocked nose, and itching. Unlike a cold, it isn't caused by infection and doesn't produce fever. It may be seasonal (pollen) or year-round (dust mites, pet dander).

**Note:** Assessed primarily from reported symptoms — there is no reliable visual sign in a standard photo.

---

# Common Symptoms

- Frequent sneezing
- Clear, runny nose
- Nasal congestion or blockage
- Itchy nose, throat, or roof of the mouth
- Watery, itchy eyes
- No fever (distinguishing it from a cold)

---

# Causes

### Allergen exposure

The immune system overreacts to harmless substances — pollen, dust mites, mold spores, pet dander — releasing histamine.

---

# Risk Factors

- Family history of allergies, asthma, or eczema
- Existing asthma or eczema
- Frequent exposure to dust, pollen, or pets
- Air pollution or smoke exposure

---

# Severity Classification

## 🟢 Low

- Occasional sneezing and mild congestion
- Doesn't affect sleep or daily activity

Recommendation: Avoid triggers; over-the-counter antihistamines may help.

---

## 🟡 Moderate

- Persistent symptoms affecting sleep, work, or study
- Symptoms lasting most days
- Not controlled by basic measures

Recommendation: Consult a doctor or ENT specialist for a management plan.

---

# Home Care

- Identify and avoid known triggers.
- Saline nasal rinses to clear allergens.
- Keep windows closed during high-pollen periods.
- Wash bedding regularly in hot water (for dust mite allergy).
- Shower after outdoor exposure during pollen season.

---

# Prevention

- Use dust-mite-proof bedding covers.
- Keep indoor humidity low to discourage mold.
- Use air filters where practical.
- Limit outdoor activity during peak pollen times.

---

# When to Consult a Doctor

Consult a doctor or ENT specialist if:

- Symptoms significantly disrupt sleep or daily life
- Over-the-counter measures aren't providing relief
- Symptoms occur year-round
- You also have asthma symptoms

---

# Emergency Warning Signs

Allergic rhinitis alone is not an emergency. However, seek urgent care if:

- Difficulty breathing or wheezing develops
- Facial or throat swelling occurs (possible severe allergic reaction)
