# Foreign Body in Ear or Nose

## Overview

A foreign body in the ear or nose occurs when an object becomes lodged
inside the ear canal or nasal passage. This is especially common in
young children who may insert small objects such as beads, toy parts,
food particles, or cotton. Prompt medical attention is important to
prevent infection, injury, or breathing complications.

**Note:** This condition is assessed using both reported symptoms
and visible findings when possible. Image analysis may help identify
visible foreign objects but cannot replace a clinical examination.

---

# Common Symptoms

- Ear pain or discomfort

- Nose pain or irritation

- Feeling that something is stuck

- Hearing difficulty (if in the ear)

- One-sided nasal blockage

- Foul-smelling nasal discharge

- Ear discharge or bleeding

- Persistent crying or irritability in children

---

# Causes

A foreign body becomes lodged when an object is accidentally or
intentionally inserted into the ear or nose.

### Common causes

Children inserting small objects during play, accidental insertion of
cotton tips, insects entering the ear, and accidental placement of small
items.

---

# Risk Factors

- Children under 6 years of age

- Playing with small objects

- Developmental or behavioral disorders

- Lack of adult supervision

- Accidental insertion during ear cleaning

---

# Severity Classification

## 🟢 Low

- Small object visible without pain

- No bleeding or discharge

Recommendation: Arrange prompt evaluation by an ENT specialist. Do not
attempt repeated removal at home.

---

## 🟡 Moderate

- Ear or nose pain

- Mild bleeding

- Hearing difficulty

- Persistent nasal blockage

Recommendation: Consult an ENT specialist as soon as possible for safe
removal.

---

## 🔴 High

Any of the following:

- Button battery lodged in the ear or nose

- Difficulty breathing

- Severe bleeding

- Severe pain

- Object pushed deep into the ear or nose

- Suspected aspiration into the airway

Recommendation: Seek immediate emergency medical care. Do not attempt to
remove the object yourself.

---

# Home Care

- Do not insert fingers, cotton swabs, or tools into the ear or nose.

- Keep the child calm.

- Avoid pushing the object further inside.

- Seek medical evaluation promptly.

- Follow the healthcare provider\'s instructions after removal.

---

# Prevention

- Keep small objects away from young children.

- Supervise children during play.

- Teach children not to place objects in their ears or nose.

- Store button batteries safely out of children\'s reach.

---

# When to Consult a Doctor

Consult an ENT specialist if:

- An object is suspected in the ear or nose.

- Pain or discomfort develops.

- There is discharge or bleeding.

- Hearing becomes reduced.

- The object cannot be safely removed.

---

# Emergency Warning Signs

Seek immediate emergency medical care if you experience:

- Difficulty breathing

- A button battery lodged in the ear or nose

- Severe bleeding

- Severe pain

- Swelling around the face

- Loss of consciousness
