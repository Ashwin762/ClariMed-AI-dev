# Tonsillitis

## Overview

Tonsillitis is inflammation of the tonsils, usually caused by a viral or bacterial infection. It causes a sore throat, difficulty swallowing, and often fever. Most viral cases resolve on their own; bacterial (strep) cases may need antibiotics.

---

# Common Symptoms

- Sore throat, often severe
- Red, swollen tonsils
- White or yellow patches on the tonsils
- Difficulty or pain when swallowing
- Fever
- Tender, swollen glands in the neck
- Bad breath

---

# Causes

### Viral infection

Most common cause — often the same viruses that cause colds.

### Bacterial infection

*Streptococcus pyogenes* (strep throat) accounts for a significant minority of cases, especially in children.

---

# Risk Factors

- Young age (school-aged children)
- Close contact with infected individuals
- Frequent exposure in crowded settings

# Severity Classification

## 🟢 Low

- Mild sore throat
- No fever
- Swallowing largely unaffected

Recommendation: Home care; likely viral and self-limiting.

---

## 🟡 Moderate

- Significant sore throat with fever
- White patches on the tonsils
- Painful swallowing

Recommendation: See a doctor — strep testing may be needed.

---

## 🔴 High

Any of the following:

- Difficulty breathing
- Inability to swallow fluids
- Drooling (unable to swallow saliva)
- Muffled voice with severe one-sided pain

Recommendation: Seek emergency care — may indicate an abscess or airway compromise.

---

# Home Care

- Rest and drink plenty of fluids.
- Gargle with warm salt water.
- Use throat lozenges or warm liquids for comfort.
- Pain relief as advised by a doctor or pharmacist.

---

# Prevention

- Wash hands frequently.
- Avoid sharing utensils or drinks.
- Avoid close contact with infected individuals.
- Replace toothbrush after a bacterial infection clears.

---

# When to Consult a Doctor

Consult a doctor or ENT specialist if:

- Sore throat persists beyond 3-4 days
- Fever accompanies the sore throat
- White patches appear on the tonsils
- You have recurrent episodes of tonsillitis

---

# Emergency Warning Signs

Seek emergency care immediately if:

- Difficulty breathing
- Inability to swallow liquids or saliva
- Drooling
- Severe one-sided throat pain with a muffled voice
