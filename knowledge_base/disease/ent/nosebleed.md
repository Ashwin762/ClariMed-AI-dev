# Nosebleed

## Overview

A nosebleed occurs when blood vessels in the nasal lining rupture, most commonly in the front part of the nose (anterior). Most nosebleeds are minor and stop with simple first aid, though frequent or heavy nosebleeds warrant evaluation.

**Note:** This condition is assessed from reported symptoms only — there is no relevant photo, so the AI does not request an image for this body-part category.

---

# Common Symptoms

- Bleeding from one or both nostrils
- Sensation of liquid at the back of the throat if bleeding is from the back of the nose
- Usually resolves within 10-20 minutes with proper first aid

---

# Causes

### Local irritation

Dry air, nose picking, forceful blowing, or minor trauma are the most common causes.

### Other contributors

Colds/sinus infections, allergies, certain medications (blood thinners), high blood pressure, and less commonly, bleeding disorders.

---

# Risk Factors

- Dry climate or indoor heating
- Frequent nose picking
- Allergies or frequent colds
- Blood-thinning medications
- High blood pressure

---

# Severity Classification

## 🟢 Low

- Occasional, brief nosebleed
- Stops with simple first aid
- No other symptoms

Recommendation: Home care; monitor for recurrence.

---

## 🟡 Moderate

- Frequent nosebleeds
- Takes longer than 20 minutes to stop with proper first aid
- Occurring alongside easy bruising elsewhere

Recommendation: Consult an ENT specialist to identify and address the underlying cause.

---

## 🔴 High — Possible Emergency

Any of the following:

- Bleeding that doesn't stop after 20-30 minutes of proper first aid
- Heavy blood loss
- Nosebleed following significant facial trauma
- Difficulty breathing due to blood

Recommendation: **Seek emergency medical care immediately.**

---

# Home Care

- Sit upright and lean slightly forward (not backward — this can cause swallowing blood).
- Pinch the soft part of the nose firmly for 10-15 minutes continuously.
- Apply a cold compress to the bridge of the nose.
- Avoid blowing the nose or bending over for several hours after bleeding stops.

---

# Prevention

- Use a humidifier in dry environments.
- Avoid vigorous nose picking or blowing.
- Keep nasal passages moist with saline spray in dry conditions.
- Manage underlying allergies or sinus conditions.

---

# When to Consult a Doctor

Consult an ENT specialist if:

- Nosebleeds occur frequently
- Bleeding is heavy or difficult to control
- You're on blood-thinning medication and experience nosebleeds
- Nosebleeds occur alongside easy bruising or other bleeding

---

# Emergency Warning Signs

Seek emergency care immediately if:

- Bleeding doesn't stop after 20-30 minutes of proper first aid
- Heavy blood loss or signs of significant blood loss (dizziness, paleness)
- Nosebleed follows significant facial trauma
- Difficulty breathing
