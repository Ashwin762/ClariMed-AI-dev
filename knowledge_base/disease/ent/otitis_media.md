# Otitis Media

## Overview

Otitis media is an infection or inflammation of the middle ear, most common in children. It often follows a cold or upper respiratory infection and usually resolves within a few days, though some cases need antibiotics.

**Note:** Assessed primarily from reported symptoms — the middle ear is behind the eardrum and not visible in a standard photo.

---

# Common Symptoms

- Ear pain, often worse when lying down
- Feeling of fullness or pressure in the ear
- Reduced hearing in the affected ear
- Fever
- Fluid or pus draining from the ear (if the eardrum ruptures)
- Irritability in young children

---

# Causes

### Bacterial or viral infection

Infection spreads from the nose/throat into the middle ear via the eustachian tube, often after a cold.

### Eustachian tube dysfunction

Blocked or poorly draining tubes allow fluid to accumulate and become infected.

---

# Risk Factors

- Young age (children under 5)
- Recent cold or respiratory infection
- Allergies
- Exposure to smoke
- Bottle-feeding while lying flat (infants)

---

# Severity Classification

## 🟢 Low

- Mild ear discomfort
- No fever
- Hearing largely normal

Recommendation: Monitor; often resolves on its own within a few days.

---

## 🟡 Moderate

- Persistent ear pain beyond 2-3 days
- Fever
- Noticeable hearing reduction

Recommendation: See a doctor or ENT specialist — antibiotics may be needed.

---

## 🔴 High

Any of the following:

- Severe ear pain with high fever
- Swelling or redness behind the ear
- Facial weakness
- Severe headache or stiff neck

Recommendation: Seek prompt medical care — may indicate a spreading infection.

---

# Home Care

- Warm compress against the ear for comfort.
- Rest and adequate fluids.
- Pain relief as advised by a doctor or pharmacist.
- Keep the ear dry; do not insert anything into the ear canal.

---

# Prevention

- Avoid exposure to cigarette smoke.
- Keep up to date with routine vaccinations.
- Practice good hand hygiene to reduce colds.
- Breastfeed infants where possible (offers some protection).

---

# When to Consult a Doctor

Consult a doctor or ENT specialist if:

- Ear pain persists beyond 2-3 days
- Fever accompanies the ear pain
- Fluid or pus drains from the ear
- Hearing is noticeably reduced

---

# Emergency Warning Signs

Seek prompt medical care if:

- Severe pain with high fever
- Swelling, redness, or tenderness behind the ear
- Facial drooping or weakness
- Stiff neck or severe headache

---
