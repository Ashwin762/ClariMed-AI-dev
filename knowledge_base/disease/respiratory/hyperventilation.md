# Hyperventilation / Panic-Related Breathing

## Overview

Hyperventilation is rapid or unusually deep breathing that reduces the
level of carbon dioxide in the blood. It commonly occurs during anxiety
or panic attacks but may also be associated with certain medical
conditions. While many episodes improve with reassurance, sudden
breathing difficulty should always be medically evaluated to rule out
serious causes.

**Note:** This condition is assessed primarily from reported
symptoms. Image analysis cannot reliably diagnose hyperventilation, so
the AI relies mainly on symptom-based assessment.

---

# Common Symptoms

- Rapid breathing

- Shortness of breath

- Chest tightness

- Dizziness or light-headedness

- Tingling or numbness in the hands, feet, or around the mouth

- Feeling anxious or panicked

- Fast heartbeat

- Difficulty taking a deep breath

---

# Causes

Hyperventilation occurs when breathing becomes faster or deeper than the
body\'s normal requirements.

### Common causes

Anxiety, panic attacks, emotional stress, intense fear, pain, strenuous
exercise, asthma, lung disease, and certain medical emergencies.

---

# Risk Factors

- Anxiety disorders

- Panic disorder

- High stress levels

- Previous panic attacks

- Asthma or other respiratory diseases

- Emotional trauma

---

# Severity Classification

## 🟢 Low

- Mild rapid breathing

- Symptoms improve with rest and reassurance

Recommendation: Monitor symptoms and consult a healthcare professional
if episodes recur.

---

## 🟡 Moderate

- Frequent episodes

- Dizziness

- Tingling sensations

- Difficulty performing normal activities

Recommendation: Consult a pulmonologist or healthcare provider for
evaluation.

---

## 🔴 High

Any of the following:

- Severe difficulty breathing

- Chest pain

- Loss of consciousness

- Blue lips or fingertips

- Symptoms that do not improve after a few minutes

- Hyperventilation following a serious injury

Recommendation: Seek immediate emergency medical care, as these symptoms
may indicate a life-threatening condition.

---

# Home Care

- Sit in a comfortable position.

- Try to remain calm.

- Practice slow, controlled breathing.

- Avoid situations that increase stress when possible.

- Follow your healthcare provider\'s recommendations if episodes are
recurrent.

---

# Prevention

- Learn stress management techniques.

- Practice relaxation and breathing exercises.

- Maintain regular physical activity.

- Get adequate sleep.

- Seek help for anxiety or panic disorders when needed.

---

# When to Consult a Doctor

Consult a healthcare professional if:

- Episodes occur frequently.

- Breathing difficulty worsens.

- Symptoms interfere with daily activities.

- Hyperventilation occurs without an obvious trigger.

---

# Emergency Warning Signs

Seek immediate medical care if you experience:

- Severe chest pain

- Difficulty breathing that continues to worsen

- Blue lips or fingertips

- Fainting or loss of consciousness

- Confusion

- Hyperventilation after a serious injury
