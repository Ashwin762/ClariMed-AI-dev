# Bronchitis

## Overview

Bronchitis is inflammation of the bronchial tubes that carry air to the lungs, causing a persistent cough often with mucus. Acute bronchitis usually follows a cold and resolves within a few weeks; chronic bronchitis is a long-term condition often linked to smoking.

# Common Symptoms

- Persistent cough, often producing mucus
- Chest congestion or discomfort
- Fatigue
- Mild shortness of breath
- Wheezing
- Low-grade fever (in acute cases)

---

# Causes

### Viral infection

Most acute bronchitis follows a viral respiratory infection.

### Irritant exposure

Smoking, air pollution, dust, or chemical fumes — the main drivers of chronic bronchitis.

---

# Risk Factors

- Smoking or secondhand smoke exposure
- Recent cold or respiratory infection
- Air pollution or occupational dust exposure
- Weakened immune system
- Existing asthma or COPD

---

# Severity Classification

## 🟢 Low

- Mild cough with some mucus
- No fever or breathlessness
- Improving over 1-2 weeks

Recommendation: Home care with rest and fluids.

---

## 🟡 Moderate

- Cough persisting beyond 3 weeks
- Fever
- Increasing breathlessness

Recommendation: Consult a doctor or pulmonologist.

---

## 🔴 High

Any of the following:

- Significant difficulty breathing
- Coughing up blood
- High fever with severe chest pain
- Bluish lips or fingertips

Recommendation: Seek emergency medical care.

---

# Home Care

- Rest and drink plenty of fluids to thin mucus.
- Use a humidifier or inhale steam.
- Avoid smoke and other irritants.
- Honey (for adults and children over 1 year) may soothe cough.

---

# Prevention

- Avoid smoking and secondhand smoke.
- Wash hands frequently.
- Get an annual flu vaccination.
- Wear a mask around occupational dust or fumes.

---

# When to Consult a Doctor

Consult a doctor or pulmonologist if:

- Cough persists beyond 3 weeks
- You have a fever above 100.4°F (38°C)
- Breathlessness is worsening
- You cough up discolored or bloody mucus
- You have recurrent episodes of bronchitis

---

# Emergency Warning Signs

Seek emergency care immediately if:

- Severe difficulty breathing or shortness of breath at rest
- Coughing up blood
- Bluish tint to lips or fingertips
- Chest pain with high fever
- Confusion
