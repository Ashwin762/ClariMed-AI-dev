# Tuberculosis (TB)

## Overview

Tuberculosis (TB) is a contagious bacterial infection caused by
*Mycobacterium tuberculosis*, primarily affecting the lungs. It
spreads through the air when an infected person coughs, sneezes, or
speaks. Early diagnosis and treatment are essential to prevent
complications and reduce transmission.

**Note:** This condition is assessed primarily from reported
symptoms. Image analysis alone cannot diagnose tuberculosis.

---

# Common Symptoms

- Persistent cough lasting more than two weeks

- Coughing up blood

- Chest pain

- Fever

- Night sweats

- Weight loss

- Fatigue

- Loss of appetite

---

# Causes

Tuberculosis is caused by infection with *Mycobacterium tuberculosis*
bacteria.

### Common causes

Exposure to airborne bacteria from a person with active tuberculosis.

---

# Risk Factors

- Close contact with a person who has active TB

- Weakened immune system

- Diabetes

- Malnutrition

- Smoking

- Living in crowded conditions

---

# Severity Classification

## 🟢 Low

- Mild cough

- No breathing difficulty

Recommendation: Seek medical evaluation and TB testing.

---

## 🟡 Moderate

- Persistent cough

- Fever

- Weight loss

- Night sweats

Recommendation: Consult a pulmonologist immediately for testing and
treatment.

---

## 🔴 High

Any of the following:

- Coughing up significant amounts of blood

- Severe difficulty breathing

- Chest pain with worsening symptoms

- Confusion

- High fever with severe weakness

Recommendation: Seek immediate emergency medical care.

---

# Home Care

- Cover your mouth when coughing or sneezing.

- Wear a mask until advised otherwise.

- Ensure good ventilation at home.

- Get adequate nutrition and rest.

- Follow the complete treatment plan prescribed by your healthcare
provider.

---

# Prevention

- Cover coughs and sneezes.

- Ensure proper ventilation.

- Avoid close contact with people who have untreated active TB.

- Attend regular health screenings if at risk.

---

# When to Consult a Doctor

Consult a pulmonologist if:

- A cough lasts longer than two weeks.

- You cough up blood.

- You have unexplained weight loss.

- Night sweats and fever persist.

---

# Emergency Warning Signs

Seek immediate medical care if you experience:

- Heavy coughing of blood

- Severe difficulty breathing

- Chest pain

- Confusion

- Loss of consciousness
