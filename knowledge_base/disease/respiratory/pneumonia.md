# Pneumonia

## Overview

Pneumonia is an infection that inflames the air sacs in one or both lungs, which may fill with fluid. It causes cough, fever, chills, and difficulty breathing, and ranges from mild to life-threatening — particularly in infants, older adults, and people with weakened immunity.

# Common Symptoms

- Cough, often producing phlegm
- Fever, sweating, and shaking chills
- Shortness of breath
- Chest pain that worsens when breathing deeply or coughing
- Fatigue and weakness
- Confusion (especially in older adults)

---

# Causes

### Bacterial infection

*Streptococcus pneumoniae* is the most common bacterial cause.

### Viral infection

Influenza and other respiratory viruses can cause viral pneumonia.

### Other

Fungal causes are less common, mainly in people with weakened immune systems.

---

# Risk Factors

- Age (very young or over 65)
- Chronic lung disease (asthma, COPD)
- Smoking
- Weakened immune system
- Recent respiratory infection
- Hospitalization or ventilator use

---

# Severity Classification

## 🟡 Moderate

- Cough with fever
- Manageable breathlessness
- Alert and able to eat/drink normally

Recommendation: See a doctor promptly — pneumonia typically needs medical treatment, not home care alone.

---

## 🔴 High — Emergency

Any of the following:

- Significant difficulty breathing
- High fever with confusion
- Bluish lips or fingertips
- Chest pain with severe breathlessness
- Inability to keep fluids down

Recommendation: **Seek emergency medical care immediately.**

---

# Home Care

Pneumonia requires medical evaluation and often prescription treatment — it should not be managed with home care alone. While awaiting care: rest, stay hydrated, and monitor breathing and temperature closely.

---

# Prevention

- Get vaccinated (pneumococcal and annual influenza vaccines).
- Wash hands frequently.
- Avoid smoking.
- Manage chronic conditions well.
- Maintain good general health and nutrition.

---

# When to Consult a Doctor

Any suspected pneumonia warrants **prompt** medical evaluation — this is not a condition to wait out at home.

---

# Emergency Warning Signs

Seek emergency care immediately if:

- Severe difficulty breathing
- Confusion or reduced alertness
- Bluish tint to lips or fingertips
- High fever with severe chest pain
- Rapid breathing or heart rate

---

# Recommended Specialist

Pulmonologist (or emergency care for severe symptoms)
