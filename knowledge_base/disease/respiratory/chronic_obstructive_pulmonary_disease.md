# Chronic Obstructive Pulmonary Disease (COPD)

## Overview

Chronic Obstructive Pulmonary Disease (COPD) is a long-term lung disease
that causes airflow obstruction, making breathing difficult. It usually
develops gradually and is most commonly associated with long-term
smoking or exposure to harmful air pollutants. Although COPD cannot be
cured, early diagnosis and proper management can help slow its
progression and improve quality of life.

**Note:** This condition is assessed primarily from reported
symptoms. Image analysis alone cannot diagnose COPD, so the AI relies
mainly on symptom-based assessment.

---

# Common Symptoms

- Shortness of breath

- Chronic cough

- Wheezing

- Chest tightness

- Increased mucus production

- Frequent respiratory infections

- Fatigue

---

# Causes

COPD develops due to long-term damage to the lungs and airways.

### Common causes

Long-term cigarette smoking, secondhand smoke, air pollution,
occupational dust and chemical exposure, and genetic factors.

---

# Risk Factors

- Smoking

- Exposure to air pollution

- Occupational dust and chemicals

- Age over 40 years

- Family history of COPD

- History of asthma

---

# Severity Classification

## 🟢 Low

- Mild shortness of breath during physical activity

- Occasional cough

Recommendation: Consult a pulmonologist for evaluation and lifestyle
modifications.

---

## 🟡 Moderate

- Persistent cough

- Increased mucus production

- Difficulty performing daily activities

Recommendation: Seek medical evaluation for appropriate treatment and
lung function assessment.

---

## 🔴 High

Any of the following:

- Severe difficulty breathing

- Blue lips or fingertips

- Confusion

- Chest pain

- Inability to speak due to breathlessness

Recommendation: Seek immediate emergency medical care.

---

# Home Care

- Avoid smoking and secondhand smoke.

- Avoid exposure to dust and pollutants.

- Stay physically active as advised by your healthcare provider.

- Eat a balanced diet.

- Follow your healthcare provider\'s treatment plan.

---

# Prevention

- Do not smoke.

- Avoid exposure to harmful fumes.

- Get recommended vaccinations.

- Exercise regularly.

- Attend routine medical check-ups.

---

# When to Consult a Doctor

Consult a pulmonologist if:

- Chronic cough persists.

- Breathlessness worsens.

- Frequent chest infections occur.

- Daily activities become difficult.

---

# Emergency Warning Signs

Seek immediate medical care if:

- Severe breathlessness

- Blue lips or fingertips

- Chest pain

- Confusion

- Loss of consciousness
