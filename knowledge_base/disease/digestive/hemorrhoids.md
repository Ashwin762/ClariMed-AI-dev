# Hemorrhoids

## Overview

Hemorrhoids are swollen veins in the lower rectum or anus, common and often related to straining during bowel movements or prolonged sitting. Most cases are manageable with simple measures and are not serious, though bleeding should always be evaluated to rule out other causes.

**Note:** This condition is assessed from reported symptoms only — the AI does not request an image for this body-part category, out of respect for privacy around this specific area.

---

# Common Symptoms

- Rectal pain or discomfort, especially during bowel movements
- Itching around the anus
- Bright red blood on toilet paper or in the toilet bowl
- A swelling or lump near the anus
- A feeling of fullness or incomplete bowel emptying

---

# Causes

### Increased pressure on rectal veins

Straining during bowel movements, chronic constipation or diarrhea, prolonged sitting, pregnancy, or heavy lifting.

---

# Risk Factors

- Chronic constipation or straining
- Prolonged sitting (especially on the toilet)
- Pregnancy
- Low-fiber diet
- Obesity
- Aging

---

# Severity Classification

## 🟢 Low

- Mild discomfort or itching
- Minimal or no bleeding
- Manageable with home care

Recommendation: Dietary changes and home care.

---

## 🟡 Moderate

- Persistent bleeding
- Significant discomfort or a protruding lump
- Symptoms not improving with home care

Recommendation: Consult a doctor or gastroenterologist for evaluation and treatment options.

---

## 🔴 High

Any of the following:

- Heavy or persistent rectal bleeding
- Severe pain
- Signs of significant blood loss (dizziness, weakness)

Recommendation: Seek prompt medical care — bleeding should always be properly evaluated, as it can occasionally indicate other conditions.

---

# Home Care

- Increase dietary fiber (fruits, vegetables, whole grains) and fluids to soften stool.
- Avoid straining during bowel movements.
- Use warm sitz baths for comfort.
- Avoid prolonged sitting, especially on the toilet.
- Over-the-counter creams may help with symptoms as advised by a pharmacist.

---

# Prevention

- Maintain a high-fiber diet and adequate hydration.
- Exercise regularly to support healthy bowel function.
- Avoid straining and prolonged toilet sitting.
- Respond promptly to the urge to have a bowel movement.

---

# When to Consult a Doctor

Consult a doctor if:

- Bleeding occurs (any rectal bleeding should be evaluated, especially if new or persistent)
- Symptoms don't improve with home care after a week or two
- A lump or swelling doesn't resolve
- You notice a change in bowel habits alongside symptoms

---

# Emergency Warning Signs

Seek prompt medical care if:

- Heavy or persistent bleeding
- Severe pain
- Dizziness or weakness suggesting significant blood loss

---

# Recommended Specialist

Gastroenterologist (or General Physician for initial evaluation)
