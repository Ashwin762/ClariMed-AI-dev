# Peptic Ulcer

## Overview

A peptic ulcer is an open sore that develops in the lining of the
stomach or the upper part of the small intestine. It commonly causes
burning stomach pain and may worsen when the stomach is empty. If left
untreated, ulcers can lead to bleeding or perforation.

**Note:** This condition is assessed primarily from reported
symptoms. Image analysis cannot diagnose a peptic ulcer.

---

# Common Symptoms

- Burning pain in the upper abdomen

- Bloating

- Nausea

- Heartburn

- Feeling full quickly

- Loss of appetite

- Vomiting

- Black or tarry stools (in severe cases)

---

# Causes

Peptic ulcers occur when the protective lining of the stomach or
intestine is damaged.

### Common causes

Helicobacter pylori (H. pylori) infection, frequent use of non-steroidal
anti-inflammatory medicines (NSAIDs), smoking, alcohol use, and
excessive stomach acid.

---

# Risk Factors

- H. pylori infection

- Long-term NSAID use

- Smoking

- Alcohol consumption

- Older age

- Family history

---

# Severity Classification

## 🟢 Low

- Mild burning stomach pain

- Symptoms improve after eating

Recommendation: Consult a gastroenterologist for evaluation.

---

## 🟡 Moderate

- Frequent abdominal pain

- Nausea

- Reduced appetite

Recommendation: Seek medical evaluation for diagnosis and treatment.

---

## 🔴 High

Any of the following:

- Vomiting blood

- Black tarry stools

- Sudden severe abdominal pain

- Fainting or dizziness

Recommendation: Seek immediate emergency medical care.

---

# Home Care

- Avoid smoking and alcohol.

- Eat smaller, regular meals.

- Avoid foods that worsen symptoms.

- Follow your healthcare provider\'s recommendations.

- Avoid unnecessary use of pain medicines unless advised by a doctor.

---

# Prevention

- Maintain good hygiene.

- Avoid smoking.

- Limit alcohol intake.

- Use pain medicines responsibly.

- Seek treatment for H. pylori infection if diagnosed.

---

# When to Consult a Doctor

Consult a gastroenterologist if:

- Burning stomach pain persists.

- You develop nausea or vomiting.

- Symptoms interfere with eating.

- You notice black stools.

---

# Emergency Warning Signs

Seek immediate medical care if you experience:

- Vomiting blood

- Black stools

- Severe abdominal pain

- Fainting

- Difficulty breathing
