# Worm Infestation

## Overview

Worm infestation is an intestinal infection caused by parasitic worms
such as roundworms, hookworms, tapeworms, or pinworms. It is common in
areas with poor sanitation and may cause digestive problems, nutritional
deficiencies, and poor growth in children if left untreated.

**Note:** This condition is assessed primarily from reported
symptoms. Image analysis cannot confirm worm infestation.

---

# Common Symptoms

- Stomach pain

- Diarrhea

- Nausea

- Poor appetite

- Weight loss

- Itching around the anus (especially at night)

- Worms visible in stool

- Fatigue

---

# Causes

Worm infestation occurs when parasitic worms enter the body through
contaminated food, water, or soil.

### Common causes

Poor sanitation, contaminated food or water, walking barefoot on
contaminated soil, and inadequate hand hygiene.

---

# Risk Factors

- Poor sanitation

- Children

- Drinking contaminated water

- Eating contaminated food

- Walking barefoot

- Poor hand hygiene

---

# Severity Classification

## 🟢 Low

- Mild stomach discomfort

- Occasional itching

Recommendation: Consult a healthcare provider for evaluation.

---

## 🟡 Moderate

- Persistent abdominal pain

- Weight loss

- Poor appetite

- Frequent diarrhea

Recommendation: Seek medical evaluation for diagnosis and treatment.

---

## 🔴 High

Any of the following:

- Severe abdominal pain

- Persistent vomiting

- Signs of intestinal blockage

- Severe dehydration

Recommendation: Seek immediate medical care.

---

# Home Care

- Wash hands thoroughly before eating.

- Drink safe, clean water.

- Wash fruits and vegetables properly.

- Maintain good personal hygiene.

- Follow your healthcare provider\'s treatment plan.

---

# Prevention

- Practice good hand hygiene.

- Use clean drinking water.

- Wear footwear outdoors.

- Maintain proper sanitation.

- Wash food thoroughly before eating.

---

# When to Consult a Doctor

Consult a gastroenterologist if:

- You notice worms in your stool.

- Symptoms persist.

- Weight loss develops.

- Severe itching occurs.
