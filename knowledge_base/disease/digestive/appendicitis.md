# Appendicitis

## Overview

Appendicitis is an inflammation of the appendix, a small pouch attached
to the large intestine. It usually begins with pain around the belly
button that later shifts to the lower right side of the abdomen. Without
prompt treatment, the appendix may rupture, leading to a serious and
potentially life-threatening infection.

**Note:** This condition is assessed primarily from reported
symptoms. Although abdominal swelling may sometimes be visible, image
analysis alone cannot diagnose appendicitis.

---

# Common Symptoms

- Pain that begins near the belly button and moves to the lower right
abdomen

- Sudden severe abdominal pain

- Loss of appetite

- Nausea and vomiting

- Fever

- Abdominal swelling

- Pain that worsens with walking, coughing, or movement

---

# Causes

Appendicitis occurs when the appendix becomes blocked, leading to
bacterial infection and inflammation.

### Common causes

Blockage by stool, enlarged lymph tissue, infections, or, less commonly,
tumors.

---

# Risk Factors

- Age between 10 and 30 years

- Family history of appendicitis

- Gastrointestinal infections

- Low-fiber diet

---

# Severity Classification

## 🟢 Low

- Mild abdominal discomfort

- No fever

- Symptoms are not worsening

Recommendation: Seek prompt medical evaluation to rule out appendicitis.

---

## 🟡 Moderate

- Persistent pain in the lower right abdomen

- Nausea and reduced appetite

- Mild fever

Recommendation: Consult a gastroenterologist or visit a hospital as soon
as possible.

---

## 🔴 High

Any of the following:

- Severe lower right abdominal pain

- High fever

- Persistent vomiting

- Rigid abdomen

- Sudden worsening pain after temporary relief

- Fainting or confusion

Recommendation: Seek immediate emergency medical care. A ruptured
appendix is a life-threatening emergency.

---

# Home Care

- Do not eat or drink if appendicitis is strongly suspected.

- Avoid taking laxatives unless advised by a healthcare professional.

- Avoid applying heat to the abdomen.

- Seek immediate medical evaluation.

---

# Prevention

There is no guaranteed way to prevent appendicitis. Maintaining a
balanced diet rich in fruits, vegetables, and fiber may support overall
digestive health.

---

# When to Consult a Doctor

Consult a doctor immediately if:

- Abdominal pain moves to the lower right side.

- Pain becomes progressively worse.

- Nausea and fever occur together with abdominal pain.

- Walking or coughing worsens the pain.

---

# Emergency Warning Signs

Seek immediate emergency medical care if you experience:

- Severe lower right abdominal pain

- Persistent vomiting

- High fever

- Hard or swollen abdomen

- Sudden relief of pain followed by worsening illness

- Loss of consciousness
