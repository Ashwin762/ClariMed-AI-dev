# Gastritis

## Overview

Gastritis is inflammation of the stomach lining, which can occur suddenly (acute) or develop gradually over time (chronic). Common causes include infection, certain medications, and excessive alcohol use. Most cases respond well to treatment.

---

# Common Symptoms

- Burning or gnawing stomach pain, often in the upper abdomen
- Nausea, sometimes with vomiting
- Bloating
- Feeling full quickly or after eating little
- Loss of appetite
- Indigestion

---

# Causes

### H. pylori infection

A common bacterial infection of the stomach lining, a leading cause of chronic gastritis.

### Other causes

Frequent use of NSAIDs (like ibuprofen), excessive alcohol consumption, chronic stress, and autoimmune conditions.

---

# Risk Factors

- H. pylori infection
- Frequent NSAID use
- Heavy alcohol consumption
- Smoking
- High stress levels
- Older age

---

# Severity Classification

## 🟢 Low

- Mild, occasional stomach discomfort
- No vomiting or bleeding signs

Recommendation: Dietary adjustments and monitoring.

---

## 🟡 Moderate

- Persistent burning pain
- Nausea affecting appetite
- Symptoms lasting more than a few days

Recommendation: Consult a doctor or gastroenterologist for evaluation, possibly including H. pylori testing.

---

## 🔴 High

Any of the following:

- Vomiting blood or material resembling coffee grounds
- Black, tarry stools
- Severe abdominal pain
- Signs of significant blood loss (dizziness, paleness)

Recommendation: Seek emergency medical care — may indicate a bleeding ulcer.

---

# Home Care

- Eat smaller, more frequent meals.
- Avoid spicy, acidic, or fatty foods during flare-ups.
- Avoid alcohol and NSAIDs.
- Manage stress levels.
- Avoid smoking.

---

# Prevention

- Limit alcohol consumption.
- Avoid frequent NSAID use where possible; use alternatives as advised by a doctor.
- Manage stress.
- Practice good food hygiene to reduce H. pylori and other infection risk.

---

# When to Consult a Doctor

Consult a doctor or gastroenterologist if:

- Symptoms persist beyond a week
- Pain is significant or recurring
- Symptoms interfere with eating or daily life

---

# Emergency Warning Signs

Seek emergency care if:

- Vomiting blood or coffee-ground material
- Black, tarry stools
- Severe abdominal pain
- Signs of significant blood loss (dizziness, rapid heartbeat, paleness)
