# Acid Reflux

## Overview

Acid reflux occurs when stomach acid flows back into the esophagus, causing a burning sensation in the chest (heartburn). Occasional reflux is common; frequent or persistent reflux is called GERD and can damage the esophagus over time.

**Note:** This condition is assessed from reported symptoms only — there is no relevant photo, so the AI does not request an image for this body-part category.

---

# Common Symptoms

- Burning sensation in the chest (heartburn), often after eating
- Sour or bitter taste in the mouth
- Regurgitation of food or liquid
- Symptoms worse when lying down or bending over
- Chronic cough or hoarseness
- Feeling of a lump in the throat

---

# Causes

### Weak lower esophageal sphincter

The muscle that normally keeps stomach contents from rising back up relaxes inappropriately.

### Contributing factors

Large meals, lying down after eating, certain foods (spicy, fatty, citrus, caffeine, alcohol), obesity, pregnancy, and smoking.

---

# Risk Factors

- Obesity
- Pregnancy
- Smoking
- Hiatal hernia
- Eating large meals late at night
- Certain medications

---

# Severity Classification

## 🟢 Low

- Occasional heartburn after certain foods
- Relieved by antacids
- Doesn't affect sleep

Recommendation: Dietary and lifestyle adjustments.

---

## 🟡 Moderate

- Heartburn more than twice a week
- Symptoms disturbing sleep
- Over-the-counter antacids not providing lasting relief

Recommendation: Consult a doctor or gastroenterologist.

---

## 🔴 High

Any of the following:

- Difficulty or painful swallowing
- Unintentional weight loss
- Vomiting blood or black, tarry stools
- Severe chest pain (rule out cardiac causes urgently)

Recommendation: Seek prompt medical care.

---

# Home Care

- Eat smaller, more frequent meals.
- Avoid lying down within 3 hours of eating.
- Elevate the head of the bed.
- Identify and avoid trigger foods.
- Maintain a healthy weight.
- Avoid smoking and excessive alcohol.

---

# Prevention

- Avoid large meals, especially late at night.
- Limit trigger foods (spicy, fatty, citrus, caffeine, chocolate).
- Maintain a healthy weight.
- Avoid tight-fitting clothing around the abdomen.

---

# When to Consult a Doctor

Consult a doctor or gastroenterologist if:

- Heartburn occurs more than twice a week
- Symptoms persist despite over-the-counter treatment
- You have difficulty swallowing
- Symptoms disturb your sleep regularly

---

# Emergency Warning Signs

Seek emergency care if:

- Severe chest pain (especially with sweating, arm/jaw pain, or breathlessness — a heart attack must be ruled out)
- Vomiting blood or coffee-ground material
- Black, tarry stools
- Difficulty swallowing with food getting stuck
