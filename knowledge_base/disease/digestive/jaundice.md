# Jaundice / Liver Concern

## Overview

Jaundice is a condition in which the skin, the whites of the eyes, and
other tissues turn yellow due to an increased level of bilirubin in the
blood. It is a sign of an underlying liver, gallbladder, or blood
disorder rather than a disease itself. Prompt medical evaluation is
important to determine the cause and begin appropriate treatment.

**Note:** Visible yellow discoloration of the skin and eyes may
assist image analysis, but diagnosis requires medical evaluation and
laboratory testing.

---

# Common Symptoms

- Yellowing of the skin

- Yellowing of the whites of the eyes

- Dark-colored urine

- Pale or clay-colored stools

- Itchy skin

- Fatigue

- Nausea

- Loss of appetite

- Abdominal discomfort

---

# Causes

Jaundice occurs when bilirubin builds up in the bloodstream due to
problems with the liver, bile ducts, or increased breakdown of red blood
cells.

### Common causes

Hepatitis, liver disease, gallstones, bile duct blockage, excessive
alcohol consumption, certain medications, and blood disorders.

---

# Risk Factors

- Liver disease

- Viral hepatitis

- Heavy alcohol use

- Gallstones

- Family history of liver disorders

- Certain medications

---

# Severity Classification

## 🟢 Low

- Mild yellowing of the eyes

- No severe pain or fever

Recommendation: Consult a gastroenterologist for evaluation.

---

## 🟡 Moderate

- Yellow skin with fatigue

- Dark urine

- Persistent nausea

Recommendation: Seek prompt medical evaluation to identify the
underlying cause.

---

## 🔴 High

Any of the following:

- Severe abdominal pain

- High fever

- Confusion

- Persistent vomiting

- Rapid worsening of jaundice

- Bleeding or easy bruising

Recommendation: Seek immediate emergency medical care, as severe liver
disease can be life-threatening.

# Home Care

- Stay well hydrated.

- Avoid alcohol.

- Eat a balanced diet.

- Follow your healthcare provider\'s recommendations.

- Attend follow-up appointments as advised.

---

# Prevention

- Get vaccinated against hepatitis when recommended.

- Practice good hygiene.

- Avoid excessive alcohol consumption.

- Maintain a healthy diet.

- Avoid sharing needles or personal items that may transmit infections.

---

# When to Consult a Doctor

Consult a gastroenterologist if:

- Yellowing of the eyes or skin develops.

- Dark urine or pale stools appear.

- Fatigue and loss of appetite persist.

- Symptoms worsen over time.

---

# Emergency Warning Signs

Seek immediate medical care if you experience:

- Severe abdominal pain

- High fever

- Confusion or drowsiness

- Persistent vomiting

- Bleeding

- Rapid worsening of yellow discoloration
