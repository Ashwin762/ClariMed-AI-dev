# Constipation

## Overview

Constipation means infrequent bowel movements (typically fewer than three per week) or difficulty passing stools. It's very common, usually related to diet, hydration, or activity levels, and most cases respond well to simple changes.

**Note:** This condition is assessed from reported symptoms only — there is no relevant photo, so the AI does not request an image for this body-part category.

---

# Common Symptoms

- Fewer than three bowel movements per week
- Hard, dry, or lumpy stools
- Straining during bowel movements
- Feeling of incomplete emptying
- Abdominal discomfort or bloating
- Sensation of a blockage

---

# Causes

### Lifestyle factors

Low fiber intake, inadequate fluids, lack of physical activity, and ignoring the urge to go.

### Other causes

Certain medications (opioids, some antacids, iron supplements), pregnancy, hypothyroidism, and IBS.

---

# Risk Factors

- Low-fiber diet
- Inadequate fluid intake
- Sedentary lifestyle
- Older age
- Pregnancy
- Certain medications

---

# Severity Classification

## 🟢 Low

- Occasional difficulty passing stool
- Responds to increased fiber and fluids

Recommendation: Dietary and lifestyle adjustments.

---

## 🟡 Moderate

- Persistent constipation beyond 3 weeks
- Not responding to dietary changes
- Significant discomfort or straining

Recommendation: Consult a doctor or gastroenterologist.

---

## 🔴 High

Any of the following:

- Severe abdominal pain with inability to pass stool or gas
- Blood in stool
- Unexplained weight loss
- Vomiting with constipation

Recommendation: Seek prompt medical care — may indicate an obstruction.

---

# Home Care

- Gradually increase dietary fiber (fruits, vegetables, whole grains, legumes).
- Drink plenty of water throughout the day.
- Exercise regularly.
- Don't ignore the urge to have a bowel movement.
- Establish a regular toilet routine.

---

# Prevention

- Maintain a fiber-rich diet.
- Stay well hydrated.
- Stay physically active.
- Respond promptly to the urge to go.
- Review medications with your doctor if constipation is a side effect.

---

# When to Consult a Doctor

Consult a doctor or gastroenterologist if:

- Constipation persists beyond 3 weeks despite lifestyle changes
- You notice blood in the stool
- You have unexplained weight loss
- Constipation is a new symptom and you're over 50
- You experience persistent abdominal pain

---

# Emergency Warning Signs

Seek prompt medical care if:

- Severe abdominal pain with inability to pass stool or gas
- Vomiting alongside constipation
- Blood in the stool
- Abdominal swelling with severe pain
