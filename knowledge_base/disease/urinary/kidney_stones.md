# Kidney Stones

## Overview

Kidney stones are hard mineral deposits that form inside the kidneys.
Small stones may pass naturally through the urinary tract, while larger
stones can block urine flow and cause severe pain. Prompt medical
evaluation is important, especially if the stone causes infection or
obstruction.

**Note:** This condition is assessed primarily from reported
symptoms. Imaging tests are required to confirm the diagnosis.

---

# Common Symptoms

- Severe pain in the back or side

- Pain radiating to the lower abdomen or groin

- Blood in the urine

- Painful urination

- Frequent urge to urinate

- Nausea and vomiting

- Cloudy or foul-smelling urine

---

# Causes

Kidney stones develop when minerals in the urine become concentrated and
form crystals.

### Common causes

Dehydration, high salt intake, certain dietary factors, obesity, family
history, and certain medical conditions.

---

# Risk Factors

- Dehydration

- Family history of kidney stones

- High-sodium diet

- Obesity

- Recurrent urinary tract infections

- Certain metabolic disorders

---

# Severity Classification

## 🟢 Low

- Mild intermittent pain

- Small stone likely to pass naturally

Recommendation: Increase fluid intake and consult a urologist.

---

## 🟡 Moderate

- Persistent severe pain

- Blood in urine

- Difficulty passing urine

Recommendation: Seek prompt medical evaluation.

---

## 🔴 High

Any of the following:

- Severe uncontrollable pain

- High fever with chills

- Persistent vomiting

- Inability to pass urine

- Severe bleeding in urine

Recommendation: Seek immediate emergency medical care.

---

# Home Care

- Drink plenty of water unless advised otherwise.

- Stay physically active if comfortable.

- Strain urine if instructed by your healthcare provider.

- Follow your healthcare provider\'s recommendations.

---

# Prevention

- Drink adequate fluids daily.

- Limit excessive salt intake.

- Maintain a balanced diet.

- Maintain a healthy weight.

- Follow medical advice if you have recurrent stones.

---

# When to Consult a Doctor

Consult a urologist if:

- Severe side pain develops.

- Blood appears in the urine.

- Pain does not improve.

- Fever occurs.

---

# Emergency Warning Signs

Seek immediate medical care if you experience:

- Severe pain that cannot be controlled

- Fever with chills

- Persistent vomiting

- Inability to urinate

- Heavy blood in urine
