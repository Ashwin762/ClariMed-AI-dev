# Frequent Urination

## Overview

Frequent urination refers to the need to urinate more often than usual.
It may occur during the day, at night, or both and can result from
increased fluid intake, urinary tract infections, diabetes, overactive
bladder, or other medical conditions. Persistent symptoms should be
medically evaluated.

**Note:** This condition is assessed primarily from reported
symptoms. Image analysis cannot diagnose frequent urination.

---

# Common Symptoms

- Frequent urge to urinate

- Passing small amounts of urine

- Waking up multiple times at night to urinate

- Sudden urgency to urinate

- Mild discomfort while urinating (in some cases)

---

# Causes

Frequent urination occurs when the bladder is irritated or affected by
an underlying medical condition.

### Common causes

Urinary tract infection, diabetes, overactive bladder, pregnancy,
excessive fluid intake, certain medications, and enlarged prostate.

---

# Risk Factors

- Diabetes

- Pregnancy

- Older age

- Urinary tract infections

- Excessive caffeine intake

- Overactive bladder

---

# Severity Classification

## 🟢 Low

- Slight increase in urination

- No pain or fever

Recommendation: Monitor symptoms and maintain adequate hydration.

---

## 🟡 Moderate

- Frequent urination affecting daily activities

- Night-time urination disturbing sleep

Recommendation: Consult a urologist for evaluation.

---

## 🔴 High

Any of the following:

- Frequent urination with severe pain

- Fever

- Blood in urine

- Inability to control urination

- Severe lower abdominal pain

Recommendation: Seek immediate medical evaluation.

---

# Home Care

- Stay well hydrated.

- Reduce caffeine and alcohol intake if they worsen symptoms.

- Avoid delaying urination.

- Maintain good personal hygiene.

- Follow your healthcare provider\'s recommendations.

---

# Prevention

- Drink adequate amounts of water.

- Limit bladder irritants such as excessive caffeine.

- Practice good hygiene.

- Manage diabetes effectively.

- Seek treatment for urinary infections promptly.

---

# When to Consult a Doctor

Consult a urologist if:

- Frequent urination lasts several days.

- It interferes with sleep or daily activities.

- Pain or fever develops.

- Blood appears in the urine.
