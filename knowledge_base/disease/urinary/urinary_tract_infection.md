# Urinary Tract Infection (UTI)

## Overview

A urinary tract infection (UTI) is a bacterial infection affecting any
part of the urinary system, including the bladder, urethra, or kidneys.
It is more common in women but can affect anyone. Early treatment helps
prevent the infection from spreading to the kidneys.

**Note:** This condition is assessed primarily from reported
symptoms. Image analysis cannot diagnose a urinary tract infection.

---

# Common Symptoms

- Burning sensation during urination

- Frequent urge to urinate

- Passing small amounts of urine

- Cloudy urine

- Strong-smelling urine

- Lower abdominal or pelvic pain

- Blood in urine (occasionally)

- Mild fever

---

# Causes

UTIs occur when bacteria enter the urinary tract and multiply.

### Common causes

Poor hygiene, sexual activity, urinary obstruction, dehydration,
catheter use, and incomplete bladder emptying.

---

# Risk Factors

- Female gender

- Pregnancy

- Diabetes

- Kidney stones

- Poor hydration

- Urinary catheter use

---

# Severity Classification

## 🟢 Low

- Mild burning during urination

- Increased urinary frequency

Recommendation: Drink plenty of fluids and consult a urologist.

---

## 🟡 Moderate

- Persistent pain

- Fever

- Cloudy or foul-smelling urine

Recommendation: Seek medical evaluation promptly.

---

## 🔴 High

Any of the following:

- High fever

- Severe back or side pain

- Persistent vomiting

- Confusion

- Symptoms suggesting kidney infection

Recommendation: Seek immediate medical care.

---

# Home Care

- Drink plenty of water.

- Do not delay urination.

- Maintain good personal hygiene.

- Avoid bladder irritants if symptoms worsen.

- Follow your healthcare provider\'s treatment plan.

---

# Prevention

- Stay well hydrated.

- Practice good hygiene.

- Urinate after sexual activity.

- Avoid holding urine for long periods.

- Wear breathable cotton underwear.

---

# When to Consult a Doctor

Consult a urologist if:

- Symptoms last longer than 24 hours.

- Fever develops.

- Blood appears in the urine.

- UTIs occur repeatedly.
