# Blood in Urine (Hematuria)

## Overview

Hematuria is the presence of blood in the urine, which may appear pink,
red, or brown. It can be caused by urinary tract infections, kidney
stones, kidney disease, trauma, or more serious conditions such as
urinary tract cancers. Any episode of visible blood in the urine should
be evaluated by a healthcare professional.

**Note:** This condition is assessed primarily from reported
symptoms. Image analysis cannot confirm hematuria.

---

# Common Symptoms

- Pink, red, or brown urine

- Pain during urination

- Frequent urination

- Lower abdominal pain

- Back or side pain

- Blood clots in urine (sometimes)

---

# Causes

Hematuria occurs when blood enters the urinary tract.

### Common causes

Urinary tract infection, kidney stones, kidney disease, bladder
infection, enlarged prostate, urinary tract injury, and urinary tract
tumors.

---

# Risk Factors

- Kidney stones

- Urinary tract infections

- Smoking

- Older age

- Family history of kidney disease

- Vigorous exercise

---

# Severity Classification

## 🟢 Low

- Small amount of blood in urine

- No severe pain

Recommendation: Arrange prompt evaluation by a urologist.

---

## 🟡 Moderate

- Recurrent blood in urine

- Pain during urination

- Back pain

Recommendation: Consult a urologist as soon as possible.

---

## 🔴 High

Any of the following:

- Heavy bleeding in urine

- Inability to pass urine

- Severe flank pain

- High fever

- Blood clots causing urinary blockage

Recommendation: Seek immediate emergency medical care.

---

# Home Care

- Drink plenty of water unless advised otherwise.

- Avoid strenuous physical activity until evaluated.

- Monitor the color of your urine.

- Seek prompt medical assessment.

---

# Prevention

- Stay hydrated.

- Treat urinary infections promptly.

- Avoid smoking.

- Maintain good urinary health.

- Attend routine medical check-ups.

---

# When to Consult a Doctor

Consult a urologist immediately if:

- Blood is visible in the urine.

- Blood in urine recurs.

- Pain accompanies the bleeding.

- Fever or back pain develops.

---

# Emergency Warning Signs

Seek immediate medical care if you experience:

- Heavy bleeding in the urine

- Inability to urinate

- Severe back or side pain

- High fever with chills

- Large blood clots in the urine
