# Urinary Incontinence

## Overview

Urinary incontinence is the involuntary leakage of urine due to loss of
bladder control. It is a common condition that can affect people of all
ages but is more common in older adults and women after pregnancy or
childbirth. Although it is not usually life-threatening, it can
significantly affect quality of life and should be evaluated by a
healthcare professional.

**Note:** This condition is assessed primarily from reported
symptoms. Image analysis cannot diagnose urinary incontinence.

---

# Common Symptoms

- Leakage of urine

- Sudden strong urge to urinate

- Difficulty reaching the toilet in time

- Leakage while coughing, sneezing, laughing, or exercising

- Frequent urination

- Night-time urination

---

# Causes

Urinary incontinence occurs when the muscles and nerves controlling the
bladder do not function properly.

### Common causes

Weak pelvic floor muscles, pregnancy, childbirth, aging, urinary tract
infections, enlarged prostate, neurological disorders, obesity, and
certain medications.

---

# Risk Factors

- Older age

- Pregnancy and childbirth

- Obesity

- Diabetes

- Neurological disorders

- Enlarged prostate in men

---

# Severity Classification

## 🟢 Low

- Occasional urine leakage

- Mild symptoms during coughing or exercise

Recommendation: Practice bladder training and consult a urologist if
symptoms continue.

---

## 🟡 Moderate

- Frequent leakage

- Symptoms interfering with daily activities

- Night-time leakage

Recommendation: Consult a urologist for evaluation and treatment.

---

## 🔴 High

Any of the following:

- Complete inability to control urination

- Inability to pass urine

- Severe pelvic pain

- Blood in the urine

- Fever with urinary symptoms

Recommendation: Seek immediate medical evaluation.

---

# Home Care

- Perform pelvic floor (Kegel) exercises.

- Maintain a healthy body weight.

- Limit caffeine and alcohol if they worsen symptoms.

- Follow a regular bladder-emptying schedule.

- Maintain good personal hygiene.

---

# Prevention

- Maintain a healthy weight.

- Strengthen pelvic floor muscles.

- Avoid smoking.

- Treat urinary tract infections promptly.

- Stay physically active.

---

# When to Consult a Doctor

Consult a urologist if:

- Urine leakage becomes frequent.

- Symptoms interfere with daily life.

- Blood appears in the urine.

- Pain or fever develops.
