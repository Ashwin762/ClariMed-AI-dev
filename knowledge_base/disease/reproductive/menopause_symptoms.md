# Menopause Symptoms

## Overview

Menopause is a natural stage in a woman\'s life that marks the end of
menstrual periods, usually occurring between the ages of 45 and 55
years. It is confirmed after 12 consecutive months without a menstrual
period. Hormonal changes during menopause can cause a variety of
physical and emotional symptoms that may affect daily life.

**Note:** This condition is assessed primarily from reported
symptoms. Image analysis is not used for this body-part category.

---

# Common Symptoms

- Hot flashes

- Night sweats

- Irregular or absent periods

- Vaginal dryness

- Mood swings

- Sleep disturbances

- Reduced libido

- Difficulty concentrating

- Joint or muscle aches

---

# Causes

Menopause occurs naturally when the ovaries gradually produce less
estrogen and progesterone, leading to the end of reproductive function.

### Common causes

Natural aging, surgical removal of the ovaries, certain cancer
treatments, or premature ovarian insufficiency.

---

# Risk Factors

- Age over 45 years

- Family history of early menopause

- Smoking

- Surgical removal of the ovaries

- Chemotherapy or radiation therapy

---

# Severity Classification

## 🟢 Low

- Mild hot flashes

- Occasional night sweats

- Slight menstrual irregularity

Recommendation: Maintain a healthy lifestyle and attend routine
gynecological check-ups.

---

## 🟡 Moderate

- Frequent hot flashes

- Sleep disturbances

- Mood changes affecting daily activities

- Vaginal dryness causing discomfort

Recommendation: Consult a gynecologist for evaluation and management.

---

## 🔴 High

Any of the following:

- Heavy vaginal bleeding after menopause

- Severe pelvic pain

- Persistent depression

- Chest pain

- Fainting or loss of consciousness

Recommendation: Seek immediate medical evaluation to rule out serious
underlying conditions.

---

# Home Care

- Wear lightweight clothing to reduce discomfort from hot flashes.

- Maintain regular physical activity.

- Eat a balanced diet rich in calcium and vitamin D.

- Stay well hydrated.

- Practice stress management and maintain good sleep habits.

---

# Prevention

Although menopause cannot be prevented, symptoms may be managed by:

- Maintaining a healthy weight.

- Exercising regularly.

- Avoiding smoking.

- Limiting alcohol intake.

- Attending regular health check-ups.

---

# When to Consult a Doctor

Consult a gynecologist if:

- Menopause symptoms significantly affect daily life.

- Vaginal bleeding occurs after menopause.

- Severe mood changes develop.

- Persistent sleep disturbances occur.
