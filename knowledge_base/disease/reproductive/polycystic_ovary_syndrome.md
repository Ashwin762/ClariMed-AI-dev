# Polycystic Ovary Syndrome (PCOS)

## Overview

Polycystic Ovary Syndrome (PCOS) is a common hormonal disorder affecting
women of reproductive age. It can cause irregular menstrual periods,
acne, excessive hair growth, weight gain, and difficulty becoming
pregnant. Early diagnosis and lifestyle management can help reduce
symptoms and long-term health risks.

**Note:** This condition is assessed primarily from reported
symptoms. Image analysis is not used for this body-part category.

---

# Common Symptoms

- Irregular or missed periods

- Excess facial or body hair

- Acne

- Weight gain

- Thinning hair on the scalp

- Difficulty becoming pregnant

- Darkened skin folds

---

# Causes

The exact cause of PCOS is unknown, but it is associated with hormonal
imbalance and insulin resistance.

### Common causes

Hormonal imbalance, insulin resistance, genetic factors, and increased
androgen (male hormone) levels.

---

# Risk Factors

- Family history of PCOS

- Obesity

- Insulin resistance

- Type 2 diabetes

- Sedentary lifestyle

---

# Severity Classification

## 🟢 Low

- Mild menstrual irregularity

- Mild acne

Recommendation: Adopt a healthy lifestyle and consult a gynecologist.

---

## 🟡 Moderate

- Persistent irregular periods

- Noticeable excess hair growth

- Difficulty managing weight

Recommendation: Seek medical evaluation for diagnosis and management.

---

## 🔴 High

Any of the following:

- Severe abnormal bleeding

- Severe pelvic pain

- Symptoms associated with uncontrolled diabetes

- Difficulty becoming pregnant despite prolonged attempts

Recommendation: Seek prompt medical evaluation.

---

# Home Care

- Maintain a healthy body weight.

- Exercise regularly.

- Eat a balanced diet.

- Get adequate sleep.

- Follow your healthcare provider\'s recommendations.

---

# Prevention

Although PCOS cannot always be prevented, the following may help reduce
complications:

- Maintain a healthy weight.

- Exercise regularly.

- Eat a balanced diet.

- Attend regular health check-ups.

---

# When to Consult a Doctor

Consult a gynecologist if:

- Periods become irregular.

- Excess facial hair develops.

- Acne becomes severe.

- Difficulty becoming pregnant occurs.
