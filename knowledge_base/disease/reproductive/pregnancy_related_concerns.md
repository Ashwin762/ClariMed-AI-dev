# Pregnancy-Related Concerns

## Overview

Pregnancy-related concerns include symptoms or health issues that may
occur during pregnancy, ranging from normal pregnancy changes to
conditions requiring urgent medical attention. While nausea, fatigue,
and mild discomfort are common, severe pain or bleeding should always be
evaluated immediately.

**Note:** This condition is assessed primarily from reported
symptoms. Image analysis is not used for this body-part category.

---

# Common Symptoms

- Missed menstrual period

- Nausea or vomiting

- Breast tenderness

- Fatigue

- Mild abdominal discomfort

- Frequent urination

- Light spotting (in some cases)

---

# Causes

Pregnancy-related symptoms occur because of hormonal and physical
changes that support fetal development.

### Common causes

Normal pregnancy changes, hormonal fluctuations, implantation bleeding,
pregnancy complications, or underlying medical conditions.

---

# Risk Factors

- Previous pregnancy complications

- High blood pressure

- Diabetes

- Maternal age over 35 years

- Multiple pregnancy

- Obesity

---

# Severity Classification

## 🟢 Low

- Mild nausea

- Fatigue

- Mild abdominal discomfort

Recommendation: Attend routine prenatal care and follow your healthcare
provider\'s advice.

---

## 🟡 Moderate

- Persistent vomiting

- Moderate abdominal pain

- Recurrent dizziness

Recommendation: Consult a gynecologist promptly.

---

## 🔴 High

Any of the following:

- Heavy vaginal bleeding

- Severe abdominal pain

- Loss of consciousness

- Severe headache with blurred vision

- Reduced fetal movements (later pregnancy)

- Convulsions

Recommendation: Seek immediate emergency medical care.

---

# Home Care

- Eat a balanced diet.

- Drink plenty of fluids.

- Get adequate rest.

- Attend all prenatal appointments.

- Avoid smoking, alcohol, and harmful substances.

---

# Prevention

- Begin prenatal care early.

- Take prenatal supplements as advised.

- Eat nutritious foods.

- Stay physically active as recommended.

- Attend regular pregnancy check-ups.

---

# When to Consult a Doctor

Consult a gynecologist if:

- Severe nausea or vomiting develops.

- Persistent abdominal pain occurs.

- Vaginal bleeding develops.

- You have concerns about your pregnancy.

---

# Emergency Warning Signs

Seek immediate medical care if you experience:

- Heavy vaginal bleeding

- Severe abdominal pain

- Convulsions

- Loss of consciousness

- Severe headache with blurred vision

- Reduced fetal movements
