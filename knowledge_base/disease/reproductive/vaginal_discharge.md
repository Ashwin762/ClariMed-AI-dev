# Vaginal Discharge / Infection

## Overview

Vaginal discharge is a normal bodily function that helps keep the vagina
clean and healthy. However, changes in the color, smell, consistency, or
amount of discharge may indicate an infection or another underlying
condition. Early evaluation helps identify the cause and prevent
complications.

**Note:** This condition is assessed entirely from reported
symptoms. No image analysis is performed to maintain user privacy.

---

# Common Symptoms

- Thick or thin abnormal vaginal discharge

- Foul-smelling discharge

- Vaginal itching

- Burning during urination

- Pain during sexual intercourse

- Redness or irritation around the vaginal area

- Pelvic discomfort

---

# Causes

Abnormal vaginal discharge usually occurs due to infection or changes in
the normal vaginal environment.

### Common causes

Bacterial vaginosis, yeast infection, sexually transmitted infections
(STIs), hormonal changes, poor hygiene, and allergic reactions.

---

# Risk Factors

- Poor genital hygiene

- Unprotected sexual activity

- Diabetes

- Pregnancy

- Recent antibiotic use

- Hormonal changes

---

# Severity Classification

## 🟢 Low

- Mild discharge without pain

- Mild itching

Recommendation: Maintain good hygiene and consult a gynecologist if
symptoms persist.

---

## 🟡 Moderate

- Persistent discharge

- Bad odor

- Burning or itching

Recommendation: Consult a gynecologist for evaluation.

---

## 🔴 High

Any of the following:

- Severe pelvic pain

- High fever

- Heavy bleeding

- Green or bloody discharge

- Severe lower abdominal pain

Recommendation: Seek immediate medical evaluation.

---

# Home Care

- Maintain good genital hygiene.

- Wear breathable cotton underwear.

- Avoid scented feminine hygiene products.

- Keep the genital area dry.

- Follow your healthcare provider\'s recommendations.

---

# Prevention

- Practice good personal hygiene.

- Avoid unnecessary vaginal douching.

- Practice safe sexual practices.

- Wear clean, breathable underwear.

- Attend regular gynecological check-ups.

---

# When to Consult a Doctor

Consult a gynecologist if:

- Discharge changes in color or smell.

- Itching or burning develops.

- Pelvic pain occurs.

- Symptoms persist for more than a few days.
