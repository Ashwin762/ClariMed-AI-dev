# Menstrual Irregularities

## Overview

Menstrual irregularities refer to changes in the normal menstrual cycle,
including missed periods, unusually heavy or light bleeding, prolonged
bleeding, or irregular cycle lengths. These changes may occur due to
hormonal imbalances, stress, lifestyle changes, pregnancy, or underlying
medical conditions. Persistent menstrual irregularities should be
evaluated by a healthcare professional.

**Note:** This condition is assessed primarily from reported
symptoms. Image analysis is not used for this body-part category.

---

# Common Symptoms

- Missed periods

- Heavy menstrual bleeding

- Very light periods

- Irregular cycle length

- Painful menstrual cramps

- Bleeding between periods

- Prolonged menstrual bleeding

---

# Causes

Menstrual irregularities occur when the normal hormonal regulation of
the menstrual cycle is disrupted.

### Common causes

Hormonal imbalance, stress, excessive exercise, sudden weight changes,
thyroid disorders, polycystic ovary syndrome (PCOS), pregnancy, and
certain medications.

---

# Risk Factors

- Hormonal disorders

- Stress

- Obesity

- Thyroid disease

- PCOS

- Extreme physical activity

---

# Severity Classification

## 🟢 Low

- Slight variation in cycle length

- Mild menstrual discomfort

Recommendation: Monitor the menstrual cycle and maintain a healthy
lifestyle.

---

## 🟡 Moderate

- Repeated missed periods

- Heavy bleeding

- Significant menstrual pain

Recommendation: Consult a gynecologist for evaluation.

---

## 🔴 High

Any of the following:

- Extremely heavy bleeding

- Bleeding lasting more than 7 days

- Severe abdominal pain

- Dizziness or fainting due to blood loss

Recommendation: Seek immediate medical evaluation.

---

# Home Care

- Maintain a healthy diet.

- Stay physically active.

- Track menstrual cycles.

- Manage stress effectively.

- Get adequate sleep.

---

# Prevention

- Maintain a healthy body weight.

- Exercise regularly.

- Manage stress.

- Attend routine gynecological check-ups.

---

# When to Consult a Doctor

Consult a gynecologist if:

- Periods are absent for several months.

- Bleeding becomes unusually heavy.

- Severe pain occurs during menstruation.

- Bleeding occurs between periods.
