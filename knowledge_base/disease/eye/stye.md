# Stye

## Overview

A stye (hordeolum) is a small, painful, red lump that forms near the edge of the eyelid, caused by a blocked and infected oil gland. It typically resolves on its own within a week to ten days with simple home care.

---

# Common Symptoms

- A small, tender, red bump on the eyelid
- Localized swelling
- Pain or tenderness when touched
- Mild watering of the eye
- A gritty sensation

---

# Causes

### Bacterial infection

Usually caused by *Staphylococcus* bacteria infecting an eyelid oil gland or hair follicle.

---

# Risk Factors

- Touching the eyes with unwashed hands
- Using old or contaminated eye makeup
- Poor contact lens hygiene
- Pre-existing blepharitis

---

# Severity Classification

## 🟢 Low

- Small, mildly tender bump
- No significant vision impact

Recommendation: Home care with warm compresses.

---

## 🟡 Moderate

- Increasing swelling or pain
- Bump does not improve after a week of home care

Recommendation: Consult an ophthalmologist — may require drainage.

---

# Home Care

- Apply a warm compress to the affected eyelid for 10-15 minutes, several times a day.
- Avoid squeezing or popping the stye.
- Avoid wearing eye makeup or contact lenses until it resolves.
- Wash hands before touching the eye area.

---

# Prevention

- Wash hands regularly, especially before touching the eyes.
- Remove eye makeup before sleeping and replace it periodically.
- Clean contact lenses properly and avoid sleeping in them.
- Manage any underlying blepharitis.

---

# When to Consult a Doctor

Consult an ophthalmologist if:

- The stye doesn't improve within a week of warm compresses
- Swelling spreads beyond the eyelid
- Vision is affected

---

# Emergency Warning Signs

Seek prompt care if:

- Redness and swelling spread rapidly around the eye
- Fever accompanies the eyelid swelling
- Vision changes occur
