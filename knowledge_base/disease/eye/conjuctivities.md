# Conjunctivitis

## Overview

Conjunctivitis is an inflammation of the conjunctiva, the thin transparent membrane covering the white part of the eye and the inner surface of the eyelids. It is one of the most common causes of red eye and may affect people of all ages.

Most cases are self-limiting, but some forms require prompt medical attention to prevent complications.

---

# Types

## Viral Conjunctivitis

Usually caused by adenoviruses.

Characteristics:

- Highly contagious
- Watery discharge
- Red eye
- Often starts in one eye before affecting the other
- Frequently associated with cold or upper respiratory infection

---

## Bacterial Conjunctivitis

Caused by bacterial infection.

Characteristics:

- Thick yellow or green discharge
- Eyelids stuck together after sleep
- Redness
- Mild irritation

Most mild cases improve with appropriate treatment.

---

## Allergic Conjunctivitis

Triggered by allergens.

Examples:

- Pollen
- Dust
- Pet dander
- Mold

Characteristics:

- Usually affects both eyes
- Intense itching
- Watering
- Swollen eyelids

Not contagious.

---

## Irritant / Toxic Conjunctivitis

Caused by:

- Smoke
- Chemical exposure
- Air pollution
- Chlorinated water
- Foreign bodies

Usually improves after removing the irritant.

---

# Common Symptoms

- Eye redness
- Watery eyes
- Eye itching
- Burning sensation
- Mild pain or irritation
- Foreign body sensation
- Swollen eyelids
- Eye discharge
- Crusting of eyelashes
- Mild sensitivity to light

# Causes

### Viral infection

Most commonly adenovirus.

### Bacterial infection

Common bacteria affecting the conjunctiva.

### Allergic reaction

Immune response to allergens.

### Environmental irritants

Smoke

Dust

Chemicals

Air pollution

# Risk Factors

- Poor hand hygiene
- Wearing contact lenses
- Seasonal allergies
- Frequent eye rubbing
- Shared towels or cosmetics
- Recent upper respiratory infection

# Severity Classification

## 🟢 Low

- Mild redness
- Mild itching
- Minimal watering
- Normal vision
- No significant pain

Recommendation:

Home care and observation.

---

## 🟡 Moderate

- Persistent redness
- Moderate discharge
- Swollen eyelids
- Symptoms lasting several days

Recommendation:

Consult an ophthalmologist.

---

## 🔴 High

Any of the following:

- Severe eye pain
- Blurred vision
- Sudden vision loss
- Significant light sensitivity
- Chemical injury
- Eye trauma
- Contact lens wearer with severe pain
- Rapid worsening symptoms

Recommendation:

Seek immediate emergency eye care.

---

# Home Care

- Wash hands frequently.
- Avoid rubbing the eyes.
- Use clean tissues.
- Clean discharge gently.
- Wash pillow covers regularly.
- Do not share towels.
- Avoid contact lenses until recovery.
- Maintain proper eye hygiene.

---

# Prevention

- Wash hands regularly.
- Avoid touching your eyes.
- Replace contaminated eye makeup.
- Avoid sharing towels.
- Properly disinfect contact lenses.
- Stay away from infected individuals whenever possible.

---

# When to Consult a Doctor

Consult an ophthalmologist if:

- Symptoms worsen
- Thick discharge continues
- Vision becomes blurry
- Symptoms persist beyond several days
- Eye pain increases
- You wear contact lenses
- A newborn develops conjunctivitis

---

# Emergency Warning Signs

Seek emergency care immediately if:

- Sudden loss of vision
- Severe eye pain
- Chemical splash into the eye
- Penetrating eye injury
- Severe swelling around the eye
- High fever with severe eye infection

# Patient Education

Most cases of conjunctivitis recover completely with appropriate care.

Maintaining proper hygiene helps reduce the spread of infectious conjunctivitis.

Avoid self-medicating with antibiotic or steroid eye drops without medical advice.

---

# Frequently Asked Questions

## Is conjunctivitis contagious?

Viral and bacterial conjunctivitis are contagious.

Allergic conjunctivitis is not contagious.

---

## Can I go to school or work?

People with infectious conjunctivitis should follow medical advice regarding return to school or work and practice good hygiene to reduce transmission.

---

## Can I wear contact lenses?

No.

Avoid wearing contact lenses until symptoms have completely resolved and your healthcare professional advises that it is safe.

---

## Can conjunctivitis affect vision?

Temporary blurred vision may occur because of discharge or irritation.

Persistent vision loss requires urgent medical evaluation.
