# Cataract

## Overview

A cataract is a clouding of the eye's natural lens, causing progressively blurry, hazy, or dimmed vision. It develops slowly, most commonly with age, and is one of the leading causes of treatable vision impairment worldwide.

---

# Common Symptoms

- Cloudy, blurry, or hazy vision
- Increased sensitivity to glare, especially at night
- Faded or yellowed color perception
- Difficulty seeing well at night
- A visibly cloudy or whitish tint to the pupil in advanced cases

---

# Causes

### Age-related lens changes

Proteins in the lens break down and clump over time, clouding the lens.

### Other contributing factors

Diabetes, prolonged UV exposure, smoking, certain medications (long-term steroid use), and eye injury.

---

# Risk Factors

- Advancing age
- Diabetes
- Prolonged sun/UV exposure
- Smoking
- Long-term corticosteroid use
- Family history

---

# Typical Image Characteristics

Image analysis may show:

- Elevated whiteness/cloudiness concentrated around the pupil area
- Low textural irregularity (cataracts appear as smooth, uniform cloudiness, not patchy)

---

# Severity Classification

## 🟢 Low

- Mild blurring, minimal daily impact
- Vision still largely functional

Recommendation: Monitor and schedule a routine eye exam.

---

## 🟡 Moderate

- Noticeable vision impairment affecting reading, driving, or night vision
- Increasing glare sensitivity

Recommendation: Consult an ophthalmologist to discuss monitoring or surgical options.

---

## 🔴 High

- Significant vision loss affecting daily independence
- Complete lens clouding

Recommendation: Prompt ophthalmology evaluation — cataract surgery is a well-established, effective treatment.

---

# Home Care

- Use brighter lighting for reading and close work.
- Wear anti-glare sunglasses outdoors.
- Update eyeglass prescriptions as vision changes.
- Avoid night driving if glare sensitivity is significant.

---

# Prevention

- Wear UV-protective sunglasses outdoors.
- Manage diabetes and blood sugar levels well.
- Avoid smoking.
- Get regular comprehensive eye exams, especially after age 40.

---

# When to Consult a Doctor

Consult an ophthalmologist if:

- Vision changes are affecting daily activities (reading, driving, recognizing faces)
- Glare or night vision is significantly worsening
- Vision changes are rapid or affect only one eye suddenly

---

# Emergency Warning Signs

Cataracts develop gradually and are rarely an emergency on their own, but seek prompt care if:

- Sudden vision loss occurs (may indicate a different, urgent condition)
- Eye pain accompanies vision changes
