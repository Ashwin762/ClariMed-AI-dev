# Eye Strain

## Overview

Eye strain, also known as Computer Vision Syndrome or Digital Eye
Strain, is a common condition caused by prolonged use of digital screens
or activities requiring intense visual focus. Although it is usually
temporary, persistent symptoms can affect comfort and productivity.

**Note:** This condition is assessed primarily from reported
symptoms. There are no reliable visual signs that can be identified
through image analysis, so the AI does not request an image for this
body-part category.

---

# Common Symptoms

- Tired or sore eyes

- Dry or watery eyes

- Blurred vision

- Headache

- Difficulty focusing

- Neck or shoulder pain

- Increased sensitivity to light

---

# Causes

Eye strain develops when the eyes work harder than usual for prolonged
periods.

### Common causes

Extended screen time, poor lighting, glare, improper viewing distance,
uncorrected vision problems, and reduced blinking while using digital
devices.

---

# Risk Factors

- Prolonged computer or mobile device use

- Poor workstation ergonomics

- Inadequate lighting

- Uncorrected vision problems

- Long hours of reading or close-up work

---

# Severity Classification

## 🟢 Low

- Mild eye discomfort

- Symptoms improve after resting the eyes

Recommendation: Reduce screen time and follow healthy eye-care
practices.

---

## 🟡 Moderate

- Persistent eye strain

- Frequent headaches

- Blurred vision affecting daily work

Recommendation: Consult an ophthalmologist if symptoms continue despite
adequate rest.

---

# Home Care

- Follow the 20-20-20 rule (every 20 minutes, look at something 20 feet
away for 20 seconds).

- Blink frequently.

- Adjust screen brightness and contrast.

- Maintain proper viewing distance.

- Take regular breaks during screen use.

---

# Prevention

- Limit continuous screen time.

- Ensure proper lighting.

- Maintain good posture while working.

- Have regular eye examinations.

- Use appropriate eyewear if prescribed.

---

# When to Consult a Doctor

Consult an ophthalmologist if:

- Symptoms persist despite rest.

- Blurred vision continues.

- Eye pain becomes severe.

- Headaches become frequent.
