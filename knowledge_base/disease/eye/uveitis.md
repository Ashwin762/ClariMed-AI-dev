# Uveitis

## Overview

Uveitis is an inflammation of the uvea, the middle layer of the eye. It
can affect one or both eyes and may develop suddenly or gradually. If
left untreated, it can lead to vision loss or other serious eye
complications.

**Note:** This condition is assessed primarily from reported
symptoms. Although redness may be visible, a photograph alone cannot
reliably diagnose uveitis. Therefore, the AI relies mainly on
symptom-based assessment.

---

# Common Symptoms

- Eye redness

- Eye pain

- Blurred vision

- Sensitivity to light (photophobia)

- Floaters (small spots moving across vision)

- Decreased vision

- Excessive tearing

---

# Causes

Uveitis can occur due to infections, autoimmune diseases, or eye
injuries. In some cases, the exact cause remains unknown.

### Common causes

Autoimmune disorders, viral or bacterial infections, eye trauma,
inflammatory diseases, and certain systemic illnesses.

---

# Risk Factors

- Autoimmune diseases

- Previous episodes of uveitis

- Eye injury

- Eye infections

- Family history of inflammatory disorders

- Smoking

---

# Severity Classification

## 🟢 Low

- Mild redness with slight discomfort

- No significant vision changes

Recommendation: Schedule an appointment with an ophthalmologist for
evaluation.

---

## 🟡 Moderate

- Persistent eye pain

- Blurred vision

- Increased sensitivity to light

Recommendation: Consult an ophthalmologist as soon as possible for
proper diagnosis and treatment.

---

## 🔴 High

Any of the following:

- Sudden loss of vision

- Severe eye pain

- Intense redness with worsening vision

- Large increase in floaters or flashes of light

Recommendation: Seek immediate emergency eye care to prevent permanent
vision loss.

---

# Home Care

- Avoid rubbing the affected eye.

- Wear sunglasses if light sensitivity is present.

- Rest your eyes and avoid prolonged screen exposure.

- Follow your eye specialist\'s instructions carefully.

- Maintain good eye hygiene.

---

# Prevention

- Wear protective eyewear during risky activities.

- Manage autoimmune or inflammatory conditions appropriately.

- Seek prompt treatment for eye infections.

- Attend regular eye examinations.

---

# When to Consult a Doctor

Consult an ophthalmologist if:

- Eye redness lasts more than a day.

- You experience eye pain or blurred vision.

- Light sensitivity becomes severe.

- Floaters suddenly increase.

---

# Emergency Warning Signs

Seek immediate emergency medical care if you experience:

- Sudden vision loss

- Severe eye pain

- Rapidly worsening blurred vision

- Flashes of light with many new floaters

- Severe redness accompanied by vision changes
