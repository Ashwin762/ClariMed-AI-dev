# Glaucoma

## Overview

Glaucoma is a group of eye conditions that damage the optic nerve, often related to elevated pressure inside the eye. Chronic glaucoma progresses slowly and painlessly, while **acute angle-closure glaucoma is a sudden, painful medical emergency** that can cause permanent vision loss within hours if untreated.

---

# Common Symptoms

### Chronic (open-angle) glaucoma
- Gradual, often unnoticed loss of peripheral vision
- No pain in early stages

### Acute (angle-closure) glaucoma — EMERGENCY
- Sudden, severe eye pain
- Halos or rainbow rings seen around lights
- Blurred vision
- Redness of the eye
- Nausea and vomiting
- Headache

---

# Causes

### Elevated intraocular pressure

Fluid buildup inside the eye increases pressure, damaging the optic nerve over time (chronic) or suddenly (acute angle closure).

---

# Risk Factors

- Age over 60
- Family history of glaucoma
- Diabetes
- High eye pressure
- Certain eye anatomy (narrow drainage angles, more common in some ethnic groups)
- Long-term steroid use

---

# Typical Image Characteristics

Image analysis may show:

- Elevated redness combined with some corneal cloudiness (whiteness) during an acute episode
- Textural irregularity around the eye

Note: many forms of glaucoma have no visible external signs at all — a normal-appearing photo does NOT rule out glaucoma. Regular eye pressure checks are the only reliable screening method for chronic glaucoma.

---

# Severity Classification

## 🟡 Moderate

- Gradual peripheral vision changes noticed
- No pain, no redness

Recommendation: Schedule an ophthalmology evaluation for eye pressure testing.

---

## 🔴 High — Possible Emergency

Any of the following:

- Sudden severe eye pain
- Halos around lights
- Sudden blurred vision with redness
- Nausea/vomiting with eye pain

Recommendation: **Seek emergency eye care immediately.** Acute angle-closure glaucoma can cause permanent vision loss within hours.

---

# Home Care

Chronic glaucoma management (medicated eye drops, monitoring) must be directed by an ophthalmologist — there is no safe home treatment for glaucoma itself. For acute symptoms, do not wait or attempt home remedies — seek emergency care immediately.

---

# Prevention

- Get regular comprehensive eye exams, including eye pressure checks, especially after age 40 or with a family history.
- Manage diabetes and blood pressure well.
- Follow prescribed glaucoma treatment consistently if already diagnosed.

---

# When to Consult a Doctor

Schedule a routine ophthalmology visit if:

- You have risk factors (age, family history, diabetes) and haven't had a recent eye pressure check
- You've noticed gradual peripheral vision changes

---

# Emergency Warning Signs

**Seek emergency care immediately if:**

- Sudden severe eye pain
- Halos or rainbow rings around lights
- Sudden vision blurring with redness
- Nausea or vomiting accompanying eye pain
- Headache with eye pain and vision changes

---

# Recommended Specialist

Ophthalmologist (emergency room if acute symptoms present)
