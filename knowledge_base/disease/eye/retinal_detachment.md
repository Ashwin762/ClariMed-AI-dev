# Retinal Detachment

## Overview

Retinal detachment is a serious eye condition in which the retina
separates from the back of the eye. This prevents the retina from
receiving oxygen and nutrients, and if left untreated, it can lead to
permanent vision loss. Immediate medical attention is essential.

**Note:** This condition is assessed primarily from reported
symptoms. Although some visible changes may occur, a photograph alone
cannot reliably diagnose retinal detachment. The AI relies mainly on
symptom-based assessment.

---

# Common Symptoms

- Sudden appearance of floaters

- Flashes of light

- Blurred vision

- A shadow or curtain moving across the field of vision

- Sudden partial or complete vision loss

---

# Causes

Retinal detachment usually occurs when a tear or hole develops in the
retina, allowing fluid to collect underneath it.

### Common causes

Age-related changes in the eye, severe nearsightedness, eye injury,
previous eye surgery, and diabetic eye disease.

---

# Risk Factors

- Age over 50 years

- Severe nearsightedness

- Previous retinal detachment

- Eye injury

- Previous eye surgery

- Diabetes affecting the eyes

- Family history of retinal detachment

---

# Severity Classification

## 🟢 Low

- Mild floaters without vision loss

Recommendation: Arrange an urgent ophthalmology evaluation.

---

## 🟡 Moderate

- Increasing flashes of light

- More floaters

- Mild blurred vision

Recommendation: Seek same-day evaluation by an ophthalmologist.

---

## 🔴 High

Any of the following:

- Curtain-like shadow over vision

- Sudden vision loss

- Rapid worsening of vision

- Large increase in flashes or floaters

Recommendation: Seek immediate emergency eye care to reduce the risk of
permanent blindness.

---

# Home Care

- Avoid strenuous physical activity.

- Do not rub the affected eye.

- Seek emergency medical care immediately.

- Follow the ophthalmologist\'s instructions carefully.

---

# Prevention

- Have regular comprehensive eye examinations.

- Wear protective eyewear during sports or hazardous work.

- Manage diabetes and other chronic medical conditions.

- Seek prompt medical attention for any sudden vision changes.

---

# When to Consult a Doctor

Consult an ophthalmologist immediately if you notice:

- Sudden flashes of light

- A rapid increase in floaters

- Blurred vision

- A shadow or curtain over your vision

---

# Emergency Warning Signs

Seek emergency medical care immediately if you experience:

- Sudden loss of vision

- Curtain-like shadow across vision

- Rapid increase in flashes and floaters

- Severe visual disturbance after an eye injury
