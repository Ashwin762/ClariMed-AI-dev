# Blepharitis

## Overview

Blepharitis is inflammation of the eyelid margins, often causing redness, irritation, and crusting around the eyelashes. It's a common, usually chronic condition that can be managed well with consistent eyelid hygiene.

---

# Common Symptoms

- Redness along the eyelid margins
- Itching or burning sensation
- Crusting or flaking at the base of the eyelashes, especially on waking
- A gritty or foreign-body sensation
- Mild eyelid swelling

---

# Causes

### Blocked oil glands

Meibomian glands along the eyelid margin become blocked or dysfunctional.

### Bacterial overgrowth

Bacteria naturally present on the skin can overgrow at the eyelid margin.

### Associated skin conditions

Seborrheic dermatitis or rosacea can contribute to blepharitis.

---

# Risk Factors

- Oily skin or dandruff (seborrheic dermatitis)
- Rosacea
- Allergies
- Contact lens wear
- Eyelash mites (in some chronic cases)

# Severity Classification

## 🟢 Low

- Mild redness and occasional crusting
- Minimal discomfort

Recommendation: Home eyelid hygiene routine.

---

## 🟡 Moderate

- Persistent crusting affecting daily comfort
- Recurrent irritation despite home care

Recommendation: Consult an ophthalmologist for a tailored management plan.

---

# Home Care

- Apply a warm compress to the eyelids for 5-10 minutes daily.
- Gently clean the eyelid margins with a diluted baby shampoo or a lid-scrub product.
- Avoid eye makeup during flare-ups.
- Replace old eye makeup and applicators.

---

# Prevention

- Maintain a consistent eyelid hygiene routine, even when symptoms improve.
- Manage underlying skin conditions like rosacea or dandruff.
- Remove eye makeup thoroughly before sleeping.

---

# When to Consult a Doctor

Consult an ophthalmologist if:

- Symptoms persist despite regular eyelid hygiene
- Vision is affected
- Eyelids become significantly swollen or painful

---

# Emergency Warning Signs

Blepharitis is rarely an emergency, but seek care if:

- Vision changes accompany the symptoms
- Signs of a spreading eyelid infection (increasing pain, swelling, fever)
