# Tremor

## Overview

A tremor is an involuntary, rhythmic shaking movement affecting one or
more parts of the body, most commonly the hands. Tremors may occur while
resting or during movement and can result from neurological conditions,
medications, or other medical disorders. Although many tremors are not
life-threatening, persistent or worsening tremors should be medically
evaluated.

**Note:** This condition is assessed primarily from reported
symptoms. Visible shaking may support the assessment, but diagnosis
requires clinical evaluation.

---

# Common Symptoms

- Shaking of the hands

- Trembling while holding objects

- Difficulty writing

- Head shaking

- Voice tremor

- Difficulty performing fine motor tasks

---

# Causes

Tremors occur due to abnormal functioning of the parts of the brain
responsible for controlling movement.

### Common causes

Essential tremor, Parkinson\'s disease, anxiety, medications, excessive
caffeine intake, thyroid disorders, and neurological diseases.

---

# Risk Factors

- Family history of tremor

- Older age

- Neurological disorders

- Excessive caffeine consumption

- Certain medications

---

# Severity Classification

## 🟢 Low

- Mild occasional hand shaking

- No effect on daily activities

Recommendation: Schedule a neurological evaluation.

---

## 🟡 Moderate

- Frequent tremors

- Difficulty writing or eating

Recommendation: Consult a neurologist for evaluation.

---

## 🔴 High

Any of the following:

- Sudden onset of tremor

- Tremor with weakness or numbness

- Tremor after head injury

- Tremor with difficulty speaking or walking

Recommendation: Seek immediate medical evaluation.

---

# Home Care

- Limit caffeine intake.

- Manage stress.

- Get adequate sleep.

- Avoid alcohol misuse.

- Follow your neurologist\'s recommendations.

---

# Prevention

- Manage underlying medical conditions.

- Reduce stress.

- Maintain healthy sleep habits.

- Attend regular medical check-ups.

---

# When to Consult a Doctor

Consult a neurologist if:

- Tremors worsen over time.

- Daily activities become difficult.

- Tremors develop suddenly.

- New neurological symptoms appear.
