# Seizure / Epilepsy Warning Signs

## Overview

A seizure is a sudden burst of abnormal electrical activity in the brain
that can cause changes in movement, behavior, awareness, or
consciousness. Seizures may occur once due to illness or injury or
repeatedly in people with epilepsy. Immediate medical attention is
required if a seizure lasts too long or occurs for the first time.

**Note:** This condition is assessed primarily from reported
symptoms. Image analysis cannot diagnose seizures.

---

# Common Symptoms

- Uncontrolled shaking of the body

- Loss of consciousness

- Staring spells

- Muscle stiffness

- Confusion after the episode

- Temporary loss of awareness

- Biting the tongue

- Loss of bladder control

---

# Causes

Seizures occur due to sudden abnormal electrical activity in the brain.

### Common causes

Epilepsy, high fever (especially in children), head injury, stroke,
brain infection, low blood sugar, alcohol withdrawal, and certain
medications.

---

# Risk Factors

- Family history of epilepsy

- Previous head injury

- Stroke

- Brain infection

- Brain tumors

- High fever in young children

---

# Severity Classification

## 🟢 Low

- Brief seizure lasting less than one minute

- Full recovery afterward

Recommendation: Schedule a neurological evaluation.

---

## 🟡 Moderate

- Recurrent seizures

- Confusion after episodes

- Minor injuries during seizures

Recommendation: Consult a neurologist promptly.

---

## 🔴 High

Any of the following:

- Seizure lasting more than 5 minutes

- Multiple seizures without recovery

- Difficulty breathing after the seizure

- First seizure in life

- Serious injury during the seizure

Recommendation: Seek immediate emergency medical care.

---

# Home Care

- Keep the person safe from injury.

- Turn the person onto their side if possible.

- Do not place anything in the mouth.

- Loosen tight clothing around the neck.

- Stay with the person until fully alert.

---

# Prevention

- Take prescribed medications regularly if diagnosed with epilepsy.

- Get adequate sleep.

- Avoid excessive alcohol consumption.

- Manage fever promptly in children.

- Follow your neurologist\'s advice.

---

# When to Consult a Doctor

Consult a neurologist if:

- A first seizure occurs.

- Seizures become more frequent.

- Recovery takes longer than usual.

- New neurological symptoms develop.

---

# Emergency Warning Signs

Seek immediate medical care if:

- Seizure lasts longer than 5 minutes.

- Multiple seizures occur without recovery.

- Difficulty breathing develops.

- Serious injury occurs.

- Pregnancy or diabetes is present.
