# Stroke Warning Signs

## Overview

A stroke occurs when the blood supply to part of the brain is
interrupted or when a blood vessel in the brain bursts. It is a
life-threatening medical emergency that requires immediate treatment to
reduce brain damage and improve recovery.

**Note:** This condition is assessed entirely from reported
symptoms. Immediate medical attention is essential if stroke is
suspected.

---

# Common Symptoms

- Sudden weakness or numbness on one side of the body

- Face drooping

- Difficulty speaking or understanding speech

- Sudden loss of vision

- Difficulty walking

- Loss of balance

- Severe sudden headache

- Confusion

---

# Causes

Stroke occurs when blood flow to the brain is interrupted.

### Common causes

Blocked blood vessels (ischemic stroke), bleeding in the brain
(hemorrhagic stroke), high blood pressure, heart disease, and blood
clots.

---

# Risk Factors

- High blood pressure

- Diabetes

- Smoking

- High cholesterol

- Heart disease

- Older age

---

# Severity Classification

## 🟢 Low

- Brief symptoms that completely resolve (possible TIA)

Recommendation: Seek immediate medical evaluation even if symptoms
disappear.

---

## 🟡 Moderate

- Mild weakness

- Speech difficulty

- Walking difficulty

Recommendation: Go to the nearest hospital immediately.

---

## 🔴 High

Remember **FAST**:

- **F -- Face drooping**

- **A -- Arm weakness**

- **S -- Speech difficulty**

- **T -- Time to call emergency services**

Other emergency signs include:

- Sudden loss of vision

- Severe headache

- Loss of consciousness

Recommendation: Seek emergency medical care immediately. Every minute
matters.

---

# Home Care

- Do **not** attempt home treatment.

- Call emergency medical services immediately.

- Keep the person comfortable.

- Do not give food or drinks if swallowing is affected.

- Note the exact time symptoms began if possible.

---

# Prevention

- Control blood pressure.

- Manage diabetes.

- Avoid smoking.

- Exercise regularly.

- Eat a heart-healthy diet.

---

# When to Consult a Doctor

Seek emergency medical care immediately if **any** stroke symptoms
occur, even if they improve within a few minutes.

---

# Emergency Warning Signs

Seek immediate emergency medical care if you experience:

- Face drooping

- Arm weakness

- Slurred speech

- Sudden vision loss

- Severe headache

- Loss of consciousness
