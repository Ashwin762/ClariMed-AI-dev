# Numbness or Tingling (Peripheral Neuropathy)

## Overview

Peripheral neuropathy is a condition that occurs when the nerves outside
the brain and spinal cord become damaged. It commonly causes numbness,
tingling, burning pain, or weakness in the hands and feet. Symptoms
often develop gradually but may worsen over time if the underlying cause
is not treated.

**Note:** This condition is assessed primarily from reported
symptoms. Image analysis cannot reliably diagnose peripheral neuropathy.

---

# Common Symptoms

- Numbness in the hands or feet

- Tingling or \"pins and needles\" sensation

- Burning pain

- Sharp or stabbing pain

- Muscle weakness

- Loss of balance

- Reduced sensation to touch

- Difficulty walking

---

# Causes

Peripheral neuropathy develops when peripheral nerves become damaged.

### Common causes

Diabetes, vitamin deficiencies, alcohol misuse, infections, autoimmune
diseases, certain medications, kidney disease, and physical injury.

---

# Risk Factors

- Diabetes

- Vitamin B12 deficiency

- Chronic kidney disease

- Excessive alcohol use

- Autoimmune disorders

- Older age

---

# Severity Classification

## 🟢 Low

- Mild tingling

- Occasional numbness

- No difficulty walking

Recommendation: Schedule an appointment with a neurologist for
evaluation.

---

## 🟡 Moderate

- Persistent numbness

- Burning pain

- Weakness affecting daily activities

Recommendation: Consult a neurologist for further assessment and
treatment.

---

## 🔴 High

Any of the following:

- Sudden loss of sensation

- Rapidly worsening weakness

- Difficulty walking

- Loss of bladder or bowel control

- Numbness following a serious injury

Recommendation: Seek immediate medical evaluation, as these symptoms may
indicate a serious neurological condition.

---

# Home Care

- Maintain good blood sugar control if you have diabetes.

- Eat a balanced diet rich in essential nutrients.

- Avoid excessive alcohol consumption.

- Protect numb areas from injury.

- Follow your healthcare provider\'s recommendations.

---

# Prevention

- Control diabetes effectively.

- Maintain adequate vitamin intake.

- Avoid smoking and excessive alcohol.

- Exercise regularly.

- Attend routine medical check-ups.

---

# When to Consult a Doctor

Consult a neurologist if:

- Numbness or tingling persists.

- Symptoms gradually worsen.

- Muscle weakness develops.

- Walking becomes difficult.

---

# Emergency Warning Signs

Seek immediate medical care if you experience:

- Sudden severe weakness

- Loss of bladder or bowel control

- Rapidly spreading numbness

- Difficulty walking

- Numbness after a serious injury
