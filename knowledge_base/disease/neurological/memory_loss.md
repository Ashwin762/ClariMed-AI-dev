# Memory Loss / Confusion

## Overview

Memory loss refers to difficulty remembering information, while
confusion involves problems with thinking, awareness, or
decision-making. Mild forgetfulness may occur with aging, but sudden
confusion or rapidly worsening memory problems may indicate a serious
medical condition requiring urgent evaluation.

**Note:** This condition is assessed primarily from reported
symptoms. Image analysis cannot diagnose memory disorders.

---

# Common Symptoms

- Forgetting recent events

- Difficulty remembering names

- Confusion about time or place

- Repeating questions

- Difficulty concentrating

- Poor judgment

- Getting lost in familiar places

---

# Causes

Memory loss and confusion may result from neurological, metabolic,
psychiatric, or infectious conditions.

### Common causes

Dementia, Alzheimer\'s disease, stroke, head injury, infections, vitamin
deficiencies, medication side effects, depression, and dehydration.

---

# Risk Factors

- Older age

- Family history of dementia

- Stroke

- Diabetes

- High blood pressure

- Previous head injury

---

# Severity Classification

## 🟢 Low

- Mild forgetfulness

- Occasional memory lapses

Recommendation: Schedule a neurological evaluation.

---

## 🟡 Moderate

- Frequent memory problems

- Difficulty performing daily activities

- Increasing confusion

Recommendation: Consult a neurologist promptly.

---

## 🔴 High

Any of the following:

- Sudden confusion

- Inability to recognize family members

- Loss of consciousness

- Confusion after head injury

- Memory loss with weakness or slurred speech

Recommendation: Seek immediate emergency medical care.

---

# Home Care

- Maintain a regular daily routine.

- Get adequate sleep.

- Stay physically and mentally active.

- Eat a balanced diet.

- Keep a list of medications and appointments.

---

# Prevention

- Control blood pressure and diabetes.

- Stay physically active.

- Exercise the brain through reading or puzzles.

- Maintain a healthy diet.

- Avoid smoking and excessive alcohol.

---

# When to Consult a Doctor

Consult a neurologist if:

- Memory problems worsen.

- Confusion develops.

- Daily activities become difficult.

- Family members notice behavioral changes.

---

# Emergency Warning Signs

Seek immediate medical care if you experience:

- Sudden confusion

- Loss of consciousness

- Slurred speech

- One-sided weakness

- Severe headache

- Confusion following a head injury
